Order under Section 69
Residential Tenancies
Act, 2006
 
File Number: HOL-06021-19
 











 
 

MALP (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to
terminate the tenancy and evict HDS (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not
pay the rent that the Tenant owes. The Landlord also claimed administration
charges related to NSF cheques.
 
This application was heard in Toronto on January 7,
2020.
 
The Landlord’s representative, FM
and the Tenant attended the hearing.
 
The Landlord’s application was
amended to indicate the monthly rent for October 2019 is $917.84.
 
Determinations:
 
1.     
The Tenant has not paid the
total rent the Tenant was required to pay for the period from March 1, 2019 to
January 31, 2020.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of
Termination effective October 3, 2019.
2.     
The monthly rent as of the
date of the hearing is $938.03.
3.     
The Tenant paid $1,862.40
after the application was filed.
4.     
There was no dispute that
the Tenant owes to the Landlord $2,044.85, which represents the arrears of rent
for the period ending January 31, 2020, administration charges related to
cheques tendered by or behalf of the Tenant, which were returned NSF, and the
application filing fee.
5.     
The Tenant has lived in the
unit for 10 years.
6.     
It was the evidence of the
Tenant that he has been using funds to pay for his courses and they became very
expensive. He purchased these programs for a marketing opportunity and to
generate an income. The Tenant acknowledged that he made a mistake by not
giving the priority to his rent. 
7.     
The Tenant’s monthly income
is his pension of $1,700.00. The Tenant requested relief and proposed to pay
January 2020 rent by January 31, 2020. The Tenant proposed a 9 month plan for
the arrears commencing on February 1, 2020. The Tenant assured the Board that
he can pay the rent when due on February 1, and the arrears.
8.     
The Landlord opposed the
Tenant’s payment plan and submitted that the Tenant is in this situation due to
the mismanagement of his funds, and buying courses rather than paying the rent.
The Landlord requested a standard order, or in the alternative, and extension
to void by January 28, 2020. 
9.     
I mentioned to the Tenant
that he placed his tenancy in jeopardy by not paying the rent, and by choosing
to pay other expenses.  
10.  
I considered that this is
the first time the Landlord has been to the Board on this tenancy; and this is a
lengthy tenancy. As the Act is remedial, I will give the Tenant an opportunity
to continue the tenancy if he pays the arrears and the rent according to his payment
plan. 
11.  
I have considered all of the
disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential
Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would not be unfair to
grant relief from eviction subject to  the condition(s) set out in this order
pursuant to subsection 83(1)(a) and 204(1) of the Act.  
It is ordered that:
 
1.     
The Tenant shall pay the
Landlord $2,044.85, which represents the arrears of rent owing up to January
31, 2020 ($1,829.85), plus $40.00 for a related administration charge for an NSF
cheque, plus the Landlord’s application filing fee ($175.00).
2.     
The Tenant shall pay the
amount set out in paragraph 1, above, according to the following schedule:
                                                On or
before January 31, 2020                 $    938.03
                                                On or
before February 1, 2020                  $    122.98
                                                On or
before March 1, 2020                       $    122.98
                                                On or
before April 1, 2020                          $    122.98
                                                On or
before May 1, 2020                           $    122.98
                                                On or

                                                On or

                                                On or

                                                On or
before September 1, 2020               $    122.98
                                                On or
before October 1, 2020                    $    122.98
3.     
The Tenant shall also pay
the monthly rent as it comes due on or before February 1, 2020, March 1, 2020,
April 1, 2020, May 1, 2020, June 1, 2020, July 1, 2020, August 1, 2020,
September 1, 2020 and October 1, 2020.  
4.     
If the Tenant fails to make
the payments under paragraph 2, above in full, the Landlord may collect the
entire outstanding balance from the amount set out in paragraph 1, immediately.

5.     
If the Tenant fail to make
payments under paragraphs 2, or 3, above in full and on time, the Landlord may
apply under section 78 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (‘the
Act’) within 30 days of the breach, without notice to the Tenant, for an order
terminating the tenancy and evicting the Tenant; and requiring that the Tenant
pay any new arrears and NSF fees and related charges that become owing after January
31, 2020.
