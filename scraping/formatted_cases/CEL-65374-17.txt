P
H C O A P L (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and
evict A S (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the
Tenant owes.
 
This
application was heard in Mississauga on June 27, 2017.
 
The Landlord’s Agent, C A, The Landlord’s Tenancy Support Agent
and the Landlord's Legal Representative, M Z and the Tenant’s Legal
Representative, V A attended the hearing.
 
Preliminary issue:
 
The Tenant’s Legal representative was seeking an adjournment for a
week because of the Tenant’s health condition and inability to attend. The
Tenant’s representative stated he did not have any further instructions to
proceed, but was unable to say why he received no instructions. The Landlord
opposed the request for adjournment because this was the third time the matter
was before the Board. The previous 2 times were adjourned at the Tenant’s
request and the arrears are standing at over $9,000.00. The Landlord argued
that the Tenant representative submitted a settlement request on Friday so he
is aware of the facts and circumstances to adequately represent the Tenant. I
denied the adjournment due to the number of times the matter has been
previously adjourned, the amount of arrears owing and that were accumulating,
the prejudice to the Landlord, and the fact that the Tenant had a legal
representative present who the Tenant could have provided fulsome instructions
to.  
 
Determinations:
 
1.      The
Tenant has not paid the total rent she was required to pay for the period from
December 1, 2016 to June 30, 2017.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served
a Notice of Termination effective February 28, 2017.
2.      The
Landlord collected a rent deposit of $92.87 from the Tenant and this deposit is
still being held by the Landlord.
3.      Interest
on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenant for the period from January 1, 2017
to February 28, 2017.
4.      The
Tenant paid $330.00 after the application was filed.
5.      The
Tenant’s Legal representative stated the Tenant has been living at the unit for
a long time, is unemployed has been receiving Ontario Works (OW). 
6.      The
Tenant was on subsidy and is now paying market rent. The Tenant had filed a
Service Manager appeal prior to seeking legal representation, and once she
obtained legal representation she was advised that there was no merit in her
pursuing judicial review. The Tenant was therefore advised to pay off the
arrears and pay market rent.
7.      The
Tenant’s representative stated that now the Tenant was paying market rent the
Legal Clinic is assisting the Tenant obtain retroactive payment of the market
rent from OW from December 1, 2016 to date. 
8.      The
Tenant currently lives with her 19 year old  son and her daughter and
son-in-law intend to move in to assist with the rent because she cannot pay $9,000.00
plus on her own.
9.      The
Tenant was proposing a 2-3 year payment plan to address the arrears. She would
pay her monthly rent plus $100.00 towards the arrears each month.
10.   The
Landlord challenged the fact that there was no documentation submitted regarding
the retroactive assistance from OW and stated it was unfair to the Landlord to
impose a payment plan which wold take over 7 years to pay off. The Landlord
pointed out that the Tenant has only paid $330.00 towards the arrears.
11.   The
Landlord is willing to work with the Tenant and is seeking a standard order
with an extended void date of July 20, 2017 to give the Tenant an opportunity
to get assistance from OW or get another place to live.
12.   I
have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with
subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and
find that it would be unfair to grant relief from eviction pursuant to
subsection 83(1) of the Act., because I find the lengthy payment plan proposed
by the Tenant to be too long and prejudicial towards the Landlord.  I have no
evidence before me that the Tenant will be able to pay the arrears owing within
a reasonable amount of time.  Under these circumstances, I find the Landlord’s
proposal of an extended termination date to be more reasonable.
 
 
 
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.      Unless
the Tenant voids the order as set out below, the tenancy between the Landlord
and the Tenant is terminated.  The Tenant must move out of the rental unit on
or before July 20, 2017.
2.      The
Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $10,441.54*, which represents the amount of
rent owing and compensation up to July 5, 2017, less the rent deposit and
interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.
3.      The
Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $43.69 per day for compensation for the use
of the unit starting July 6, 2017 to the date she moves out of the unit.
4.      The
Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $190.00 for the cost of filing the
application.
5.      If
the Tenant does not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before July
20, 2017, the Tenant will start to owe interest.  This will be simple interest
calculated from July 21, 2017 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
6.      If
the unit is not vacated on or before July 20, 2017, then starting July 21,
2017, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office
(Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.
7.      Upon
receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to
give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord, on or after July 21, 2017.
8.      If,
on or before July 20, 2017, the Tenant pays the amount of $10,492.00** to the
Landlord or to the Board in trust, this order for eviction will be void.  This
means that the tenancy would not be terminated and the Tenant could remain in
the unit.  If this payment is not made in full and on time, the Landlord may
file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the
eviction may be enforced.
9.      The
Tenant may make a motion to the Board under subsection 74(11) of the Act to set
aside this order if they pay the amount required under that subsection on or
after July 21, 2017 but before the Sheriff gives vacant possession to the
Landlord. The Tenant is only entitled to make this motion once during the
period of the tenancy agreement with the Landlord.
