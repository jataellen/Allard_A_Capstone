P P R I. (the 'Landlord') applied for an
order to terminate the tenancy and evict J S (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant
did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes.
 
This application was heard in Barrie on
July 7, 2017.
 
The Landlord's Legal
Representative, D. C and the Tenant attended the hearing.
 
At the hearing the
Tenant raised an issue regarding mould in the rental unit pursuant to section
82 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act').
 
Determinations and
Reasons:
 
1.     
The monthly rent is
$1,475.00.
2.     
The Tenant has not paid the
total rent the Tenant was required to pay for the period from May 1, 2017 to
July 31, 2017.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of
Termination effective May 21, 2017.
3.     
The Tenant paid $1,475.00
after the application was filed.
4.     
The Landlord collected a
rent deposit of $1,475.00 from the Tenant and this deposit is still being held
by the Landlord.
5.     
Interest on the rent deposit
is owing to the Tenant for the period from March 16, 2017 to May 21, 2017.
            Mould
6.     
The Tenant testified that
there is mould in the basement ceiling and he believes the mould may also be in
the ductwork of the unit.  The rental unit is a 3 level townhouse.  The Tenant
submitted photos of the unfinished basement ceiling to support his testimony. 
The Tenant also submitted a Mould Inspection report from Air Detectives dated
July 4, 2017.
7.     
The Tenant reported the
issue to the Landlord by telephone about 3 months ago. He also sent an email to
the Landlord on June 20, 2017 stating that he is arranging a mould inspection
because he did not hear back from the Landlord when he previously reported the
issue.  He sent a follow up email to the Landlord on June 21, 2017.  The Tenant
submitted copies of these emails into evidence.
8.     
The Landlord conducted an
inspection regarding mould and sent the Tenant an email stating that there was
no issue regarding mould found.  This undated email was submitted into evidence
by the Tenant.
9.     
The Landlord's Legal
Representative submitted that the Landlord is willing to re-inspect the unit
however the Landlord believes there is no mould and only mildew present.
10.   The Landlord breached its obligation pursuant to
section 20 of the Act by failing to inspect and remediate mould in the basement
of the rental unit. The best evidence presented is the mould inspection
report.  Much of the report addresses mould in general however the inspector
states that mould was visible on the underside of the flooring (basement
ceiling). The recommended treatment is cleaning with mould remediation
chemicals.  There is no mention in the mould inspection report of mould
suspected in the ductwork.  The Landlord failed to provide any reports
regarding the inspection done in the unit and the inspector did not attend the
hearing to provide any evidence.
11.   Based on the evidence, the presence of any mould
on the basement ceiling presents a minimal impact to the Tenant.  There was
insufficient evidence to conclude that there is any mould in the ductwork of
the unit.  Therefore it is not appropriate to award a rent abatement of one
month as requested by the Tenant.  However, the Tenant is entitled to an
inspection and remediation of any mould in the basement of the unit.
Relief from
eviction
12.   The Tenant requests a payment plan to repay the
arrears within 4 months.  
13.   The Landlord's Legal Representative submitted
that the Landlord requests that arrears are repaid with 3 payments.
14.   I have considered all of the disclosed
circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Act, and find that it
would not be unfair to grant relief from eviction subject to  the conditions
set out in this order pursuant to subsection 83(1)(a) and 204(1) of the Act. To reduce the prejudice to the Landlord, the relief from eviction is
subject to conditions set out in this order which, if not met, would allow the
Landlord to apply to the Board, without further notice to the Tenant, for an
order terminating the tenancy and evicting the Tenant pursuant to section 78 of
the Act.
 
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.     
The Landlord shall inspect
and remediate any mould in the basement of the rental unit on or before August
18, 2017.
2.     
The Tenant shall pay the
Landlord $2,950.00 which represents the amount of rent arrears owing up to July
31, 2017.
3.     
The Tenant shall also pay
the Landlord $175.00 for the cost of filing the application.
4.     
The Tenant shall pay the
Landlord the full amount owing of $3,125.00, and the additional rent that comes
due, as follows
a.    On or before July 28, 2017                   $781.25
(arrears and filing fee)
b.    On or before  August 1, 2017               August
rent
c.    On or before  August 15, 2017 $781.25 (arrears)
d.    On or before  September 1, 2017        September
rent
e.    On or before  September 15, 2017     $781.25
(arrears)
f.      On or before  October 1, 2017 October rent
g.    On or before  October 15, 2017           $781.25
(balance of arrears)
5.     
If the Tenant fails to make
any one of the payments in paragraph 4 of this order, in full and on time, the
Landlord may apply under section 78 of the Act, without notice to the Tenant
for an order terminating the tenancy and evicting the Tenant. The Landlord must
make this application no later than 30 days after the Tenant’s failure to make
a payment. As part of the application, the Landlord can also request an order
for rent that became owing after the date of this order and NSF cheque fees and
related administration charges.
