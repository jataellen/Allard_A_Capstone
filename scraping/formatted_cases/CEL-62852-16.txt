C
P H (1982) L (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and
evict S T (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the
Tenant owes.
 
This
application was heard in Mississauga on December 14, 2016.
 
The Landlord's Legal Representative, B R and the Tenant attended
the hearing.
 
Determinations:
 
1.      The
monthly rent is $1,289.50.
2.      The
Tenant has not paid the total rent he was required to pay for the period from
June 1, 2015 to December 31, 2016.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served
a Notice of Termination effective November 17, 2016.
3.      The
Tenant paid $939.06 after the application was filed.
4.      The
Tenant receives a rent subsidy of $939.06 and her portion of the rent is $350.44.
5.      The
Tenants testified that she has two children and their father stopped supporting
the family about a year ago.  She cannot not work full time because she does not
have child care and works just 3 shifts a month and only during the day. She
realised after getting advice from Tenant Duty Counsel that she can apply to
Ontario Works while working. The Tenant receives the Child tax benefit that
helps with food and rent.
6.      The
Tenant has lived at the unit for 6 years and her children go to the French
immersion school in the area. She would like to maintain her tenancy and
proposed to make payments of $1000.00 of the 20th of each month to
clear the arrears and pay her portion of the rent amounting to $350.44 on the
first of the month. The Landlord objected to a payment plan.
7.      I
have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with
subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and
find that it would not be unfair to grant relief from eviction subject to  the
condition(s) set out in this order pursuant to subsection 83(1)(a) and 204(1)
of the Act. I am allowing the payment plan suggested by the Tenant and the
prejudice to the Landlord is minimized by the provisions of section 78. of the
Act.
It is ordered that:
 
1.      The
Tenant shall pay the Landlord $6,672.87which
represents the amount of rent arrears owing up January 31, 2017 and includes
the $170.00 cost of filing the application.     
2.      The
Tenant shall pay the Landlord the full amount owing and the additional rent
that comes due, as follows:
a.    On
or before January 20, 2017          ($1,000.00 arrears and filing fee)
b.    On
or before February 1, 2017        February 2017 rent
c.    On
or before February 20, 2017      ($1,000.00 arrears)
d.    On
or before March 1, 2017             March 2017 rent
e.    On
or before March 20, 2017           ($1,000.00 arrears)
f.     On
or before April 1, 2017                April 2017 rent
g.    On
or before April 20, 2017             ($1,000.00 arrears)
h.    On
or before May 1, 2017                 May 2017 rent
i.      On
or before May 20, 2017               ($1,000.00 arrears)
j.      On
or before June 1, 2017               June 2017 rent
k.    On
or before June 20, 2017             ($1,000.00 arrears)
l.      On
or before July 1, 2017                 July 2017 rent
m.   On
or before July 20, 2016               ($672.87 balance of arrears)             
3.      If
the Tenant fails to make any one of the payments in paragraph 2 of this order,
in full and on time, the Landlord may apply under section 78 of the Residential
Tenancies Act, 2006, without notice to the Tenant for an order terminating
the tenancy and evicting the Tenants. The Landlord must make this application
no later than 30 days after the Tenant’s failure to make a payment. As part of
the application, the Landlord can also request an order for rent that became
owing after the date of this order and NSF cheque fees and related administration
charges.
4.      This
does not include charges related to lack of sufficient funds for pre-authorized
debits as this would be considered a penalty that is not allowed under
Subsection 134(1) of the Act.  Subsection 85(5).only allows
NSF cheque charges to be claimed by the Landlord on an arrears application.
