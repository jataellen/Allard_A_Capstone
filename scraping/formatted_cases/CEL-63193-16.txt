Review Order
 
K P L (the
'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict O A M (the
'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes.
 
The Landlord’s L1
application was resolved by order CEL-63193-16, issued on January 10, 2017.
 
On January 19,
2017, the Tenant requested a review of that order.
 
The Tenant’s review
request was heard in Mississauga on February 2, 2017.
 
The Landlord's
Agent, G. P, and the Tenant attended the hearing.
 
Determinations
and Reasons:
 
1.      The Tenant alleges that he was not reasonably
able to participate in the hearing on January 4, 2017 because he did not
receive the Notice of Hearing until after the hearing had already taken place. 
2.      The Tenant stated that: he checks his mail
regularly and cannot explain the delay in receiving the Notice of Hearing from
Canada Post; and he would have attended the hearing if he got the notice in
time since he attended a prior Board hearing concerning CEL-60968-16.
3.      The Tenant complied with the interim order issued
on January 20, 2017 and paid the February rent (minus 10 cents) to the Board in
trust. This minimized the prejudice to the Landlord from the review since the
rent arrears have not increased.
4.      The Tenant was not reasonably able to
participate in the original hearing. The Tenant’s review request is granted and
the L1 rent arrears application is heard de novo (re-heard).
5.      The monthly rent is $1,205.10. 
6.      The Landlord offers a prompt payment discount of 2% ($24.10) of the
monthly rent if the rent is paid on the first day of the month.
7.      The Tenant has not paid the total rent the Tenant was required to
pay for the period from November 1, 2016 to February 28, 2017. Because of the
arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective December 2,
2016.
8.      The Tenant paid $1,161.00 to the Landlord after the application was
filed.
9.      The Tenant paid $1,205.00 to the Board in trust after the
application was filed.
10.   The money which was paid into the Board by the Tenant, and any
accumulated interest, shall be paid out to the Landlord. 
11.   After the two payments above are credited, there is a balance of
$2,604.50 owing to the Landlord up to February 28, 2017. The amount includes
the $170.00 fee to file the application.
12.   The Tenant disputed the $2,604.50 amount because he thought it
included a $20.00 charge to replace his FOB (electronic key) and extra charges of
$24.10 for the loss of the 2% prompt payment discount.
13.   The amount does not include any extra charges.
14.   The Landlord’s rent ledger, the N4 notice of termination and the L1 application
all confirm that only the monthly rent of $1,205.10 was claimed. No additional
charges were claimed in the N4 notice or the L1 application.
15.   The undisputed $20.00 charge to replace the FOB was paid by the Tenant
in October 2016 before the N4 notice was even created.
16.   The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $1,181.00 from the Tenant
and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.
17.   Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenant for the period
from July 1, 2016 to December 2, 2016.
18.   I have considered all of the disclosed
circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential
Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would be unfair to grant
relief from eviction pursuant to subsection 83(1) of the Act. This tenancy just
commenced in July 2016 and the Tenant has been unable or unwilling to pay the
rent on time and/or in full each month since then. A prior L1 rent arrears
order was issued in October 2016. The Tenant was able to pay the amount owing by
the date in the order which voided the order. However, immediately after that,
he fell into arrears for the November rent.
19.   The Tenant stated he was injured in an accident
shortly after he moved in and was off work. However, he is now back at work and
hopes to pay the amount owing by the end of February.
20.   The Tenant still has the option set out below in
paragraph 9 of the order if he wants to preserve this tenancy.
It is ordered
that:
 
1.      Order CEL-63193-16, issued on January 10, 2017, is cancelled and
replaced by the following: 
2.      Unless the Tenant voids the order as set out
below, the tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant is terminated. The
Tenant must move out of the rental unit on or before February 14, 2017.
3.      The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $203.47*, which represents the
amount of rent owing and compensation up to February 3, 2017, less the rent
deposit and interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.
4.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $39.62 per day for
compensation for the use of the unit starting February 4, 2017 to the date the
Tenant moves out of the unit.
5.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $170.00 for the cost of
filing the application.
6.     
If the Tenant does not pay
the Landlord the full amount owing on or before February 14, 2017, the Tenant
will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from
February 15, 2017 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
7.      If the unit is not vacated on or before February 14, 2017, then
starting February 15, 2017, the Landlord may file this order with the Court
Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.
8.      Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff)
is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord, on or after
February 15, 2017.
9.      If, on or before February 14, 2017, the Tenant pays the amount of
$2,604.50** to the Landlord or to the Board in trust, this order for eviction
will be void.  This means that the tenancy
would not be terminated and the Tenant could remain in the unit.  If this
payment is not made in full and on time, the Landlord may file this order with
the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.
10.   The Tenant may make a motion to the Board under subsection 74(11) of
the Act to set aside this order if they pay the amount required under that
subsection on or after February 15, 2017 but before the Sheriff gives vacant
possession to the Landlord. The Tenant is only entitled to make this motion
once during the period of the tenancy agreement with the Landlord.
11.   The Board shall pay to the Landlord the amount of $1,205.00 together
with any accrued interest.
