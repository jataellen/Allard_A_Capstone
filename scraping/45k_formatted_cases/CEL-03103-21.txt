Order under Section 69
Residential Tenancies
Act, 2006
 
File Number: CEL-03103-21
 
 



In the
  matter of:


36 SLEDMAN STREET MISSISSAUGA ON L4T1K5


 




Between:


Kuldeep Mann


Landlord




 


 
and


 




 


Major Singhera


Tenant



 
 
 
Kuldeep Mann (the 'Landlord')
applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Major Singhera (the 'Tenant') because
the Tenant, another
occupant of the rental unit or someone
the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has substantially
interfered with the reasonable enjoyment or lawful right, privilege or interest
of the Landlord or another tenant. ; because the Tenant, another occupant of
the rental unit or a person the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has
seriously impaired the safety of any person
 
This application was heard in Passcode: 956 0593 7653# on January
27, 2022. The Landlord’s
representative James Hill, and the Tenant, represented by Roselea Thompson
attended the hearing.
 
Determinations:
 
1.    
The Tenant’s representative
submits there are four units in the residential complex and that the notice served on the Tenant does not include
the unit number as required
under section 43.1(a) of the Residential Tenancies Act.
 
2.     The Landlord’s representative acknowledged the error on the notice.
 
3.    
I
find the notice served on the Tenant is defective and the tenancy
cannot be terminated based on the notice served by
the Landlord.
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.     The Landlord's application is dismissed.
