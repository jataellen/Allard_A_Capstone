Smart Living Properties (the
'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Tamara Layefa
Adokeme, Kenechukwu Michael,
Kundisai Mudambo, David Tyowua and Yannick
Avognon (the 'Tenants') because the Tenants did not pay the rent that the
Tenants owe.
 
This application was heard by videoconference on November 23, 2021.
Only the Landlord’s representative, A. Traboulsi attended the hearing. Determinations:
1.     The Landlord and the Tenants
signed an agreement
to terminate the tenancy on December 31, 2021.
 
2.    
The Landlord collected a rent deposit
of $3,250.00 from the Tenants
and this deposit
has been applied to the rent for December 2021.
 
3.     The Landlord agreed
to give the Tenants an abatement of rent in the
amount of
$2,100.00
 
4.     The Tenants agreed
to pay arrears in instalments commencing on October
9, 2021 and ending on December 31, 2021. A total of $3,740.99 came due
after the hearing.
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.     the tenancy between
the Landlord and the Tenants
is terminated effective December
31, 2021. The Tenants must move out of the rental unit on or before
December 31, 2021.




File Number: HOL-09083-20
 
 
 
2.     The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlord $115.89
per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting January 1, 2022
to the date the Tenants move out of the unit.
 
3.     The Tenants shall owe interest
on outstanding arrears
and/or compensation. This will be simple interest calculated from
February 27, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
 
4.     If the unit has not been vacated,
the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) immediately so that the eviction may be enforced.
 
5.     Upon receipt of this order,
the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give
vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord, on or after February 16, 2022
