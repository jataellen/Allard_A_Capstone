William (Billy) McBain

On November 17, 2021.The
Corporation Of The County Of Wellington (the 'Landlord') applied
for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict William (Billy) McBain
Jr. (the 'Tenant') and for an order to have the Tenant pay the rent the Tenant
owes because the Tenant failed to meet a condition specified in the order
issued by the Board on January 15, 2021 with respect to application
SOL-15342-20.
 
Determinations:
 
1.    
The order provided that the
Landlord could apply to the Board under section 78 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the
'Act') without notice
to the Tenant to terminate
the tenancy and evict the
Tenant if the Tenant did not
meet certain condition(s) specified
in the order.
 
2.    
I
find that the Tenant has not met the following
conditions specified in the order:
 
The
Tenant, his guests or occupants were not to create loud noise including
smashing, slamming, banging
or yelling, nor be aggressive towards other people in the residential complex.
 
On
October 21, 2021, at 6:25 pm, the Tenant attempted to attack/lunge at another
tenant while the other tenant was in their car, screamed threats at them, and
swearing at them. This is alleged to
have occurred in the residential complex parking lot. On October 21, 2021, between 9:10 pm to 9:30 pm, the Tenant
threatened the same tenant involved
in the above incident in a common
hallway of the residential
complex (Police Occurrence Number: GU21059037).
 
On
October 22, 2021, at 7:45 am, 9:00 am and again at 12:00 pm, the Tenant was
loudly and aggressively banging on multiple other tenants’ door. The Tenant was
heard yelling and swearing at the tenants through their door, calling them
names while demanding cigarettes.




 
On October
25, 2021, at 3:00 am, the Tenant was loudly and aggressively banging on another tenant’s door. The Tenant was heard yelling and swearing at the tenants through
their door, calling them names while demanding cigarettes.
 
On
October 25, 2021, at 7:30 am, the Tenant assaulted and threatened another
tenant in the residential complex.
Police were called
and charge the Tenant with Assault, Utter Death Threats and
Mischief under $5,000.00 (Police occurrence Number: GU21059757). The Criminal Court issued a release
condition for the Tenant, ordering no contact with the other tenant that was
assaulted and threatened.
 
On
October 28, 2021, at 5:45 pm and 8:45 pm, the Tenant was aggressively knocking
on another tenant’s rental unit door and yelling through the door, adjacent to the assault
victim on October
25, 2021 rental
unit. This was in violation of the Tenant’s release
condition.
 
On
October 29, 2021, at 1:13 pm, the Tenant was knocking on the assault victim’s
porch door, asking to be let in the building.
Police were called (Occurrence Number:
GU21060664) and the Tenant was charged with failure to comply with the
no contact order.
 
3.    
The Landlord had agreed to waive
the $175.00 filing fee provided the Landlord is not required to file an L4 application. The Landlord is requesting on order for the filing fee.
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.   
Order SOL-15342-20 is cancelled.
 
2.     
The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant
is terminated. The Tenant must move
out of the rental unit on or before January 18, 2022.
 
3.     
The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $175.00.
This amount represents the costs related to the application fee for the
previous application.
 
4.     
The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord
$4.77 per day for compensation for the use of
the unit starting January 8, 2022 to the date the Tenant moves out of the unit.
 
5.     
If the Tenant does not pay the Landlord
the full amount owing* on or before
January 18, 2022, the Tenant
will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from
January 19, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
 
6.     
If the unit is not vacated
on or before January 18, 2022, then starting January
19, 2022, the Landlord may
file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the
eviction may be enforced.
 
7.     
Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give
vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord on or after January 19, 2022.
