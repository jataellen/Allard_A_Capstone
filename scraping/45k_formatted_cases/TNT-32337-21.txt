Xiao Liu (the 'Tenant') applied for an order determining that Yuqiao Hu (the 'Landlord') has collected or retained money illegally.
 
This application was heard by videoconference on January 19, 2022.
 
The Tenant and the Landlord attended
the hearing. The Landlord was represented by Rong Wei Yu. The Tenant consulted with Tenant
Duty Counsel prior to the hearing.
 
Determinations:
 
1.    
The Tenant’s T1 application was
filed on April 9, 2021 and alleged the Landlord had charged illegal rent to the Tenant. The application goes on to explain the reasons as follows:












 
2.    
The problem with the Tenant’s reasons
is twofold; first,
he is raising issues that cannot be considered under this application and
are more appropriate under a T2 and/or T6




File Number: TNT-32337-21
 
application and second that the Tenant seeks remedies
(rent abatement) that is not available under this application,
filed pursuant to subsection 135 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006.
 
3.     Section 135 of the Act states:
 
135 (1) A tenant or former tenant of a rental
unit may apply to the Board for an order that the landlord, superintendent or agent of the landlord
pay to the tenant any money the person collected or
retained in contravention of this Act or the Tenant Protection Act, 1997.
 
4.    
At the hearing, the Tenant indicated
he understood the preliminary issue I raised
and provided no further submissions in this regard.
 
5.     Given the above, the Tenant’s
application must be dismissed.
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.     The Tenant's T1 application is dismissed.
