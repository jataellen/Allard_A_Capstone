Order under Section 69
Residential Tenancies Act, 2006
File Number: HOL-10276-21
 



In the matter
of:


4008, 55 BREMNER BOULEVARD TORONTO ON M5J0A6


 




Between:


Xia Song


Landlord




 


and


 




 


Adrian Florea
  Haitang Yu


Tenants



 
Xia Song (the 'Landlord') applied
for an order requiring Haitang Yu and Adrian Florea (the 'Tenants') to pay compensation for the damages
caused by them or a person they permitted in the residential complex
 
This application was heard via video/teleconference on October 7, 2021.
 
Only the Landlord and the Landlord's Legal Representative Elaine
Page attended the hearing.
 
As of 11:52 a.m., the Tenants
were not present
or represented at the hearing
although properly served with
notice of this hearing by the Board.
 
Determinations:
 
1.     The rental unit is a condominium apartment
owned by the Landlord
in a
high rise multi unit residential complex.
 
2.    
Pursuant to section
12.1 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the ‘Act’) after April 30, 2018 a landlord is now required to
provide all new tenancies entered into after April 30, 2018 a written tenancy
agreement completed on the LTB prescribed Standard Lease forms.
 
3.    
The Landlord's Legal Representative submitted
a copy of the current
tenancy agreement entered
into between the Landlord and the Tenants dated March 15, 2020.
Clause 11 stipulates that – the
Tenants must have liability insurance
at all times, and it is
up to the Tenant to get content insurance if they want.
 
Damages
 
4.     On April 16, 2021 the Landlord filed an application
with the Board claiming that the Tenants were responsible for water damage
to the rental unit and the unit below
resulting from the Tenants allowing a kitchen tap to overflow causing a
major flood on March 20, 2021.




 
5.    
The Landlord's Legal Representative provided a copy of an emergency services
report from the condominium building management identifying the water
flooding incident on March 20, 2021.
 
6.     The Landlord submitted copies of charge
back invoices received
from the condominium management the for the cost
of the damage incurred to the rental unit $5,783.77, and
$16,500.60 for the costs of damage
to other units in the complex totaling
$22,284.37.
 
7.     Section 89 (1) of the Act provides, that a landlord
may apply to the Board for an order requiring a tenant to pay reasonable costs
that the landlord has incurred or will incur for the repair of or, where repairing is not reasonable, the replacement of damaged property, if the tenant, another occupant
of the rental unit or a person whom the tenant permits in the residential
complex wilfully or negligently causes undue damage to the rental unit or the
residential complex.
 
8.     The Tenants was still in possession of the rental
unit as of the date the application was filed.
 
9.     The Tenants did not attend
the hearing to make submissions.
 
10.  I was satisfied based on the uncontested testimony of the Landlord
and the documentary evidence submitted, on a
balance of probabilities, the Tenants, or another occupant of the rental unit
or someone they permitted in the residential complex has wilfully or
negligently caused undue damage to the rental unit and residential complex and
the Landlord incurred or will incur costs of $22,284.37 to repair the damage
and/or replace property that was damaged.
 
11.  The order contains
all the reasons for the decision within the order.
No other reasons
will be issued.
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.     The Tenants shall pay to the Landlord
$22,284.37*, which represents the reasonable costs
of repairing the damage the rental unit and the unit below.
 
2.     The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlord $186.00*
for the cost of filing the application.
 
3.     The Tenants shall pay the total amount
owing** to the Landlord on or before
February 28, 2022.
 
4.    
If the Tenants do not pay the
Landlord the full amount owing on or before February 28, 2022, they will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest
calculated from March 1, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance
outstanding.




5.    
If the Tenants do not pay the
Landlord the full amount owing* on or before
February 28, 2022, the Landlord
has the right anytime after March 1, 2022 to collect from the Tenants the amount owing or any
outstanding balance as a result of this order.
