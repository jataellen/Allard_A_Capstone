Peel Housing
Corp Op As Peel Living (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the
tenancy and evict Amanda Edmonds (the 'Tenant') and for an order to have the
Tenant pay the rent the Tenant
owes because the Tenant failed to meet a condition
specified in the order issued by the Board on July 5, 2021 with
respect to application CEL-98045-21.
 
Determinations:
 
1.     The Board issued an order pertaining to file number CEL-98045-21 on July 5, 2021 which required the Tenant to make
regularly scheduled payments to the Landlord that were to be applied against
outstanding rental arrears. The order also required the Tenant to continue
making payments of lawful rent for each consecutive month in full, and on time,
beginning in July 2021 through to June 2022.
 
 
2.    
The order provided that
the Landlord could apply to the Board under section 78 of the Residential
Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act') without notice to the Tenant to terminate
the tenancy and evict the Tenant if the Tenant
did not meet certain conditions specified in the order.
 
 
3.    
Pursuant to subsection 78(5)
of the Act, an application made under section
78 must be filed no later than 30 days after the
breach of the order
 
 
4.    
Within the subject L4 application the Landlord claims
that the Tenant had not met the following conditions as set out in
the order:
 
▪  The lawful monthly rent of $1204.00
was not paid in full and on time for the months of July 2021, August 2021,
September 2021, October 2021, and November 2021.




File Number: CEL-04264-21
 
Payments for July 2021 and August 2021 were paid in two installments with the first payment being made in the early
part of the month, and the second payment made within the following weeks of the same month. A balance of $448.00 remains owing
for the months of September 2021, October 2021, and November 2021.
▪  The arrears rental payments
of $227.33 was paid for July 2021, and remains
in default for August 2021, September 2021, and October 2021
 
5.    
The Landlord’s application to the Board dated November 19, 2021 was filed by email on December 6, 2021 which, pursuant to s. 78 (5) of the Act, is more than 30 days after the
Tenant failed to meet a condition set out in hearing order CEL-98045-21 dated
July 5, 2021.
 
6.    
The Board
does not have jurisdiction to consider this application
as it was filed beyond the statutory 30-day period after
the alleged breaches had taken place.
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.    The Landlord's application under section
78 of the Act is dismissed.
