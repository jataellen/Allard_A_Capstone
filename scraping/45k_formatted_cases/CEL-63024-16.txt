W
M I (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict A
M M S (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant
owes.
 
This
application was heard in Mississauga on January 4, 2017.
 
The
Landlord's Legal Agent, E M and the Tenant attended the hearing.
 
The
spelling of the Tenant’s name was corrected. 
 
Determinations:
 
1.      The
monthly rent is $1,475.00.
2.      The
Tenant has not paid the total rent the Tenant was required to pay for the
period from May 13, 2016 to January 31, 2017.  Because of the arrears, the
Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective November 11, 2016.
3.      The
Landlord collected a rent deposit of $1,475.00 from the Tenant and this deposit
is still being held by the Landlord.
4.      Interest
on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenant for the period from June 2, 2016 to
November 11, 2016.
5.      The
Tenant stated he started a new job on November 22, 2016 and his car broke down,
and his new job requires him to have use of a car. He stated he had to pay $3,000.00
to repair the car. 
6.      The
Tenant proposed that he can pay $800.00 on top of his rent over the next 7
months to address the arrears. The Landlord was not interested in a payment
plan.
7.      I
have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with
subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and
find that it would not be unfair to grant relief from eviction subject to  the
condition(s) set out in this order pursuant to subsection 83(1)(a) and 204(1)
of the Act. I am allowing the payment plan suggested by the Tenant and the
prejudice to the Landlord is minimized by the provisions of section 78. of the
Act.
It is ordered that:
 
1.      The
Tenant shall pay the Landlord $6,386.04 which represents the amount of rent
arrears owing up January 31, 2017 and includes the $170.00 cost of filing the
application.     
2.      The
Tenant shall pay the Landlord the full amount owing and the additional rent
that comes due, as follows:
a.    On
or before January 20, 2017          ($800.00 arrears and filing fee)
b.    On
or before February 1, 2017        February 2017 rent
c.    On
or before February 20, 2017      ($800.00 arrears)
d.    On
or before March 1, 2017             March 2017 rent
e.    On
or before March 20, 2017           ($800.00 arrears)
f.     On
or before April 1, 2017                April 2017 rent
g.    On
or before April 20, 2017             ($800.00 arrears)
h.    On
or before May 1, 2017                 May 2017 rent
i.      On
or before May 20, 2017               ($800.00 arrears)
j.      On
or before June 1, 2017               June 2017 rent
k.    On
or before June 20, 2017             ($800.00 arrears)
l.      On
or before July 1, 2017                 July 2017 rent
m.   On
or before July 20, 2016               ($800.00 arrears)
n.    On
or before August 1, 2017           August 2017 rent
o.    On
or before August 20, 2017         ($786.04 balance of arrears)             
3.      If
the Tenant fails to make any one of the payments in paragraph 2 of this order,
in full and on time, the Landlord may apply under section 78 of the Residential
Tenancies Act, 2006, without notice to the Tenant for an order terminating
the tenancy and evicting the Tenant. The Landlord must make this application no
later than 30 days after the Tenant’s failure to make a payment. As part of the
application, the Landlord can also request an order for rent that became owing
after the date of this order and NSF cheque fees and related administration
charges.
4.      This
does not include charges related to lack of sufficient funds for pre-authorized
debits as this would be considered a penalty that is not allowed under
Subsection 134(1) of the Act.  Subsection 85(5).only allows
NSF cheque charges to be claimed by the Landlord on an arrears application.
