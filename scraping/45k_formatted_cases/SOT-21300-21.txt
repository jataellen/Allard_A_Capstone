Jill Kwasniak and Lindsey
Marshall (the 'Tenants') applied for an order determining that Bhupinder Dult and Gurtaj
Bhatti (the 'Landlords') have collected or retained money illegally.
 
This application was heard via videoconference on January 26, 2022, at 9 A.M. Only the Tenants attended the
hearing.
 
Determinations:
 
1.    
At the hearing, the Tenants
advised they submitted a Tenant Application for a Rebate (T1) application, pursuant to s.135(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (Act) in their efforts to
establish that the Landlord served a notice of termination in bad faith.
 
2.    
The Landlord served a Notice to End your Tenancy Because
the Landlord, a Purchaser,
or a Family Member Requires the Rental Unit (N12) on November 5, 2020, with a
termination date of January 6, 2021, for a purchaser’s own use.
 
3.    
This application is seeking
compensation payment in the amount
of $2025.00, based on the N12 Notice.
 
4.    
Section 185(1) of the Act states
that an application must be filed with the Board in the
form approved by the Board.
 
5.    
Unfortunately, the Tenants did not file the correct type of application to raise this issue.
The substance of the Tenants’ claim is addressed in section 57 (1)(b) of the Act which states:




File Number: SOT-21300-21
 
The Board may make an order
described in subsection (3) if, on application by a
former tenant of a rental unit, the Board determines that,
 
(b)    
the landlord gave a notice of
termination under section 49 in bad faith, the former tenant vacated the rental
unit as a result of the notice or as a result of an application to or order
made by the Board based on the notice, and no person referred to in clause 49
(1) (a), (b), (c) or (d) or 49 (2) (a), (b),
(c)  
or (d) occupied the rental unit within a reasonable time after the former
tenant vacated the rental unit; or
 
 
6.    
In order to make a claim under section 57 (1)(b) of the Act, the Tenants were required to file the application entitled Landlord Gave a Notice of
Termination in Bad Faith (T5).
 
7.    
I
also informed the Tenants
of s.57(2), and s.57.1(2) of the Act, and informed them of
the limitation period for such an application.
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.    
The Tenants' application is
dismissed.
