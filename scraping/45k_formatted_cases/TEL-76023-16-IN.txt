under Section 16.1 of the 
Statutory Powers Procedure Act 
and the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006
 
File Number: TEL-76023-16-IN
 
 
 
 
INTERIM ORDER 
 
SP (the
'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict NW and CL
(the 'Tenants') because the Tenants did not pay the rent that the Tenants owe.
The Landlord also claimed administration charges related to NSF cheques.
 
This
application came before the Board in Toronto on February 1, 2017.
 
The
Landlord and the second-named Tenant attended the hearing.
 
It
is ordered that:
 

This
     application is adjourned to a date to be set by the Board.
The
     Tenants’ section 82 claims are limited to the following issues that were
     identified at the hearing on February 1, 2017:

i)    
The Landlord’s renting out of the basement unit;
ii)   
The lack of fire/smoke alarms;
iii)   The
kitchen window leaking;
iv)  
The living room window leaking;
v)   
The bedroom window and mould;
vi)  
Stove not working;
vii) The door
to the basement not being to Building Code standards;
viii)       
The behaviour of the basement tenants;
ix)  
The basement not being up to Building Code standards;
x)   
The laundry facilities;
xi)  
Moisture in the entranceway or foyer; and
xii) Mail
delivery.
3.    As
soon as possible and no later than ten days prior to the next hearing date, the
Tenants shall give to the Landlord a copy of any document, photograph, receipt,
recording or like thing they intend to rely on at the hearing. 
4.    As
soon as possible and no later than five days prior to the next hearing date,
the Landlord shall give to the Tenants a copy of any document, photograph,
receipt, recording or like thing he intends to rely on at the hearing.
5.    Pursuant
to Rule 19.1(3) of the Board’s Rules of Practice a party that does not comply
with an order for disclosure may not be permitted to rely on any evidence not
properly disclosed.
6.    The
Tenants shall pay into the Board in trust new rent as it comes due and owing
for the period commencing March 1, 2017, until this application is disposed of
or the Board orders otherwise. 

If
     the Tenants do not comply with paragraph 6 of this order then:

i)    
pursuant to s. 195(4) of the Act the Board may refuse to consider
the Tenants’ evidence and submissions;
ii)   
the Board may invite submissions as to whether or not it should
exercise its jurisdiction under Rule A8.2 to declare the Tenants to be vexatious
litigants with respect to section 82 claims (please see paragraphs 29 to 31 of Shields
v Lancelotte, 2016 ONSC 4433 (CanLII) for background available on-line
at http://canlii.ca/t/gsdbv ); and
iii)   the
Board may invite submissions with respect to releasing an un-redacted version
of its final order.
