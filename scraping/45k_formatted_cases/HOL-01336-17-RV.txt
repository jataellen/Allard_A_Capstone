Review Order
 
 
B K and S K (the
'Landlords') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict A A B (the
'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes.
 
The application was
resolved by order HOL-01336-17, issued on May 18, 2017.
 
On June 1, 2017,
the Tenant requested a review of that order.
 
The Tenant’s review
request was heard in Mississauga on June 23, 2017.
 
The
Landlord's Legal Representative, H. L, and the Tenant attended the hearing.
 
Determinations and Reasons:
 
1.     
The Tenant did not attend
the original hearing on May 17, 2017. She claims she was in family court on
that day. She did not provide any proof of a conflicting court appearance on
that day. However, during the review hearing, it became apparent that there are
errors in the order that need to be corrected.
2.     
There are serious errors in
the order. The amount of rent arrears that was ordered is incorrect. A payment of
$1,000.00 made on May 3, 2017 after the application was filed is not listed on
the Landlord’s L1/L9 update form that was presented at the original hearing and
was therefore not credited to the Tenant in the order. The undisputed last
month's rent deposit was also not credited in the order.
3.     
The review is granted to
correct the errors in the order.
4.     
The monthly rent is
$1,800.00. It is due on the first day of each month.
5.     
The tenancy commenced on May
1, 2016.
6.     
The payments the Tenant has
made to the Landlord throughout the tenancy are no longer disputed.
7.     
The Landlord's Legal
Representative did not dispute the list of e-transfer payments that the Tenant has
given to the Landlord. This list was provided at the review hearing. In
addition to this list, it was agreed that the Tenant also paid $1,800.00 in
August 2016 by cheque that should be credited to the Tenant.
8.     
There is no dispute that the
Tenant paid a last month's rent deposit of $1,800.00 on or around April 27,
2016. 
9.     
In addition to the
payment for the deposit, the
Tenant has paid the Landlord a total of $23,751.59 since the tenancy commenced.
This amount includes the list of e-transfer payments provided at the hearing
plus the $1,800.00 cheque from August 2016 plus $1,000.00 just paid on June 20,
2017.
10.   The monthly rent the Tenant should have paid the
Landlord, in addition to the $1,800.00 last month's rent deposit, is $1,800.00
rent for 14 months (May 2016 to June 2017 = 14 months x $1,800.00 = $25,200.00)
11.   The $25,200.00 that should have been paid minus
the $23,751.59 that was actually paid = $1,448.41 balance owing for rent up to
June 30, 2017.
12.   The Tenant also owes the Landlord $175.00 for
the application filing fee since there was some rent owing when the application
was filed on April 10, 2017 to justify the application.
13.   The Tenant has not paid $1,448.41 of the total
rent the Tenant was required to pay for the period from May 1, 2016 to June 30,
2017.  Because of the arrears, the Landlords served a N4 Notice of Termination.
14.   The Landlords collected a rent deposit of
$1,800.00 from the Tenant and this deposit is still being held by the
Landlords.
15.   Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the
Tenant for the period from April 27, 2016 to June 1, 2017.
16.   I have considered all of the disclosed
circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential
Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would not be necessary to
grant relief from eviction pursuant to subsection 83(1) of the Act. The Tenant
intends to pay the amount required to void the order.
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.     
Order HOL-01336-17, issued
on May 18, 2017, is cancelled and replaced by the following:
2.     
Unless the Tenant voids the
order as set out below, the tenancy between the Landlords and the Tenant is
terminated. The Tenant must move out of the rental unit on or before July 8,
2017.
3.     
As of the date of this
order, the amount of the rent deposit and interest the Landlords owe on the
rent deposit exceeds the arrears of rent and compensation the Landlords are
entitled to by $583.40*.
4.     
However, the Landlords are
authorized to offset the following amounts from the amount the Landlords owe
the Tenant: $59.18 per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting
June 28, 2017 to the date the Tenant moves out of the unit, and $175.00 for the
cost of filing the application.
5.     
The Landlords or the Tenant
shall pay to the other any sum of money that is owed as a result of this order.
6.     
If the unit is not vacated
on or before July 8, 2017, then starting July 9, 2017, the Landlords may file
this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may
be enforced.
7.     
Upon receipt of this order,
the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of
the unit to the Landlords, on or after July 9, 2017.
8.     
If the Tenant wishes
to void this order and continue the tenancy, the Tenant must pay to the
Landlords or to the Board in trust:
i)        $1,623.41 if the payment is made on or before June 30, 2017, or
ii)       $3,423.41 if the payment is made on or before July 8, 2017**.
If the Tenant does not make full payment in accordance with this paragraph and
by the appropriate deadline, then the Landlords may file this order with the
Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.
9.     
The Tenant may make a motion
to the Board under subsection 74(11) of the Act to set aside this order if they
pay the amount required under that subsection on or after July 9, 2017 but
before the Sheriff gives vacant possession to the Landlords. The Tenant is only
entitled to make this motion once during the period of the tenancy agreement
with the Landlords.
