Weston Property Management (the 'Landlord') applied
for an order to terminate
the tenancy and evict Julie Cimino and Ashley Cimino
(the 'Tenants') because the Tenants did not pay the rent that the Tenants owe.
 
This application was heard by
videoconference on November
8, 2021.
 
The Landlord’s representative, A. Trent, and the Tenant
Julie Cimino attended
the hearing.
 
Determinations:
 
1.    
The Tenants have not paid the
total rent the Tenants were required to pay for the period from June 1, 2020 to November
30, 2021. Because
of the arrears, the Landlord
served a Notice of Termination
effective October 13, 2020.
.
2.    
The Tenants paid $12,430.00 after the application was filed.
 
3.    
The Tenant owes $5,580.84 up to November
30, 2021.
 
4.    
I
have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies
Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would not be unfair to grant relief
from eviction subject to the
condition(s) set out in this order pursuant to subsection 83(1)(a) and 204(1)
of the Act.
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.    
The Tenants shall pay to the Landlord
$5,580.84, which represents the amount of rent
owing up to November 30, 2021 and the cost of filing the application.
