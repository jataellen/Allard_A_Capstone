G O (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate
the tenancy and evict M P and J M (the 'Tenants') because the Tenants did not
pay the rent that the Tenants owe.  (L1 Application)
 
The Landlord also applied for an order to terminate
the tenancy and evict the Tenants because they, another occupant of the rental
unit or someone they permitted in the residential complex have wilfully or
negligently caused undue damage to the premises. The Landlord has also applied
for an order requiring the Tenants to compensate the Landlord for the damage;
because they, another occupant of the rental unit or someone they permitted in
the residential complex have substantially interfered with the reasonable
enjoyment or lawful right, privilege or interest of the Landlord or another
tenant; and because the number of persons living in the unit on a continuing
basis is more than permitted by health, safety or housing standards. The
Landlord also claimed compensation for each day the Tenants remained in the
unit after the termination date.  (L2 Application)
 
These applications were heard in Mississauga on July
27, 2018.
 
Only the Landlord attended the hearing.
 
Preliminary Matter
 
Landlord requested that the L2
claim relating to “the number of persons living in the unit…more than permitted
by health, safety of housing standards” be deleted.  The Landlord indicated
that this particular claim was inadvertently included in his application when
it should not have been.  As this does not prejudice the Tenants, I consented
to deleting this particular claim.
 
 
 
Determinations:
 
L1 Application
 
1.     
The Tenants have not paid
the total rent they were required to pay for the period from May 1, 2018 to
July 31, 2018.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of
Termination effective June 18, 2018.
2.     
The Landlord collected a
rent deposit of $1,400.00 from the Tenants and this deposit is still being held
by the Landlord.
3.     
Interest on the rent deposit
is owing to the Tenants for the period from February 15, 2018 to June 18, 2018.
L2 Application
4.     
The N5 notice of termination
underlying the application was served on the Tenants on June 20, 2018 (exhibit
LL#1) and sufficiently identified the claimed:  undue damage caused by the
Tenants to the wall of the house/rental unit; and failure of the Tenants to pay
hydro, water and gas bills.   The Landlord is seeking $500.00 in estimated
costs to repair the damage and the termination of the tenancy on July 31, 2018
(as indicated on the N5).  
5.     
The Landlord testified that
the Tenants moved into the rental unit on February 15, 2018, under a
month-to-month tenancy for which the rent is due on the first of each month. 
Further, the Landlord testified the lawful monthly rent is $1,400.00 and the
Tenants are responsible for paying monthly utilities.  Specifically, the
agreement between the parties is that the Tenants are to pay the Landlord
$200.00 as a separate monthly amount toward utilities.
6.     
In February 2018, the
Landlord testified the Tenants drove their truck into the side of house/rental
unit wall.  The Landlord submitted a colour photo (exhibit LL#1) in support of
this claim that the Tenants or another occupant have wilfully or negligently
damaged the rental unit.  
7.     
The Landlord testified the
Tenants apologized for the damage but have not done anything to remedy or void
the N5 notice served on them.
8.     
The Landlord was unable to
provide any written estimates or quotations to support the claim of $500.00
damage
9.     
With respect to the Tenants
paying the utility bills, the Landlord testified the Tenants have not paid any
amount toward the utility bills since the outset of the tenancy.  The Landlord
confirmed that he must pay the utility bills himself in full so that none of
the services is affected due to non-payment.
10.  
The Landlord confirmed, as
well, that the Tenants have not attempted to remedy or void the N5 notice in
respect to unpaid utility bills.
11.  
As provided for under
subsections 62(3) and 64(3) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the
'Act'), and based on the uncontested testimony and submissions, I find Tenants
failed to void the N5 Notice of Termination that was served on the Tenants on June
20, 2018. 
12.  
As well, and on a balance of
probabilities, I find the Tenants have wilfully or negligently damaged the
rental unit wall and the Tenants’ conduct with regard to utility bills has
substantially interfered with the Landlord’s lawful right or interest of the
Landlord.   With regard to the claim of $500.00 for repair costs, I denied this
claim as the Landlord was unable to substantiate that cost at the hearing.
13.  
I made these determinations
orally at the hearing.  The Landlord confirmed his request under the L2
application for a non-remedial termination of the tenancy, and requested that
the termination date be set for the standard eleven day period.  If granted,
the Landlord requested the L1 application be amended to be an order for arrears
only.
14.  
I have considered all of the
disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Act, and
find that it would be unfair to grant relief from eviction pursuant to
subsection 83(1) of the Act.  The L1 application is hereby converted to an
order for arrears only as the tenancy is to be terminated in a non-remedial,
standard way under the L2 application.
It is ordered that:
 
L1 and L2 Applications
 
1.     
With respect to the L1
application for rent arrears, the Tenants shall pay to the Landlord $2,937.31*,
which represents the amount of rent owing and compensation up to August 3, 2018
(the date of this order), less the rent deposit and interest the Landlord owes
on the rent deposit.
2.     
The Tenants shall also pay
to the Landlord $46.03 per day for compensation for the use of the unit
starting August 4, 2018 to the date they move out of the unit.
3.     
The Tenants shall also pay
to the Landlord $190.00 for the cost of filing the application.
4.     
If the Tenants do not pay
the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before August 14, 2018, the Tenants
will start to owe interest.  This will be simple interest calculated from
August 15, 2018 at 3.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
5.     
With respect to the L2
application that seeks an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenant because
they have caused undue damage to the premises and have
substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment or lawful right,
privilege or interest of the Landlords or another tenant(s):   the tenancy shall terminate in a final way (i.e. cannot
be voided) on August 14, 2018.  
6.     
The Tenant must move out of
the unit on or before August 14, 2018.
7.     
If the unit is not vacated
on or before August 14, 2018, then starting August 15, 2018, the Landlord may
file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the
eviction may be enforced.
8.     
Upon receipt of this order,
the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of
the unit to the Landlord, on or after August 15, 2018.
