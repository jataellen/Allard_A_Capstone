Thakurdei Singh
and Sulakshana Balkaran (the 'Landlords') applied for an order to terminate the
tenancy and evict Patricia Hason
and Jeffrey Mark Mario (the 'Tenants') because
they have been persistently late in paying their
rent.
 
This application was heard via videoconference on September 1, 2021
at 9:00 a.m. The Landlords and the Tenants both attended the hearing.
 
Determinations:
 
1.     The tenancy commenced
June 1, 2020.
 
2.    
The rent is
due on the first of
each month.
 
3.     The Tenants were in possession of rental unit when the application was filed.
 
4.     The lawful monthly
rent is $2,650.00
 
5.    
The Landlord
submitted a rent ledger that shows that rent payments
have been made as
follows:
 
I.       
September 2020    -           payment was made September 4th and 20th, 2020
 
II.       
October 2020          -           payment was made October 2, 20202
and November
1, 2020




 
 
 
III.       
November 2020      -           payment was made November
1 and December 1, 2020
 
IV.       
December 2020      -           payment was made December
4, 2020 and
January 2 and 20th, 2021
 
V.       
January 2021          -           payment was made January
30, 2021
 
VI.       
February 2021        -           payment was made February
20th and 28th, 2021
 
VII.       
March 2021             -           payment was made March 19th and 26th, 2021
 
VIII.       
April 2021                -           no payments were made in
April,
 
IX.       
May 2021                -           a payment was made May 7
 
X.       
June 2021               -           payment was made June 19th and 26th, 2021
 
XI.       
July 2021                 -           payment was made July 16th and 19th, 2021
 
XII.       
August 2021           -           payment was made August
14, 2021
 
6.    
Based on the information submitted, and by the Tenants’
own admission, “Rent has been paid in positions”, the Landlord has
established that the Tenants have been paid the rent after it was due
and payable and have been persistently late paying the rent in 11 of the past
12 months.
 
 
Section 83 considerations:
 
 
7.    
The Tenants
submitted that they have had some health concerns which has caused financial hardship.
 
8.     They are a family
with 4 children aged 18, 14, 12 and 10 years
old.
 
 
It is ordered that:
 
1.    
 The Landlord’s application for eviction
of the Tenants is denied
on the condition that under
the following conditions:
 
a)    
Commencing October 1,
2021 and for the following 11 months, up to and including September 1, 2022, the Tenants shall
on or before the first day of each month pay the Landlord the full monthly rent.




 
2.    
If the Tenant fails to comply
with the conditions set out in paragraph 1 above, then within 30 days of the breach, the Landlord may
apply under section 78 of the Act for an order terminating the tenancy and
evicting the Tenant. The Landlord must make the application within 30 days of a
breach of a condition set out in paragraph 1 of this order.
 
3.    
The Tenants
shall also pay the Landlord
$186.00 for the cost of filing the application.
 
4.    
If the Tenant does not
pay the Landlords the full amount owing on or before January 17, 2022, the Tenant will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest
calculated from January 18,
2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
