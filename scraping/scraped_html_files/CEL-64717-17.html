Order under Section 87 and 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: CEL-64717-17

 


In the matter of:

 

 

 

 

 

Between:

O A(2503-2507) Inc.

Landlord

 

 

 

 

and

 

 

 

 

 

D K

Tenant

 

 

 

 

O A (2503-2507) Inc. (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict D K (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes (L1 Application).

 

The Landlord also applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenant because he has been persistently late in paying his rent (L2 Application).

 

This application was heard in Mississauga on March 7, 2017.

 

The Landlord's Agent, A. P and the Tenant attended the hearing.

 

Preliminary Issue:

 

The Tenant requested an adjournment to obtain legal representation.  The Landlord's Agent opposed the request.  The Tenant said he obtained legal advice from tenant duty counsel.  I denied the request for an adjournment.  Section 183 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act') directs the Board to adopt the most expeditious method of determining the questions arising in a proceeding while affording the parties an adequate opportunity to be heard. The Tenant had an opportunity to retain legal representation prior to the hearing. 

 

At the hearing the Tenant raised the following issues pursuant to section 82 of the Act:

 

a)   Falling concrete from the living room ceiling;

b)   Peeling paint and plaster throughout the unit;

c)   Leaking kitchen sink;

d)   Broken electrical outlets;

e)   Broken front closet door;

f)     Missing silicone around the bathtub;

g)   Clogged kitchen and bathroom vents;

h)   Damaged parquet flooring in the living room;

i)     Toilet missing 3 screws;

j)      Front door lock is loose;

k)   Downstairs buzzer is not working;

l)     Unit requires painting.

 

Determinations and Reasons:

 

Regarding L1 rent arrears application

 

1.      The Tenant has not paid the total rent he was required to pay for the period from February 1, 2017 to March 31, 2017.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective February 16, 2017.

2.      The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $1,142.40 from the Tenant and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

3.      Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenant for the period from September 1, 2016 to February 16, 2017.

Section 82 issues

4.      The Tenant seeks a repair order regarding the maintenance issues complained of pursuant to section 82 of the Act. 

5.      Section 20 of the Act specifies that a landlord is responsible for providing and maintaining a rental unit in a good state of repair, fit for habitation and for complying with health, safety, housing and maintenance standards.

6.      It is undisputed that the leaking kitchen sink, clogged vents in the kitchen and bathroom, parquet floor in the living room and malfunctioning buzzer were repaired by the Landlord on February 27, 2017.

Living room ceiling

 

7.      A portion of the living room ceiling has broken and concrete like pieces have fallen into the Tenant’s living room space on January 22, 2017 and again on February 26, 2017.  The Tenant cleaned up the debris on January 22nd and did not tell the Landlord until February 26th at which time the Landlord assisted him with the cleanup.  The Tenant submitted photos of the damaged ceiling and debris.

8.      The Landlord has not repaired the issue and believes the ceiling damage might have been caused by the Tenant banging the ceiling with a broom.

9.      The Landlord breached section 20 of the Act by failing to repair the living room ceiling.  The Landlord has known about this issue since February 26th and acknowledged that no action to repair the ceiling has been taken.  I am not satisfied that the photo presented by the Landlord of a round spot on the living room ceiling with three marks is indicative that the Tenant banged the ceiling with a broom handle.  I find the Tenant’s testimony that this spot was where a smoke detector was previously installed to be more plausible.  In any event, the Landlord is required to maintain the unit in a good state of repair pursuant to section 20 of the Act.

Peeling paint and plaster

 

10.   The Landlord breached section 20 of the Act by failing to repair peeling paint and plaster in the rental unit. There is peeling paint and plaster on the wall beside the stove in the kitchen, walls in the bedroom, the bathroom ceiling and wall above the sink and the top of the living room window.  The Tenant submitted photos of these areas and reported these issues to the Landlord on February 17, 2017.

11.   The Landlord attended the unit on February 18th to investigate the repair issues however these repairs have not yet been done.

Kitchen Electrical outlets

12.   The Landlord replaced the kitchen outlet covers on February 27th.  The Tenant acknowledged that the electrical outlets were replaced on February 27th.  However, at the hearing the Tenant said the internal part of the outlet does not function properly and the light switch is loose.

13.   The Tenant did not report the loose light switch or the malfunctioning electrical outlet to the Landlord.   Therefore no breach is found regarding these issues and these claims are dismissed.

Front closet door

 

14.   The Landlord breached section 20 of the Act by failing to maintain the front hall closet door in a good state of repair. The front hall closet door requires painting.  The Tenant told the Landlord in mid-February 2017 and the Landlord was aware of the issue when the Landlord attended the unit on February 18th to inspect this door.

Silicone in bathroom

 

15.   The issue regarding missing silicone in the bathroom is not indicative of a repair issue and although the Tenant’s reasons for requesting that the base of the tile wall around the tub and base of the tub be sealed are reasonable, I do not find a breach of section 20 of the Act.  This is more of a cosmetic issue.  This claim is dismissed.

Toilet

 

16.   It is disputed whether the repair to securely affix the toilet to the floor was completed on February 27, 2017 by the Landlord. The best evidence was the service report from the Landlord dated February 27, 2017 which indicates that the loose screws at the base of the toilet were fixed.  No breach is found because the Tenant did not report the issue to the Landlord after February 27, 2017 and the Landlord cannot be held responsible for an issue it did not know about.  This claim is dismissed.

Entry door lock

17.   The Tenant did not provide sufficient evidence to establish that repair was required regarding his entry door lock.  The photo submitted by the Tenant did not demonstrate that repair was required.  The Landlord’s inspection report dated February 18th did not indicate any repair to the entry door lock.  The Tenant is requesting a new keyed entry lock for the bottom door knob which is not a repair.  As there is no breach of section 20 of the Act, this claim is dismissed.

Repaint the unit

18.   The Tenant requested repainting of the rental unit.  There was insufficient evidence presented to support a finding that the Landlord breached section 20 of the Act by failing to repaint the rental unit.  This claim is dismissed other than where painting is ordered in relation to the repair of peeling paint and plaster.

L2 persistent late application

19.   The Landlord served the Tenant with an N8 notice of termination effective April 30, 2017 which alleged that the Tenant persistently paid rent late.

20.   The Tenant persistently paid rent late. It is undisputed that the Tenant paid rent late 8 times out of the last 12 months. The rent is due on the first day of each month and this is a monthly tenancy.

Relief from eviction

21.   Subsection 83(3) of the Act states in part: “[Without] restricting the generality of subsection 83(1), the Board shall refuse to grant the application where satisfied that, (a) the landlord is in serious breach of the landlord’s responsibilities under this Act or of any material covenant in the tenancy agreement” [emphasis added].

22.   The photographs of the fallen concrete pieces submitted by the Tenant are indicative of a serious breach of the Landlord’s responsibilities pursuant to section 20 of the Act.  I note that on January 22nd a piece of concrete that could cause injury to a person fell on his couch. While the chunks of concrete appear smaller on February 26th, in my view they present a safety risk.  I find that this issue, which the Landlord has known about since February 26th and has failed to take steps to repair is a health or safety concern for the Tenant and does not represent a good state of repair. 

23.   Therefore, pursuant to subsection 83(3) of the Act, as the Landlord is in serious breach of the Landlord’s responsibilities under the Act, eviction must be refused. The L1 application is converted to an L9 application which permits an order for arrears of rent only.  The L2 application must be dismissed.

 

It is ordered that:

 

L9 Application for rent arrears only:

 

1.      The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $2,331.42, which represents the amount of rent owing up to March 31, 2017.

2.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $190.00 for the cost of filing the application.

3.      If the Tenant does not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before March 31, 2017, the Tenant will start to owe interest.  This will be simple interest calculated from April 1, 2017 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.

4.      The Landlord shall repair the living room ceiling on or before April 13, 2017*.

5.      The Landlord shall complete the following repairs to the rental unit on or before April 21, 2017*:

a)   Repair the plaster and paint the wall beside the stove in the kitchen, walls in the bedroom, the bathroom ceiling and wall above the bathroom sink and the top of the living room window;

b)   Paint the front closet door.

L2 Application persistent late:

 

6.      The L2 application is dismissed.
