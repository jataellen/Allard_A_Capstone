Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

File Number: CEL-66033-17

 

 


DW and SW (the 'Landlords') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict RM and MC (the 'Tenants') because the Tenants did not pay the rent that the Tenants owe.

 

The Landlords also applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenants because they have been persistently late in paying their rent (L2 Application).

 

This application was heard in Mississauga on April 12, 2017 and May 11, 2017.

 

The Tenants and the Tenants Legal Representative, PR attended the hearing.


The Landlords and the Landlords Legal Representative, SS attended the hearing.

 

Preliminary Matters:

 

1.   This matter was heard with CET-65230-17. Separate orders will be issued.

Determinations:

 

            Lawful Rent

 

1.      The Tenants dispute their lawful rent and the arrears claimed by the Landlord because they aid that they should be paying only $1,500.00 a month.

2.      The Tenants testified that there was a verbal agreement with the Landlords to reduce the rent from $1,850.00 to $1,500.00 if the Tenant’s nephew moved out of the rental unit in February 2017.

3.      The Landlords dispute that there was an agreement to reduce the rent from $1,850.00.

4.      The Tenant’s nephew (AM) was deemed an occupant of the household in order CET-65230-17.

5.      There was insufficient evidence to establish that the parties had a verbal agreement to reduce the rent based on the number of people occupying the home. Therefore, I find that the lawful rent is $1,850.00 and the Tenants owe the arrears as claimed by the Landlords.

6.      The Tenants have not paid the total rent they were required to pay for the period from February 1, 2017 to May 31, 2017.  Because of the arrears, the Landlords served a Notice of Termination effective April 6, 2017. The arrears outstanding up-to May 31, 2017 are $4,111.40, plus the $190.00 application filing fee, which totals $4,301.40.

7.      The Tenants said that the rent was not due on the fixed date every month. This was disputed by the Landlords who said that it was due on the first of the month.

8.      I find that the rent is more likely than not due on the first of the month, particularly since the tenancy commenced on May 1, 2016. I do not find there was an agreement between the parties to pay on any date during the month.

9.      I find that the Tenants have persistently failed to pay the rent on the date it was due every month since the June 2016. A copy of the Landlord’s ledger was submitted (Landlord Exhibit #1).

10.   The Landlords collected a rent deposit of $1,850.00 from the Tenants and this deposit is still being held by the Landlords.

11.   Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenants for the period from May 1, 2016 to April 6, 2017.

12.   The Tenants proposed a payment plan in order to secure their tenancy. The Tenants proposed an additional $600.00 a month until the arrears are paid. In the alternative, the Tenants requested three months to move because their children attend school in the neighbourhood. The Tenants said that one of their children was accepted into a French immersion program nearby.

13.   The Landlords opposed a payment plan because the Tenants have only made minimal payments towards the rent and due to the amount of arrears outstanding. The Landlords reside next door and said that the relationship between the parties has also deteriorated significantly. The Landlords are seeking to terminate the tenancy for May 31, 2017.

14.   I am denying the Tenants request for a payment plan because of their failure to pay rent on time for the past twelve months, the failure to pay the rent in full every month, the significant amount of arrears outstanding, there have been no good faith payments made to the Landlords even though both Tenants are fully employed and because the current relationship between the parties is strained.

15.   However, I am granting the Tenants an extension to June 30, 2017 to allow the Tenants additional time to find alternate accommodations in the neighborhood and for their children to finish the school year. The financial prejudice to the Landlords is minimized given that there is a last month’s rent deposit.

16.   I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would not be unfair to postpone the eviction until June 30, 2017 pursuant to subsection 83(1)(b) of the Act.

It is ordered that:

 

1.      The tenancy between the Landlords and the Tenants is terminated.  The Tenants must move out of the rental unit on or before June 30, 2017.

2.      The Tenants shall pay to the Landlords $2,123.87*, which represents the amount of rent owing and compensation up to May 29, 2017, less the rent deposit and interest the Landlords owe on the rent deposit.

3.      The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlords $60.82 per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting May 30, 2017 to the date they move out of the unit.

4.      The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlords $190.00 for the cost of filing the application.

5.      If the Tenants do not pay the Landlords the full amount owing* on or before June 9, 2017, the Tenants will start to owe interest.  This will be simple interest calculated from June 10, 2017 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.

6.      If the unit is not vacated on or before June 30, 2017, then starting July 1, 2017, the Landlords may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

7.      Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlords, on or after July 1, 2017.
