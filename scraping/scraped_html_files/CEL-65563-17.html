K M (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict J F, M F and F R (the 'Tenants') because the Tenants did not pay the rent that the Tenants owe.

 

This application was heard in Bracebridge on April 13, 2017.

 

The Landlord, Landlord's Legal Representative, K. F and the Tenants J. F, M. F (MF) and F. R (FR) attended the hearing. The Tenants were assisted by tenant duty counsel, J. S.

 

Determinations:

 

1.      The Tenants have not paid the total rent they were required to pay for the period from October 1, 2016 to April 30, 2017.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective March 31, 2017.

2.      The Tenants vacated the rental unit as of March 31, 2017.

Rent deposit

3.      The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $1,200.00 from the Tenants.

4.      The Landlord said the rent deposit was used for September 2016 arrears.

5.      The Tenants dispute the use of the rent deposit.

6.      Subsection 106(10) of the Residential Tenancies Act 2006 (the 'Act') provides that a landlord shall apply a rent deposit in payment of the rent for the last rent period before the tenancy terminates.

7.      Applying the rent deposit to September 2016 arrears contravenes subsection 106(10) of the Act because this was not the last rent period before the tenancy terminated on March 31, 2017.

8.      Therefore I find that the rent deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

9.      Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenants for the period from March 1, 2015 to March 31, 2017.

Amount of rent arrears

10.   The monthly rent is $1,200.00.

11.   The Tenants dispute the amount of rent arrears for rent owed from October 2016 to January 2017.

12.   It is undisputed that rent is unpaid for February and March 2017.

13.   FR testified that she made a cash payment of $1,220.00 sometime in October to the Landlord’s girlfriend who was at his home.  In support of her testimony, the Tenants submitted bank statements showing withdrawals of $800.00 and $420.00 on October 3rd from two different bank accounts.  The Landlord denies receipt of this payment.

14.   I find it more probable than not that the Tenants made the payment of $1,220.00 to the Landlord. I acknowledge that FR did not know the exact date she made the payment. However her testimony was supported by bank statements and she provided details about counting the money with the Landlord’s girlfriend whose name she also provided during cross examination.  Her testimony was essentially consistent when cross examined by the Landlord's Legal Representative.

15.   MF testified that he made a cash payment to the Landlord for $1,500.00 for November rent. He said he made the payment to the Landlord likely on November 3rd or 4th at either his house or shop which is located next door. He submitted a bank statement showing a withdrawal of $1,500.00 on November 3, 2016.  The Landlord denies receipt of this payment.

16.   I am satisfied on a balance of probabilities that MF made the payment of $1,500.00 to the Landlord.  The amount and date of the withdrawal on the bank statement corroborated MF’s testimony.

17.   MF testified that he made a cash payment to the Landlord for $860.00 on January 14, 2017.  He submitted a bank statement showing two withdrawals on January 3, 2017 for $360.00 and $500.00.  He also submitted text messages he sent to the Landlord from January 4th to January 13th informing the Landlord that he had the rent and a response from the Landlord that payment on January 14th was acceptable.  The Landlord denies receipt of this payment.

18.   I find that it is more probable than not that MF made a payment of $860.00 to the Landlord on January 14th.  MF provided bank statements and text messages to support his testimony and provided details about the meeting with his Landlord when he made the payment.

19.   I am not satisfied that the Tenants made a payment of $1,280.00 in December.  The Tenants first testified that they were unsure when this payment was made and then FR testified that she made the payment in December. FR submitted her bank statement showing a withdrawal on December 1, 2016 for $1,000.00 and a bank statement from one of the other Tenants with a withdrawal dated November 18, 2016 for $280.00.  These withdrawal dates are several weeks apart and the testimony from the Tenants was not consistent.

20.   I am mindful of the submissions by the Landlord's Legal Representative regarding an absence of receipts for the payments made by the Tenants.  However, the Landlord's Legal Representative also stated that this was a long standing friendship.  In this context, it is plausible that payment of rent through cash payments and without receipts would occur.  The receipt submitted by the Landlord was for a period of time and not for each individual payment. The Landlord did not provide a copy of the ledger to demonstrate his record keeping for rent payments.

21.   I also acknowledge that the Tenants were unable to recall specific dates and times when payments were made.  However, my findings are based on consideration of the overall evidence provided by the Tenants whose testimony was tested through cross examination.  I agree with the submission from tenant duty counsel that the Tenants’ ability to recall is likely affected by the passage of time, in this case, 4-6 months ago.

22.   The Tenants paid $3,580.00 after the application was filed.

23.   I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Act and find that it would not be necessary to grant relief from eviction pursuant to subsection 83(1) of the Act because the Tenants vacated the rental unit.

It is ordered that:

 

1.      The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenants terminated as of March 31, 2017.

2.      The Tenants shall pay to the Landlord $2,376.40*, which represents the amount of rent owing to March 31, 2017, less the rent deposit and interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.

3.      The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlord $190.00 for the cost of filing the application.

4.      If the Tenants do not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before May 5, 2017, the Tenants will start to owe interest.  This will be simple interest calculated from May 6, 2017 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
