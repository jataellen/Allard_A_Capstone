Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: SWL-00849-17

 


RP (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict RJ (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or someone the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has wilfully or negligently caused undue damage to the premises. The Landlord has also applied for an order requiring the Tenant to compensate the Landlord for the damage. The Landlord also claimed compensation for each day the Tenant remained in the unit after the termination date. (L2)

 

The Landlord also applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenant because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes. (L2)

 

The Landlord also applied for an order determining whether the Tenant altered the locking system without the Landlord's consent. (L8)

 

This application was heard in [CITY] on June 28, 2018.  The Landlord attended.

As of 1:00 p.m., the Tenant was not present or represented at the hearing although properly served with notice of this hearing by the Board.

 

BACKGROUND

 

These applications were first scheduled for hearing on April 27, 2017.  On that day the Landlord attended.  The Tenant attended the hearing along with the Tenant’s Legal Representative, TC.  The Tenant’s Legal Representative requested that the hearing be adjourned until the Tenant’s action in Superior Court File No. 6677/17 had been resolved.  The hearing was adjourned and reconvened by telephone hearing to determine the status of the matte several times. 

 

When the matter resumed by telephone hearing on June 12, 2018, the Tenant’s Legal Representative advised that the Superior Court matter had been settled and that the Tenant concedes that the Board has jurisdiction. 

 

When the hearing resumed on June 28, 2018, the Tenant’s Legal Representative requested that he be removed as solicitor of record because he had been unable to obtain instructions from his client.  He confirmed that the Tenant was aware of the hearing date but was not in attendance.  I granted the Tenant’s Legal Representative’s request and the matter proceeded in the Tenant’s absence.

 

Determinations:

Application to Terminate a Tenancy and Evict a Tenant (L2)

Notice to Terminate a Tenancy Early (N5)

 

1.      The Tenant has wilfully or negligently caused undue damage to the rental unit by: removing a screen door, and railing from the porch and by putting a gas stove outside. 

 

2.      The Landlord will incur costs of $850.00 to repair the damage and replace property that was damaged and cannot be reasonably repaired.

Application Because the Tenant Changed the Locks

 

3.      The Landlord withdrew the application about changing locks.

Application to Evict a Tenant for Non-payment of Rent and to Collect the Rent the Tenant Owes (L1)

4.      The Tenant has not paid the total rent the Tenant was required to pay for the period from February 1, 2015 to December 31, 2017.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective March 16, 2017.

 

5.      The Tenant is in possession of the rental unit.

 

6.      The monthly rent is $600.00.

 

7.      The Landlord is not holding a last month's rent deposit.

 

8.      The Tenant has made no payments since the application was filed.

 

9.      I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would be unfair to grant relief from eviction pursuant to subsection 83(1) of the Act.

It is ordered that:

Application to Terminate a Tenancy and Evict a Tenant (L2)

 

1.      The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $850.00, which represents the reasonable costs of repairing the damage and replacing the damaged property.

 

2.      The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant is terminated.  The Tenant must move out of the rental unit on or before July 14, 2018.

Application to Evict a Tenant for Non-payment of Rent and to Collect the Rent the Tenant Owes (L1)

3.      The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $14,967.64*, which represents the amount of rent owing and compensation up to July 3, 2018.

 

4.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $19.73 per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting July 4, 2018 to the date the Tenant moves out of the unit.

 

5.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $190.00 for the cost of filing the application.

 

6.      If the Tenant does not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before July 14, 2018, the Tenant will start to owe interest.  This will be simple interest calculated from July 15, 2018 at .00% annually on the balance outstanding.

 

7.      If the unit is not vacated on or before July 14, 2018, then starting July 15, 2018, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

 

8.      Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord, on or after July 15, 2018.
