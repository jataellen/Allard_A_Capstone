Review Order

 

 

MLPM # O L (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict F D S (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes. (L1 application)

 

The Landlord also applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenant because the Tenant has been persistently late in paying the Tenant's rent. The Landlord also claimed compensation for each day the Tenant remained in the unit after the termination date. (L2 application)

 

This L1/L2 application was resolved by order TSL-06955-19 issued on August 8, 2019.

 

On August 19, 2019, the Tenant requested a review of the order.

 

The request was heard in Toronto on September 19, 2019.

 

The Landlordâs legal representative L. C and the Tenant attended the hearing.

 

 Determinations:

 

Review

1.      The Tenantâs review request is on the ground of being not reasonably able to participate in the proceedings. The Tenant did not attend the hearing that gave rise to the order under review.

 

2.      The Tenantâs review request was not contested by the Landlord and so, at the hearing, I granted the review request. The Landlordâs L1/L2 application proceeded de novo.

 

3.      The Landlord seeks an order for $324.00 which is the cost the Landlord incurred to file TSL-06955-19 issued on August 8, 2019 with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) for enforcement. This amount is non-refundable.

 

4.      The Tenant objects to the Board awarding this amount to the Landlord.

 

5.      Pursuant to s. 204(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act') the Board may order a party to an application to pay the costs of another party. The Boardâs Interpretation Guideline 3 on Costs is silent on the issue of this enforcement fee but the Act is not. In a slightly different context, subsection 74(15) of the Act makes it very clear that if a landlord incurs non-refundable enforcement costs the tenant must pay those costs if he or she wishes to remain in the unit and avoid eviction. There is no discretion in the Board not to order a tenant to pay those costs.

 

6.      I believe that in these rare cases where a Landlord has incurred a non-refundable fee to enforce the eviction but the termination order is cancelled on review, the Tenant can and should be ordered to compensate the Landlord for the non-refundable fee. In cases like this the tenantâs request for a review is akin to a motion to void, in which tenants are ordered to pay the Sheriffâs fee as a matter of course. In neither instance is the tenant necessarily at fault. The main consideration is that a landlord, who is also not at fault, has incurred a non-refundable fee when validly enforcing an order to evict.

 

7.      Accordingly, the Tenant will be ordered to compensate the Landlord for the Sheriffâs fee.

L1 application

8.      The Tenant has not paid the total rent the Tenant was required to pay for the period from April 1, 2019 to September 30, 2019.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination.

 

9.      The Tenant also owes the Landlord $175.00 for the cost of filing the application.

 

10.   The total amount owing is $4,047.15.

 

11.   I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Act, and find that it would not be unfair to grant relief from eviction subject to the conditions set out in this order pursuant to subsection 83(1)(a) and 204(1) of the Act. The Tenant proposed a payment plan by which the arrears would be paid off in two months.

 

L2 application

 

12.   The Tenant has paid rent persistently late.

 

13.   I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Act, and find that it would not be unfair to grant relief from eviction subject to the conditions set out in this order pursuant to subsection 83(1)(a) and 204(1) of the Act. The Tenant needs time to recover from recent financial problems but once he has recovered he will be able to pay rent promptly.
