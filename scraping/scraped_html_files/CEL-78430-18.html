File Number: CEL-78430-18

Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

AGSPM (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict AG (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes. (L1 Application)

 

The Landlord also applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenant because she, another occupant of the rental unit or someone she permitted in the residential complex has substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment or lawful right, privilege or interest of the Landlord or another tenant and/or she, another occupant of the rental unit or someone she permitted in the residential complex has wilfully or negligently caused undue damage to the premises (N5 Notice); because she or another occupant of the rental unit has committed an illegal act or has carried out, or permitted someone to carry out an illegal trade, business or occupation in the rental unit or the residential complex (N6 Notice); and because she has been persistently late in paying her rent (N8 Notice).  (L2 Application)

 

These applications were heard in Barrie on September 21, 2018.

 

Only the Landlordâs Legal Representative, AS, and the Landlordâs Agent, RH, attended the hearing.  CH appeared as a witness for the Landlord.

 

Determinations:

 

1.      CH testified she works for the Landlord and helps to property-manage the complex, including the rental unit.

2.      CH testified that on September 12, 2018, she attended the rental unit.  At that time, CH spoke with the Tenant.  CH stated the Tenant had told her that she was going to attend the scheduled hearing to present her case.

3.      AS submitted that the hearing should proceed on an uncontested basis.  AS stated the Tenant has been before the Board on other applications, is well aware of the process, and had indicated to CH that she would be in attendance today.  I agreed and therefore proceeded to hear the applications on an uncontested basis.

 

L1 Application

 

4.      The Tenant has not paid the total rent the Tenant was required to pay for the period from July 1, 2018 to September 30, 2018.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective July 26, 2018.

5.      The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $1,528.02 from the Tenant and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

6.      Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenant for the period from February 1, 2018 to July 26, 2018.

 

L2 Application

N8 Notice

7.      In support of the Landlordâs claim the Tenant has been persistently late in paying her rent, AS submitted the Tenantâs ledger (exhibit LL#1) and cited three previous L1 applications disposed of before the Board in the past three years.

8.      I find the Tenant has persistently failed to pay the rent on the date it was due.  I am prepared to grant the termination of the tenancy as requested by the Landlord, subject to the findings under the other notices under this L2 application and any section 83 considerations.

N6 Notice

9.      RH testified that on July 17, 2018, he conducted a routine inspection of the rental unit with a Barrie Fire Prevention Officer and found the smoke/CO alarm was removed from the ceiling.  He stated he found the alarm on table belonging to the Tenant.

10.   RH testified the rental unit is one of two units on one side of a semi-detached building.  RH stated the only other alarm that he found in place was in the other unit.

11.   RH also observed two black-smoke type marks on the wall and ceiling of the main hallway.

12.   RH testified the Fire Prevention Officer confirmed the black-smoke mark on the ceiling was due to smoke damage, but could not determine the origin of the wall mark.  AS submitted a number of exhibits as part of RHâs testimony.

13.   Exhibit LL#2 was submitted, which is the Tenantâs signed acknowledgment in the original lease respecting the maintenance of operational smoke/CO detectors.

14.   Exhibit LL#3 comprises photos of the alarm that CH testified he found on the Tenantâs table, and the alarm properly reinstalled after his inspection was completed. 

15.   Exhibits LL#4 and LL#5 are copies of the Chief Fire Prevention Officerâs September 17, 2018 email (confirming a ticket was issued on July 17th against the Tenant) and of the actual ticket for $360.00 that was issued.

16.   Exhibit LL#6 contains photos of the black-smoke marks found by RH on July 17th.

17.   Exhibit LL#8 comprises photos from a number of inspections and in respect of the July 17th inspection, a photo clearly showing an ashtray with fresh looking cigarette butts.  RH testified that the rental unit is a ânon-smokingâ unit, as is the complex.  RH referred to LL#1 to show the Tenant is well aware that the unit is a ânon-smokingâ unit.

18.   RH confirmed that on September 12, 2018, he conducted a follow-up inspection of the unit and found the smoke/CO in its proper place and operational.

19.   Based on the uncontested testimony and evidence, I find the Tenant or an occupant or guest of the Tenant, has committed an illegal act in the rental unit by disabling and removing the smoke/CO alarm from its installed place.  Reference is made to the Fire Protection and Prevention Act, 1997, Ontario Regulation 213/07, subsections 6.3.3.6 and 6.3.3.5(1)(c).

N5 Notice

20.   RH testified that when the tenancy began on December 1, 2014, the Tenant inspected the rental unit and signed-off on a âRental Unit Condition Reportâ (exhibit LL#7).  RH confirmed there were no problems or issues raised in that report.

21.   RH was asked to go through photographs in exhibit LL#8, which contained photographs taken at inspections of the rental unit on December 1, 2014 (when the tenancy began), July 17, 2018, July 26, 2018 and August 14, 2018.

22.   RH confirmed that the rental unit was in very good, clean condition on December 14, 2018.

23.   For the July 17 and 26, 2018 inspections, RH identified, item by item, the damage to the rental unit that he had found.  The damaged items he identified are all contained and clearly laid out in the two N5 notices that had been served on the Tenant (the 1st N5 was served on July 17, 2018 and the 2nd N5 was served on July 31, 2018).  The first N5 notice identifies an estimated a total of $8,950.00 (including taxes) to repair all of the undue damage.

24.   At the hearing, RH identified two missing kitchen cabinet doors, punctures and damaged areas in certain walls, a damaged toilet tank, damaged fridge drawers and shelf, missing screens, soiled/damaged carpet, water damage to kitchen ceiling, rubbish/garbage on the deck and in the back yard, and clutter throughout the unit including a partially blocked stairwell.

25.   For the July 26th inspection, RH identified some of the effort by the Tenant to roughly patch over some of the punctured and damage walls, but RH confirmed that the Tenant did not remedy the first N5 notice within the 7-day voiding period.  For example, the two kitchen cabinet doors were still missing.

26.   RH testified that since the Tenant did move some of the clutter out of the way to make the unit cleaner, he was able to confirm other undue damage such as damaged bi-fold closet doors and missing window screens were not repaired.  As well, whatever patchwork the Tenant had attempted was unsatisfactory as the patches were rough in appearance.

27.   For the August 14th inspection, RH confirmed that one of the two missing kitchen cabinet doors was re-installed.  RH testified the Tenant had attempted to paint over some of the patchwork but the paint was not the same colour as the original paint. 

28.   RH testified that he went out and secured three quotations for the repair all of the undue damage in the rental unit, including carpet replacement and painting of the unit.  The three quotations were submitted as exhibit LL#9.  I noted that all three quotations by the arms-length companies exceed the $8,950.00 that was quoted in the first N5 notice and L2 application.  AS confirmed that the Landlord understands this and is willing to accept the lower dollar amount, as given in the N5 notice.

29.   Based on the testimony and evidence provided, I find the Tenant has wilfully or negligently caused damage to the rental unit and/or the residential complex.  Further, I find the Tenant did not void the N5 Notice within the seven-day period provided for under subsection 62(3) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the âActâ).  

30.   It is clear to me from the chronology of events as testified to by RH, and I so find, that the second N5 notice served on the Tenant is in compliance with the provisions of subsections 68(1) and (2) of the Act.

31.   As a result, I find the Landlord has incurred, or will incur, reasonable costs of $8,950.00 to repair the damage and/or replace property that was damaged and which could not be reasonably repaired.

32.   I made these findings at the hearing orally and indicated I am willing to grant the termination of the tenancy on a non-remedial (final) basis.

Final Submissions on Possible Relief from Eviction

33.   AS requested a non-remedial termination under the L2 application be tied to the 11-day standard period that was she requested for the L1 application.  AS argued the testimony and evidence has shown that the cluttered and damaged state of the unit, along with the illegal action taken by the Tenant in respect of the disabled smoke alarm, should warrant such a termination date.

34.   AS indicated that she is not aware of any other circumstance that might affect the granting of the remedy the Landlord is seeking.

 

Section 83

 

35.   I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Act, and find that it would be unfair to grant relief from eviction pursuant to subsection 83(1) of the Act.

L1 and L2 Applications

 

1.      The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant is terminated effectively on October 8, 2018. 

2.      The Tenant must move out of the rental unit on or before October 8, 2018.

3.      The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $2,866.07*, which represents the amount of rent owing and compensation up to September 27, 2018 (the date of this order), less the rent deposit and interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.

4.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $50.24 per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting September 28, 2018 to the date the Tenant moves out of the unit.

5.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $190.00 for the cost of filing the application.

6.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $8,950.00, which represents the reasonable costs of repairing the damage and/or replacing the damaged property.

 

7.      If the Tenant does not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before October 8, 2018, the Tenant will start to owe interest.  This will be simple interest calculated from October 9, 2018 at 3.00% annually on the balance outstanding.

8.      If the unit is not vacated on or before October 8, 2018, then starting October 9, 2018, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

 

 

 

9.      Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord, on or after October 9, 2018.
