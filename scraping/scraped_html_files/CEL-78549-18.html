File Number: CEL-78549-18

 

Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 


CF (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict CM (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes.  (L1 Application)

 

The Landlord also applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenant because the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or someone the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment or lawful right, privilege or interest of the Landlord or another tenant. The Landlord also claimed compensation for each day the Tenant remained in the unit after the termination date. (L2 Application)

 

These applications were heard in Barrie on September 21, 2018.

 

Only the Landlord and the Landlordâs Legal Representative, IS, attended the hearing.  AM signed in as a witness for the Landlord but did not testify.

 

Determinations:

 

L1 Application

 

1.      The Tenant has not paid the total rent the Tenant was required to pay for the period from July 1, 2018 to September 30, 2018.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective August 16, 2018.

2.      The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $1,550.00 from the Tenant and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

3.      Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenant for the period from January 1, 2018 to August 16, 2018.

 

L2 Application

4.      The Landlord served the Tenant with a Notice to End Your Tenancy for Interfering With Others, Damage of Overcrowding (the âN5 noticeâ) on August 1, 2018, which clearly set out the Landlordâs claims relating to the Tenant not paying their share of the utility bills, the Tenantâs unauthorized parking of their car, the Tenant causing undue noise and the Tenantâs failure to properly dispose of garbage.  The Landlord is seeking the non-remedial (non-voidable) termination of the tenancy in this L2 application.

5.      IL submitted the following exhibits in support of the Landlordâs L2 claims:

â¢        Exhibit LL#4 â photographs of the Tenantâs blue Hyundai vehicle parked incorrectly in the other building tenantâs spot on the driveway on July 24, 2018, August 2, 2018 and August 28, 2018;

â¢        Exhibit LL#5 â copies of the Landlordâs July 12, 2018 letter of notice to the Tenant, advising the Tenant that he has parked incorrectly in the other tenantâs spot for over 5 weeks without correction, and of the Tenantâs emailed reply on July 13, 2018, stating his cousin and girlfriend were âhouse sittingâ and that he will remedy the situation;

â¢        Exhibit LL#6 â copy of the Landlordâs July 16, 2018 letter of notice to the Tenant, advising the Tenant of excessive noise coming from the rental unit and of a large amount of garbage piling outside the rental unit;

Utility Bills

6.      IL stated that despite a number of attempts by the Landlord to notify the Tenant as to unpaid utility bills (as exemplified by LL#1, LL#2, LL#3), the Tenant has yet to pay his 60% share of the utility bills, which now totals $480.28.  The Landlord confirmed, as well, that the Tenant has not attempted to remedy or void the N5 notice in respect to unpaid utility bills.

7.      As provided for under subsection 64(3) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and based on the uncontested submission, I find Tenant failed to void the N5 notice that was served on the Tenant on August 1, 2018.  As such, I find the Tenantâs conduct with regard to utility bills has substantially interfered with the Landlordâs lawful right or interest of the Landlord.

Parking

8.      IL submitted that the Landlord duly notified the Tenant about the Tenant parking in the other tenantâs spot and that the Tenant did indicate he would correct the situation.  However, IL had the Landlord confirm at the hearing that the Tenant never did correct the parking issue and is still parking in the wrong spot (per LL#4, LL#5).

9.      Here, I find the Tenantâs conduct with regard to the issue of incorrect parking was not voided within the seven day voiding period and has substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment by the other tenant of the rental unit premises.

Noise

10.   IL submitted that the issue of noise, as evidenced by LL#6, was indeed remedied by the Tenant satisfactorily and the Landlord withdraws that claim from this L2 application.  As this presented no prejudice to the Tenant, I accept the Landlordâs withdrawal of this particular claim.

Garbage

11.   IL explained that the Tenant and the other tenant of the building (in a different unit) both have been given their own garbage, recycling and compost bins for garbage disposal.

12.   IL stated that despite a number of attempts by the Landlord to notify the Tenant about the increasing collection of garbage immediately outside the rental unit went unheeded by the Tenant to the point that the garbage started to rot badly enough to bring maggots to the property (LL#7).  The Landlord testified that after the City of Brampton placed him on notice (LL#8) about the unacceptable amount of garbage piling up, he paid the other tenant of the rental unit premises to collect and properly dispose of the garbage in August 2018.  However, IL stated the Tenant has continued to store his garbage outside as late as September 4, 2018 (last set of photos is LL#8).

13.   Again, I find the Tenantâs conduct with regard to the issue of not properly collecting and disposing his garbage was not voided within the seven day voiding period and has substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment of the Landlord or another tenant, and the lawful rights of the Landlord.

Section 83

14.   I made the foregoing determinations orally at the hearing.  The Landlord confirmed his request under the L2 application for a non-remedial termination of the tenancy, and requested that the termination date be set for the standard eleven day period.  If granted, the Landlord requested the L1 application be amended to be an order for arrears only.

15.   I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Act, and find that it would be unfair to grant relief from eviction pursuant to subsection 83(1) of the Act.  The L1 application is hereby converted to an order for arrears only as the tenancy is to be terminated in a non-remedial, standard way under the L2 application.
