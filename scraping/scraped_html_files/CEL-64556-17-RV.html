Review Order

 

T T T (TTT) (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict O St (OS) aka N M D B (NMDB) (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes.

 

The Landlord’s L1 application was resolved by order CEL-64556-17, issued on March 16, 2017.

 

On March 27, 2017, the Tenant requested a review of that order.

 

The Tenant’s review request was heard in Mississauga on April 11, 2017.

 

The Landlord and the Tenant attended the hearing.

 

Preliminary Matter:

 

The Tenant provided documentation of a name change. He stated that his legal name is OS but since March 2016 he is also known as NMDB. The application was amended to include the second name that the Tenant is now known as. The Landlord was not aware of the name change but was not opposed to amending the application to add the second name.

 

Determinations and Reasons:

 

1.      The Tenant was not reasonably able to participate in the original hearing on March 15, 2017. He arrived late for the hearing on March 15th and sat in Hearing Room 1. He was unaware his case had been transferred to Hearing Room 2 and it was heard in his absence.

2.      The Tenant’s review request was granted at the review hearing and the Landlord’s L1 application was re-heard.

3.      An interim order was issued on March 27, 2017 which required the Tenant to pay the April rent of $650.00 to the Board in trust on or before April 4, 2017.

4.      The Tenant did not comply with the interim order. No amount was paid to the Board. No amount was paid to the Landlord for April rent either. The Tenant stated that his employment is seasonal, his income fluctuates and he does not have the money to pay the April rent.

5.      The interim order states that the Board may refuse to hear the evidence and submissions of the Tenant at the review hearing if the April rent was not paid to the Board in trust. Yet, the Tenant was permitted to present his rent receipts and evidence regarding the amount of rent arrears owing.

The Rent Arrears Owing

6.      The monthly rent is $650.00.  There is a last month's rent deposit of $650.00. This is undisputed.

7.      However, the amount of rent arrears owing is disputed.

8.      The Landlord claims that there is $4,080.00 in rent arrears owing to March 31, 2017 plus $650.00 for the April rent for a total of $4,730.00 rent owing to April 30, 2017.

9.      The Tenant did not state an exact amount that he thinks is owing. He merely presented a number of rent receipts and a letter from the Landlord dated January 8, 2017 and stated that the amount owing is less than the Landlord is claiming.

10.   It is undisputed that the tenancy began in February 2015.

11.   The rent paid in 2015 is not in dispute. The rent paid in 2017 is also not in dispute. The Tenant’s dispute relates to the rent paid and owing for 2016.

12.   The Tenant presented a number of rent receipts at the hearing. Those receipts establish that the rent was fully paid for each month in 2015.

13.   The rent receipts also establish that the full rent was not paid for every month in 2016 or in 2017.

14.   For the twelve month period in 2016 the Tenant should have paid the Landlord $7,800.00 in rent ($650.00 per month x 12 months).

15.   The Tenant presented the following seven receipts for 2016: $650.00 paid February 11, 2016; $650.00 paid June 24, 2016; $650.00 paid July 20, 2016; $380.00 paid August 4, 2016; $400.00 paid August 24, 2016; $500.00 paid September 21, 2016; and $400.00 paid October 7, 2016. These receipts total $3,630.00.

16.   The amount that should have been paid in 2016 is $7,800.00. The receipts establish that $3,630.00 was paid. According to the receipts, this leaves a balance of $ 4,170.00 owing for 2016.

17.   However, the Tenant stated that other amounts were paid to the Landlord that he does not have receipts for. He relied on a letter from the Landlord dated January 8, 2017 to establish additional payments and the correct amount owing.

18.   That letter establishes that the Tenant had a balance of only $2,980.00 as of December 31, 2016 and not $4,170.00 (a difference of $1,190.00 less).

19.   The lower $2,980.00 amount is claimed by the Landlord up to December 31, 2016.

20.   Despite the fact that the rent receipts the Tenant presented establish a balance of $4,170.00, the lower amount of $2,980 claimed by the Landlord is what is being ordered for 2016.

21.   For 2017 the Tenant should have paid the Landlord $2,600.00 in rent for the four month period January 1, 2017 to April 30, 2017 ($650.00 per month x 4 months).

22.   The Tenant presented the following two receipts for 2017: $500.00 paid January 8, 2017; and $350.00 paid February 25, 2017. These receipts total $850.00.

23.   A total of $850.00 was paid to the Landlord in 2017. This is undisputed. There is no dispute that only two payments have been made in 2017. Therefore, the Tenant owes the Landlord $1,750.00 for rent arrears for the period from January 1, 2017 to April 30, 2017 ($2,600.00 - $850.00 paid).

24.   The total amount of rent arrears owing to the Landlord is $2,980.00 for 2016 and $1,750.00 for 2017. This totals $4,730.00. This is the amount that is claimed by the Landlord.

25.   The Landlord’s documents are all consistent regarding the amount owing.

26.   The Tenant had no other documents, receipts or bank records to present to establish any additional amounts that should be credited to him.

27.   The Tenant continues to dispute the $4,730.00 amount. He believes the amount owing to the Landlord is lower. Although he did not present an exact figure, his belief that the amount is lower is coming from some confusion regarding how (and to which months) his payments were applied. For example, the Tenant believes that he has not been given a credit for the $500.00 paid in September 2016 or the $400.00 paid in October 2016 since the Landlord is showing those months as still owing.

28.   Yet, this is because the Tenant rarely paid a full $650.00 amount in each month in 2016. His payments were sporadic and usually less than the full $650.00 monthly rent. As a result, the payments made in August, September and October 2016 were applied back to the amounts still owing from prior months in 2016.

29.   As the calculations set out above clearly show, the Tenant has been given a credit for every single payment that he presented a rent receipt for. He also received an additional credit of $1,190.00 for payments not reflected in the rent receipts since the Landlord claimed $1,190.00 less than the amount the rent receipts established which was consistent with his January 8, 2017 letter.

30.   The Tenant has not paid $4,730.00 of the total rent the Tenant was required to pay for the period from August 1, 2016 to April 30, 2017. Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a N4 Notice of Termination.

31.   The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $650.00 from the Tenant and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

32.   Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenant for the period from February 2, 2015 to March 31, 2017.

33.   I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would be unfair to grant relief from eviction pursuant to subsection 83(1) of the Act. No rent has been paid since February. There are over seven months of rent owing to the Landlord. The Tenant did not comply with the interim order. The interim order was meant to ensure that the amount of rent arrears ordered in March did not increase when April rent came due. Yet, that was not accomplished. No amount was paid to the Board. The Tenant said he is currently unable to pay the April rent.

It is ordered that:

 

1.      Order CEL-64556-17, issued on March 16, 2017, is cancelled and replaced by the following:

2.      Unless the Tenant voids the order as set out below, the tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant is terminated. The Tenant must move out of the rental unit on or before April 24, 2017.

3.      The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $3,683.50*, which represents the amount of rent owing and compensation up to April 13, 2017, less the rent deposit and interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.

4.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $21.37 per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting April 14, 2017 to the date the Tenant moves out of the unit.

5.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $190.00 for the cost of filing the application.

6.      If the unit is not vacated on or before April 24, 2017, then starting April 25, 2017, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

7.      Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord, on or after April 25, 2017.

8.      If, on or before April 24, 2017, the Tenant pays the amount of $4,920.00** to the Landlord or to the Board in trust, this order for eviction will be void.  This means that the tenancy would not be terminated and the Tenant could remain in the unit.  If this payment is not made in full and on time, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

9.      The Tenant may make a motion to the Board under subsection 74(11) of the Act to set aside this order if they pay the amount required under that subsection on or after April 25, 2017 but before the Sheriff gives vacant possession to the Landlord. The Tenant is only entitled to make this motion once during the period of the tenancy agreement with the Landlord.
