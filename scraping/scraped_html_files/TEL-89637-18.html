Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: TEL-89637-18

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 


Lc O.B. Ltd (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict A.P. (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes.(L1 application)

 

Lc O.B. Ltd (the 'Landlord') applied for an order requiring A.P. (the 'Tenant') to pay compensation for the damages caused by the Tenant or a person the Tenant permitted in the residential complex. (L2 application)

 

 

This application was heard in Toronto on May 15, 2018.

 

Only the Landlord’s representative, D.F., attended the hearing.

 

Determinations:

 

1.      The Tenant has not paid the total rent the Tenant was required to pay for the period from December 1, 2017 to May 31, 2018.  Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective January 31, 2018.

2.      The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $854.12 from the Tenant and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

3.      Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenant for the period from January 1, 2018 to January 31, 2018.

4.      The Tenant paid $1,683.00 after the application was filed.

5.      I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would be unfair to grant relief from eviction pursuant to subsection 83(1) of the Act.

L2 application

 

1.      The Tenant was still in possession of the rental unit as of the date the application was filed.

2.      The Tenant or a person permitted in the residential complex by the Tenant has wilfully or negligently caused undue damage to the rental unit by damaging the door, door frame and locking mechanism on the door of the rental unit.

3.      The Landlord has incurred costs of $367.25 to repair the damaged door. This is supported by the uncontested evidence of the Landlord along with the supporting invoices for repairs, and a letter signed by the tenant, dated February 26, 2018, acknowledging that she is responsible for the damage and repair. The Tenant will be ordered to pay this amount to the Landlord.

 

It is ordered that:

 

L1 application

 

1.      Unless the Tenant voids the order as set out below, the tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant is terminated.  The Tenant must move out of the rental unit on or before May 27, 2018.

2.      The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $1,326.16*, which represents the amount of rent owing and compensation up to May 16, 2018, less the rent deposit and interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.

3.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $28.08 per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting May 17, 2018 to the date the Tenant moves out of the unit.

4.      The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $190.00 for the cost of filing the application.

5.      If the Tenant does not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before May 27, 2018, the Tenant will start to owe interest.  This will be simple interest calculated from May 28, 2018 at 3.00% annually on the balance outstanding.

6.      If the unit is not vacated on or before May 27, 2018, then starting May 28, 2018, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

7.      Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord, on or after May 28, 2018.

8.      If, on or before May 27, 2018, the Tenant pays the amount of $1,156.67** to the Landlord or to the Board in trust, this order for eviction will be void.  This means that the tenancy would not be terminated and the Tenant could remain in the unit.  If this payment is not made in full and on time, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

9.      The Tenant may make a motion to the Board under subsection 74(11) of the Act to set aside this order if they pay the amount required under that subsection on or after May 28, 2018 but before the Sheriff gives vacant possession to the Landlord. The Tenant is only entitled to make this motion once during the period of the tenancy agreement with the Landlord.

L2 application

10.   The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $367.25, which represents the reasonable costs of repairing the damage.

11.   If the Tenant does not pay the Landlord the full amount owing on or before June 15, 2018, the Tenant will start to owe interest.  This will be simple interest calculated from May 28, 2018 at 3.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
