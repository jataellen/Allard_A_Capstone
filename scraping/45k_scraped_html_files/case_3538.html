Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: SWL-53465-21

 

 

In the matter of:

104, 80 CONFEDERATION DRIVE ST. THOMAS ON N5P3P4

 

Between:

Confederation Property Management Inc.

Landlord

 

 

and

 

 

Janis Bennell

Tenant

 

 

 

Confederation Property Management Inc. (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Janis Bennell (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or someone the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment or lawful right, privilege or interest of the Landlord or another tenant.

The Landlord also claimed compensation for each day the Tenant remained in the unit after the termination date.

 

This application was heard by way of video conference on October 25, 2021. The following individuals attended the hearing:

 

•         Brenda Martyn – Landlord Agent

•         Luisa Goncalves – Landlord’s legal representative

•         Janis Bennell – Tenant

•         Susana Pereira – Tenant’s legal representative

 

Determinations:

 

1.   The Landlord’s application is based on an N5 notice served to the Tenant on July 20, 2021 with a termination date of August 20, 2021. The N5 alleges that the Tenant has failed to maintain an ordinary state of cleanliness within the rental unit and that the rental unit consists of excessive clutter.

 

Landlord’s evidence:

 

2.   Brenda Martyn provided oral testimony at the hearing and was the Landlord’s sole witness. Ms. Martyn is employed by the Landlord as the building superintendent.

 

3.   The Landlord’s witness testified that on June 16, 2021 the Landlord entered the rental unit to inspect the condition of the rental unit. Upon entering, the Landlord observed that the Tenant had not maintained an ordinary state of cleanliness and that the rental unit had

 

excessive clutter. The Landlord entered into evidence pictures taken on the date of the inspection (LL exhibit #1).

 

4.   On June 17, 2021 the Landlord’s witness testified that the Tenant was served with a letter advising the Tenant of the Landlord’s findings during the June 16, 2021 inspection and advising the Tenant to correct the issues.

 

5.   On June 30, 2021 the Landlord’ witness testified that the Landlord conducted a second inspection of the rental unit and noticed no change to the June 16, 2021 inspection. The Landlord’s witness testified that the rental unit was still cluttered and not in an ordinary state of cleanliness. The Landlord entered into evidence photo images of the rental unit taken on the date of inspection. (LL exhibit #2).

 

6.   In response to the June 16th inspection, the Landlord served the Tenant with an N5 notice of termination on July 20, 2021.

 

7.   The Landlord’s witness testified that the Landlord conducted a third inspection of the rental unit on July 29, 2021. During this inspection, the Landlord acknowledged that the Tenant had made some progress in clearing the excess clutter and cleaning the rental unit but noted that the rental unit still contained excess clutter and cleanliness concerns. The Landlord entered into evidence photo images of the July 29, 2021 inspections (LL exhibit #3).

 

8.   The Landlord’s witness testified that as of the date of the hearing, the Tenant had still reduced the excess clutter in the rental unit. The Landlord advised the Tenant to contact their onsite staff if she required assistance, but the Tenant had not reached out to the Landlord.

 

Tenant’s evidence:

 

9.   The Tenant testified that she has resided in the rental unit for 36 years and has never been in a prior legal dispute with her Landlord prior to this proceeding. The Tenant testified that her rental unit has only recently been inspected and that she did not receive lawful notice of entry as required by the Act.

 

10. The Tenant does not dispute that the rental unit contains excessive clutter and stated that all of the excess belongings are packed in boxes and are going to be removed from the rental unit and sent to her parents’ home. The Tenant requests that the Board allow her additional time to remove the excess clutter.

 

11. The Tenant testified that the unit is an ordinary state of cleanliness and that there are no pest control issues within her unit.

 

Analysis:

 

12. Section 33 and 64(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the Act) state

 

33. The tenant is responsible for ordinary cleanliness of the rental unit, except to the extent that the tenancy agreement requires the landlord to clean it

 

64 (1) A landlord may give a tenant notice of termination of the tenancy if the conduct of the tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or a person permitted in the residential complex by the tenant is such that it substantially interferes with the reasonable enjoyment of the residential complex for all usual purposes by the landlord or another tenant or substantially interferes with another lawful right, privilege or interest of the landlord or another tenant

 

13. Based on the evidence before the Board, I am satisfied that the Tenant has substantially interfered with the Landlord’s reasonable enjoyment, lawful right and/or privileges. The evidence and testimony before the Board is clear that on June 16, June 30 and July 29, 2021 the rental unit contained excess clutter. The photo images entered into evidence further show that the rental unit is not in an ordinary state of cleanliness, the bathroom and kitchen area of the rental unit are not cleaned and contain excess amounts of garbage, clutter and unclean appliances. The excessive clutter and cleanliness issues could lead to a pest control infestation and/or fire hazard within the rental unit and residential complex.

 

14. The Tenant’s legal representative argued that the Landlord’s application should be dismissed because the Landlord’s inspections were based on an illegal entry. I do not accept the Tenant’s argument. The basis of the application is that the Tenant has not kept the rental unit in an ordinary state of cleanliness and has stored excess clutter. If the Tenant believes that the Landlord has illegally entered the rental unit, the Tenant may wish to explore the appropriate Tenant application to address this concern.

 

15. The Tenant’s representative also argued that the application should be dismissed because the Landlord’s N5 notice made reference to pest control issues but provided no proof of pest control infestation in their evidence. While I agree with the Tenant’s representative that the Landlord has not proved on a balance of probabilities that the Tenant’s conduct has contributed to pest control infestations, I do not find that this is grounds to dismiss the application. The Landlord has still established that the rental unit is cluttered and overall is not in an ordinary state of cleanliness as per the Tenant’s obligation under section 33 of the Act.

 

Relief from eviction:

 

16. The Landlord is not seeking termination of tenancy, but rather an order that the Tenant reduce the excess clutter and maintain an ordinary state of cleanliness going froward.

 

17. The Tenant at the hearing requested approximately 3 months to rectify the clutter and cleanliness issues. As of the date of this order, the Tenant has had over two months to rectify the issues. However, I find that it would not be prejudicial to allow the Tenant some additional time to fulfill their request.

 

18. I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would not be unfair to

 

grant relief from eviction subject to the condition(s) set out in this order pursuant to subsection 83(1)(a) and 204(1) of the Act

 

 

 

It is ordered that:

 

 

1.   On or before February 28, 2022 the Tenant shall bring the rental unit to an ordinary state of cleanliness, free of excessive clutter within the rental unit.

 

2.   If the Tenant complies with paragraph 1 of this order, then commencing March 1, 2022 and continuing until February 28, 2022 the Tenant shall maintain an ordinary state of cleanliness, free of excessive clutter within the rental unit.

 

3.   If the Tenant fails to comply with the terms in accordance with paragraph 1 or 2 of this order, the Landlord may, without notice to the Tenant and within 30 days of the breach, apply to the Board pursuant to section 78 of the Act for an order terminating the tenancy and evicting the Tenant

 

4.   The Tenant shall also pay the Landlords $186.00 for the application filing fee on or before February 28, 2022.

 

5.   If the Tenants do not pay the Landlord the full amount in accordance with paragraph 4, the Tenants will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from March 1, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
