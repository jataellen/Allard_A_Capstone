Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: TEL-03717-19

 

 

 

 

PPIL (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict HNM and MJ (the 'Tenants') because the Tenants did not pay the rent that the Tenants owe.

 

This application was heard by videoconference on October 19, 2021 at 10:45am.

 

Only the Landlord attended the hearing. The Landlord was represented by DC. Also in attendance was the Landlord’s witness, DL.

Determinations:

 

PROCEDURAL HISTORY

 

1.     This application first came before the Board on September 18, 2019 where it was adjourned as additional time was required to hear the matter. Both the Landlord and the Tenant were in attendance.

 

2.     The next time the application came before the Board was on January 15, 2020 where the matter was adjourned at the Landlord’s request as they had changed their legal representation firm who were unavailable on the day of the hearing. This adjournment was peremptory on the Landlord. At this hearing, the Landlord’s former representative and the Tenants were present.

 

3.     At the hearing on March 12, 2021, only the Landlord’s representative, BR, was present; I adjourned the matter on this day as it was apparent that the Landlord’s representative had not reviewed the hearing recording and out of an abundance of caution due to the possibility that the Tenants were not served with the notice of hearing since they moved out of the rental unit on November 30, 2020.

 

4.     At the hearing on October 19, 2021, once again, only the Landlord’s representative was present. I heard evidence of his personal efforts to reach the Tenants such as calling the last known phone number for the Tenant which was disconnected, no email address provided by the Tenant, sending the disclosure to the rental unit with the hope that it would be forward to the Tenant (the Landlord rep advises this was not returned back to

the Landlord’s office) and looking up the Tenants’ information on 411 but not finding anything there. I am satisfied the Landlord has complied with the interim order issued on March 17, 2021 to make best efforts in connecting with the Tenants.

 

5.     I proceeded to hear the Landlord’s claim for rent arrears and the Landlord’s response to the issues raised by the Tenants at the hearing on September 18, 2019.

 

L1 APPLICATION

 

6.     The Tenants have not paid the total rent the Tenants were required to pay for the period from October 1, 2018 to October 31, 2021. Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective July 26, 2019.

 

7.     The Tenants were in possession of the rental unit.

 

8.     The Landlord’s uncontested evidence was the Tenants vacated the rental unit on November 30, 2020. As such, the request for eviction under this application was no longer necessary.

 

9.     According to the Landlord’s ledger, the Tenants paid $17,773.00 after the application was filed.

 

10.  The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $1,280.00 from the Tenants and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

 

11.  Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenants for the period from January 1, 2019 to July 26, 2019.

 

12.  The Landlord seeks an order for the arrears and costs owing. TENANT’S EVIDENCE

13.  At the hearing on September 18, 2019, the Tenants dispute the following:

 

a)     The second-named Tenant, MJ, was a co-signor and not a Tenant of the rental unit;

 

b)     The Tenants did not receive a notice of rent increase for April 2020;

 

c)      The lawful monthly rent is incorrect as the Tenants cancelled a locker effective July 1, 2019; and

 

d)     The amount claimed on the notice of termination is incorrect.

 

Is MJ a Tenant?

 

14.  At the hearing on September 18, 2019, the second-named Tenant, MJ, testified that he was not a Tenant of the rental unit, rather, a co-signor. MJ presented a letter dated March 26, 2018 listing him as a co-tenant in support of his assertion. He testified that he resided in the rental unit as an occupant.

 

15.  The Landlord maintained the position that MJ was listed as a Tenant on the lease; there were no guarantors in the file with respect to this tenancy; any correspondence including notices of rent increase were addressed to both Tenants and the occupants section did not list MJ as a named individual.

 

16.  I permitted the Landlord to submit a copy of the lease agreement by September 20, 2019 as a post-hearing submission. The lease agreement shows the second-named Tenant listed as a Tenant on the lease with co-tenant beside his name; the lease was also signed by both Tenants. Under the occupants section of the lease, MJ’s name was not listed.

 

17.  Based on the evidence before the Board, I find that MJ is a Tenant of the rental unit and both the N4 notice of termination and the L1 application name him properly.

 

Notice of Rent Increase for April 2020

 

18.  The Tenants testified that they did not receive the notice of rent increase that was to take effect April 2020. As such, the rent claimed by the Landlord is incorrect as it is based on an increased amount. The Tenants maintained that the rent was $1,302.50 before they cancelled the locker effective July 1, 2019.

 

19.  At the hearing on October 19, 2021, the Landlord’s witness, DL, who is the property administrator testified that the server of the notice of rent increase that was to take effect April 2020 was not available; further, the superintendent of the property was no longer employed by the Landlord.

 

20.  DL testified that the Tenants rent increased every April and that the general process with respect to these notices was either the superintendent or the property administrator hand delivered them or put them through the mail slot of each unit. Furthermore, the building had 52 units and no other tenants complained about not receiving the notice of rent increase during that period.

 

21.  Aside from the notice of rent increase dated December 2, 2019 to take effect April 1, 2020, the Landlord did not produce any evidence confirming service of this notice to the Tenants. Given the above, based on the evidence before the Board, I am not satisfied that the notice of rent increase for April 2020 was served to the Tenants.

 

22.  While other tenants may not have complained about not receiving the notice of rent increase, I find that to be irrelevant as this is a separate tenancy. Absent anything more, I find the lawful monthly rent continued to be $1,302.50 effective April 2020 onwards.

 

Locker Cancellation effective July 2019

 

23.  The Tenants testified that they cancelled one of their lockers in July 2019 after they moved back into the rental unit. The Tenants confirmed that while they did not provide a written notice to the Landlord, they spoke to the superintendent R, advising her of such.

 

24.  The Landlord submits that the Tenants rented two lockers in February 2019, after the fire in their unit; since then, the Landlord has not received any notice of cancellation for this tenancy and as previously mentioned, R is no longer at the building and could not be present to testify. Furthermore, the building policy has always been to put such requests in writing and document these requests in the requestor’s file.

 

25.  Based on the evidence before the Board, I find the evidence is insufficient to establish that the Tenants cancelled a locker effective July 1, 2019. I say this as there was no documentation before me nor did the Tenants produce the superintendent, R, to confirm their assertion. Thus, I find the rent remained $1,302.50 effective July 2019 onwards.

 

Amount on the N4 Notice of Termination

 

26.  The Landlord’s L1 application is based on a N4 notice of termination served the Tenants on July 12, 2019 with a termination date of July 26, 2019. This N4 claims rent for the period October 2018 and July 2019.

 

27.  The Tenants testified that between October 6, 2018 and May 31, 2019, the Tenants did not reside in the rental unit because there was a fire in their unit and the Tenants had to move out for the Landlord to conduct repairs. During this period, the insurance company paid the rent as reflected on the Landlord’s ledger.

 

28.  The Tenants testified that they received keys to the rental unit on June 1, 2019 after they complained to the Rental Housing Enforcement Unit; however, because they had to provide notice to their landlord, they could not move into the unit until July 1, 2019. Thus, they do not believe they should be responsible for the rent for June 2019. This is because the Landlord had previously told them the unit was ready for move in effective April 2019 and they had provided their current landlord with a notice to move out, only to find out that they could not move in, due to insurance issues in April 2019.

 

29.  The Landlord’s evidence was the rent for the period October 6, 2018 to March 2019 was paid by the insurance company. The Landlord waived the rent for the months of April 2019 and May 2019 as a unit inspection was required to obtain an occupancy permit from the Building Code department before it was turned over to the Tenants. The Tenants received keys to the rental unit on June 1, 2019 and were entitled to move into it as of that date; they had possession of the rental unit as of that date and should be held responsible for the rent for June 2019.

 

30.  Based on the evidence before the Board, I find the Tenants are responsible for the rent for June 2019. I say this based on the Tenants’ own evidence that they had keys to the

 

unit effective that date and were able to have possession as of that date but chose not to do so.

 

31.  Subsection 13(2) of the Act states:

 

(2) A tenancy agreement takes effect when the tenant is entitled to occupy the rental unit, whether or not the tenant actually occupies it.

 

32.  As such, I find that between the period of October 2018 to July 2019, the Tenants owed the Landlord $73.11 for the two lockers that were in use for the months of March 2019 – May 2019 - that was not paid by insurance or waived by the Landlord, less the interest credit on their account for the year 2018 ($16.89) plus the rent for June 2019 ($1,302.50) and the balance of the rent that was not paid for July 2019 ($52.50) and the rent for the period October 1 – 6, 2018 ($246.60 – which is not a deductible as claimed by the Tenants). In other words, I find the amounts claimed on the N4 notice of termination to be correct.

 

33.  Furthermore, contrary to the Tenants’ assertion, the amount owing for the period October 1-6, 2018 was not paid by insurance and remains outstanding.

 

34.  For ease of reference for all parties I find the rent for the following periods to be as follows:

 

a)     Between October 2018 – January 2019, the rent was $1,250.00;

 

b)     Between February 2019 – March 2019, the rent was $1,280.00 (addition of 2 lockers x $15.00); and

 

c)      Between April 2019 – November 2020 the rent was $1,302.50.

 

35.  Thus, I find the arrears and costs owing to November 30, 2020 less the last month's rent deposit totals $3,448.65.

 

36.  The Landlord incurred costs of filing the application and is entitled to its reimbursement in the amount of $175.00.

 

37.  As the Tenants have moved out of the rental unit and were not present at the hearing to provide submissions with respect to repayment of the arrears outstanding, a standard order shall issue.

 

38.  This order contains all of the reasons for the decision within it. No further reasons shall be issued.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.     The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenants is terminated as of November 30, 2020.

 

2.     The Tenants shall pay to the Landlord $3,448.65*, which represents the amount of rent owing and compensation up to November 30, 2020, less the rent deposit and interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.

 

3.     The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlord $175.00 for the cost of filing the application.

 

4.     The total amount owing under this order is $3,623.65.

 

5.     If the Tenants do not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before January 18, 2022, the Tenants will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from January 19, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.


 

January 7, 2022
