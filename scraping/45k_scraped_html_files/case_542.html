Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: TEL-10036-20

 

 

In the matter of:

BASEMENT, 540 OLD HARWOOD AVENUE AJAX ON L1T3L1

 

Between:

Marcus Daly Debra Daly

Landlords

 

and

 

 

April Gonzales

Tenant

 

 

 

Marcus Daly and Debra Daly (the 'Landlords') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict April Gonzales (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or someone the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has wilfully or negligently caused undue damage to the premises. The Landlords has also applied for an order requiring the Tenant to compensate the Landlords for the damage. The Landlords also claimed compensation for each day the Tenant remained in the unit after the termination date.

 

This application was heard by videoconference on October 12, 2021.

 

The Landlords, the Landlord's Legal Representative, Daniel Greanya, and the Tenant attended the hearing.

 

The Tenant spoke with Duty Counsel prior to the hearing.

 

Determinations:

 

1.     On February 6, 2020 the Landlords served a notice of termination because the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or someone the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has caused damage to the premises. The termination date on the notice was February 28, 2020.

 

2.     The Tenant was in possession of the rental unit when the application was filed. The Tenant moved into the rental unit mid-July 2017.

 

3.     It is undisputed that the Landlord is holding a $1,000.00 damage deposit. The Landlords agreed that this money should be deducted off any amounts the Tenant is found liable for.

 

4.     The Landlord is not holding a last month rent deposit as it was applied to the last month’s rent. The Tenant vacated August 13, 2020.

 

 

 

5.     I find the Tenant did not willfully or negligently cause undue damage to the rental residential unit. I say this for the reasons that follow.

 

Landlords’ testimony

 

6.     Debra Daly testified to the following on behalf of both Landlords.

 

7.     The Tenant was operating a t-shirt printing press in the unit. Approximately October 2018 the Landlords noticed that the fumes from the printing press had damaged the ceiling because the Tenant used the fans in the kitchen and bathroom to get rid of the fumes. The Landlord entered into evidence a picture of the printing press and a smaller press which appeared to be located between the kitchen and bathroom, in a hallway area. On October 18, 2018 the Landlords texted the Tenant about coming over to clean the ceiling, and on October 1, 2019 they texted their concern over the printing press and having to paint the ceiling; the texts were entered into evidence. The Landlords had to wipe black residue off the bathroom ceiling and then paint. This cost a total of $600.00, approximately $200.00 was for the paint; there was no receipt.

 

8.     Approximately October 25, 2018, the Tenant damaged the toilet lid; she was supposed to replace it but could not find a lid for the toilet. On October 31, 2019, the Landlords texted that they found a toilet bowl cover; the text was entered into evidence. The Landlords also testified that the toilet ended up being replaced. The receipt from Home Depot dated September 19, 2019 in the amount of $197.75 for the installation of the new toilet was entered into evidence as well as another Home Depot receipt also dated September 12, 2019 installation of kitchen/bathroom sink and faucet in the amount of $299.45.

 

9.     On February 13, 2019 the Tenant texted that she noticed mice in the unit so had bought a trap. They caught a mouse and one of her children went to flush the dead mouse down the toilet and accidentally dropped the trap also; now the toilet will not flush properly. The text was entered into evidence. It cost the Landlords $282.50 to fix the toilet. No receipt was provided.

 

10.  In September 2019 the Landlords noticed damage to the granite countertop. It was only about 2 ½ years old and had chips out of it. The estimate to replace the countertop, from Richy Granite in the amount of $1,850.00 plus H.S.T., was entered into evidence. The estimate indicates that plumbing costs are not included; the Landlords estimated this cost at $299.45.

 

11.  Approximately March 2019, the Landlords notice that the Tenant has damaged floor tiles in the kitchen and one of the bedrooms. The rental unit is in the basement; therefore, the ceramic tiles are on cement; it looked like something heavy was dropped because wear and tear would not break the tile. Two pictures were entered into evidence showing a small chip in two tiles and a larger chip in another tile. The cost to repair the tiles was

$452.00; the work was done by Collin Exiter, a tiler who has done work for them before. No receipt was provided.

 

Tenant’s testimony

 

12.  The Tenant testified to the following.

 

13.  The black on the ceiling had nothing to do with her printing press; no ink is used as it is an iron on press. The second ‘press’ pointed out by the Landlords is a cutter; it cuts the vinyl that is iron pressed onto the t-shirt. The black on the bathroom ceiling is actually mould; when she brought it to the Landlords attention, they blamed her and the printing press for it. She knows it is mould because the bathroom is attached to the master bedroom and the mould is in there also and has damaged clothing; pictures of the mould on the bathroom ceiling and damaged clothing were entered into evidence.

 

14.  When the Tenant reported the mice to the Landlords, they ignored her, therefore she bought a trap. When attempting to flush the dead mouse down the toilet, the trap was accidentally dropped and got flushed down; she texted the Landlords about what happened because the toilet did not flush right afterwards.

 

15.  With respect to the toilet cover, the Landlords said they had an extra cover and put this on, therefore there was no need to replace the toilet.

 

16.  In regard to the countertop, there was no damage done to it, there is one nick that would be considered wear and tear, not damage. The Tenant entered into evidence pictures of the countertop; one of the pictures showed a tiny nick on the edge of the counter. Picture number 4 was taken September 2019, the others were taken in January 2020; the counter does not show any signs of damage.

 

17.  As for the tiles, the two tiles with the very small chips are in the kitchen; she does not know when or how they got chipped. The larger chipped tile is in the kids’ bedroom; when moving the bunkbed, the tile chipped. The tile must have already had a crack or been damaged because moving the bed should not have chipped it. Plus, there was nothing deliberate of intentional about the chip happening.

 

Analysis

 

18.  As the Tenant has vacated the rental unit this application is now based on Section 89(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the ‘Act’).

 

19.  Section 89(1) of the Act states that a Landlord may apply to the Board for an order requiring a Tenant to pay reasonable costs that the Landlord has incurred or will incur for the repair of or, where repairing is not reasonable, the replacement of damaged property, if the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or a person whom the Tenant permits in the residential complex willfully or negligently causes undue damage to the rental unit or the residential complex and the Tenant is in possession of the rental unit.

 

20.  The Landlords testified that the Tenant damaged the toilet, countertop, ceramic tiles, and caused damage to the bathroom ceiling in the form of a black residue, because of her printing press. However, there is insufficient evidence to support these allegations.

 

21.  The Landlords did present any pictures of the alleged damage to the countertop. The pictures entered into evidence by the Tenant show a near pristine countertop; one picture shows a tiny nick on the edge which the Tenant testified must have been from wear and tear. Therefore, based on the evidence and testimony before me, I find on a balance of probabilities that the countertop was not damaged, and the one nick would be from wear and tear. Therefore, this part of the application must be dismissed.

 

22.  With respect to the toilet, there is insufficient evidence to find the Tenant willfully or negligently caused damage to it. The mousetrap was accidentally dropped into the toilet, and the Landlords testified that it had to be fixed twice in one week. I do not find this issue to fall under either willful or negligent conduct, it was merely an accident. Therefore, the Tenant is not responsible for any alleged costs incurred by the Landlords; costs which did not have any supporting documentation. Further, having to repair the toilet does not explain why the Landlords replaced it. In regard to the toilet lid, the October 31, 2019 text from Landlords say they had one to replace it, therefore there was no cost incurred. Nor was there any testimony given by either party as to how the lid got damaged, therefore there is no proof of willful or negligent conduct and the problem was addressed. Further, one of the receipts from Home Depot was for the installation of “kitchen/bath sink and faucet”; this has nothing to do with the toilet, nor were these issues part of the application. Therefore, for all these reasons this part of the application must also be dismissed.

 

23.  In regard to the ceramic tiles I find based on the evidence and testimony before me that the chips were caused from regular wear and tear, not willful or negligent conduct on the part of the Tenant. The Tenant’s testimony that the chips in the two kitchen tiles were very small is supported by the Landlords’ pictures. Plus, the Tenant’s undisputed testimony was that she did not know when or how the chips happened. As for the larger chip in the bedroom tile, I accept the Tenant’s undisputed testimony that this happened when she moved the bunkbed and that moving a bed does not equate to willful or negligent conduct. I also accept that it is more likely that the tile had either a defect or problem that caused it to chip as it did when the bed was moved. For all these reasons, this part of the application must be dismissed.

 

24.  Based on the Tenant’s undisputed testimony as to the type of printing press and how it is used, specifically that there is no ink involved, I find the Landlords did not prove any willful or negligent conduct on the part of the Tenant that caused the black on the bathroom ceiling. The Tenant’s pictures appear to be more akin to a mould issue and totally separate from Tenant’s t-shirt printing press and vinyl cutter. Also, the Tenant’s testimony and pictures support it being more likely that mould that extended from the bathroom into the master bedroom and grew on her clothing. Therefore, I find on the testimony and evidence before me, on a balance of probabilities, the black on the bathroom ceiling was not caused by the Tenant’s t-shirt press. Accordingly, this part of the application must also be dismissed.

 

25.  As this was the Landlords’ application, this order does not address the $1,000.00 damage deposit being held by the Landlords.

 

26.  This order contains all the reasons for the decision within it. No further reasons shall be issued.

 

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.     The Landlords application is dismissed.

 

 

 

 

Diane Wade
