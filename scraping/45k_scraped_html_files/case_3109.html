Order under Section 78(6)

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

 

File Number: SWL-57396-21

 

In the matter of:

 

707, 737 OUELLETTE AVENUE WINDSOR ON N9A6T2

 

Between:

Skyline Living

Landlord

 

 

and

 

 

Carl Jevsnik

Tenant

 

 

 

On December 9, 2021, Skyline Living (the 'Landlord') applied in this L4 application for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Carl Jevsnik (the 'Tenant') and for an order to have the Tenant pay the rent the Tenant owes because the Tenant failed to meet a condition specified in the consent order issued by the Board on August 19, 2021 with respect to L1 application SWL- 50976-21.

 

Determinations:

 

1.        The previous consent order provided that the Landlord could apply to the Board under section 78 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act') without notice to the Tenant to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenant if the Tenant did not meet certain condition(s) specified in the consent order.

 

2.        I find that the Tenant has not met the following conditions specified in the order: The Tenant has not paid $977.96 rent on or before December 1, 2021. This was a breach of paragraph 2 of the consent order. This L4 application was filed within 30 days of the breach.

 

3.        The previous L1 application included a request for an order for the payment of arrears of rent. The resulting order required the Tenant to pay rent or some or all of the arrears of rent. Accordingly, in addition to eviction, in this L4 application the Landlord is entitled to request an order for the payment of arrears of rent and compensation.

 

4.        In paragraph 1 of the consent order, the Tenant agreed to pay $4,075.80 for rent arrears and the costs related to the Landlord's L1 application fee in Order SWL-50976-21. The Tenant only paid $1,031.12 so the amount that is still owing from that order is $3,044.68, which is included in this order. As a result, the previous consent order SWL-50976-21 is cancelled.

 

5.        Since the date of the order, the Tenant has failed to pay the full rent that became owing.

 

6.        On January 23, 2018 the Landlord collected a rent deposit of $977.96 from the Tenant and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

 

7.        Interest on the rent deposit was last paid out to the Tenant up January 31, 2021. Interest is owing to the Tenant for the period from February 1, 2021 to January 11, 2022.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.        The previous consent order SWL-50976-21 is cancelled.

 

2.        The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant is terminated. The Tenant must move out of the rental unit on or before January 22, 2022 (standard 11 days from the issuance date of this order).

 

3.        If the Tenant made any payments to the Landlord after this L4 application was filed, those amounts shall be deducted from the calculations below.

 

4.        The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $3,044.33*. This amount represents the rent owing up to December 31, 2021 and the costs related to the application fee for the previous L1 application, less the rent deposit and interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.

 

5.        The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $32.15 per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting January 1, 2022 to the date the Tenant actually moves out of the rental unit or gets evicted.

 

6.        If the Tenant does not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before January 22, 2022, the Tenant will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from January 23, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.

 

7.        If the unit is not vacated on or before January 22, 2022, then starting January 23, 2022, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

 

8.        Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord on or after January 23, 2022.
