Order under Section 30

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: CET-93576-20

 

 

In the matter of:

1382 MUSKOKA RD 169 GRAVENHURST ON P1P1R2

 

Between:

Breyan Sinnott

Tenant

 

 

and

 

 

Carolyn Cruise David Cruise

Landlords

 

 

 

Breyan Sinnott (the 'Tenant') applied for an order determining that Carolyn Cruise and David Cruise (the 'Landlords') failed to meet the Landlords' maintenance obligations under the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act') or failed to comply with health, safety, housing or maintenance standards.

 

 

This application was heard at 1:03pm in videoconference room 117 on October 28, 2021. The Tenant and David Cruise and Carolyn Cruise (the 'Landlords') attended the hearing.

 

Determinations:

1.     The rental unit is a 4-bedroom, 3 bath, single detached home with a crawlspace basement on a rural property. The tenant moved into the unit on May 1, 2019 and moved out on November 30, 2019.

 

2.     The tenant filed an application about maintenance issues (T6) on March 2, 2020.

 

3.     The application alleges the following:

 

•         Water / flooding in spare bedroom-ensuite

•         Well / water quality

•         Mold

•         Electrical / hydro

•         Fire risk

 

Water / flooding and mold

 

4.     The Tenant said that in May 2019 the shower in the spare bedroom ensuite would leak when being used. The Tenant stated that it looked to her that this shower had previous leaking problems. When the tenant used the shower, it would drip water into the living room and other areas of the home. The tenant stated that the water would enter into light fixtures and lights would flicker. The drop ceiling and plastic light fixtures would fill with water. The tenant notified the Landlord at the beginning of June 2019. A contractor came by after 2 weeks. According to the Tenant, the leak repair was done approximately one month after.

 

5.     The Landlord testified that the tenant’s unit had 3 bathrooms and she was told not to use that specific bathroom. The landlord stated the Tenant advised them around May 28, 2019 and they sent over a repairman to seal the drain and sent over a repair on June 3, 2019. I accept that the tenant notified the Landlord on or around March 28, 2019.

 

Mold

 

6.     At the beginning of the tenancy the tenant noticed mold in the house. There was dark green and black mold growth and it was evident along the walls and ceiling joints and behind the wallpaper. According to the Tenant, the mold was visible in the stairwell to second level and master bedroom ensuite and in the floorboards. The tenant provided photo evidence to support her claim. The tenant stated that she notified the Landlord via text message on June 1, 2019 and sent pictures on numerous occasions notified the Landlord. She said she persistently emailed and sent requests to the Landlord to investigate. After a month of constant messages, the landlord sent over a family friend to do a visual inspection and offered to have the areas painted over. The tenant testified that the mold was still ongoing and continued to grown and new spores were appearing everywhere. No painting or repairs were never done.

 

7.     The Landlord advised that they sent out a family friend who was a contractor and that they washed it down with Javex.

 

 

 

Electrical / hydro

 

8.     The tenant stated that due to the leaks, the electrical was unstable and dangerous. There were fluorescent bulbs in the kitchen, directly under the flooding area. Water remained in the ceiling light. The lights would flicker or didn’t work. The electricity would short out or there would even been power surges.

 

9.     The dryer had some issues. It began to smoke one day and the room filled with smoke. The tenant explained that it was the wire connection and that the home had exposed wires throughout the house.

 

10.  There were open junction boxes in the unit. The exterior light of the house stopped working and the tenant notified the Landlord a minimum 6 times. The tenant felt this was unsafe as she worked night shift and if was impossible to see at night. The tenant stated the landlord told her they would send an electrician to come over but never did. The

 

tenant explained that the landlord had canceled appointments and advised the tenant that he would look at it when he would be in the area. The tenant would need to leave the house and unlock for the Landlord to gain access. The tenant was not prepared to do this for obvious safety reason. She said the Landlord refused to do the work unless she agreed.

 

The Landlord responded by saying that the hydro always flickered. Since the home is in rural area, the hydro is unsteady. The landlord acknowledged that the lights to the exterior were out but stated not for very long. The Landlord stated that the Tenant could have left the living room light for lighting. The landlord completed the repairs two days after the tenant moved out. The landlords further stated that the house was sold in January 2021 and there were no issues reported regarding the electrical. As for the dryer, the Landlord stated that when she was notified about the dryer, the Tenant was given a replacement dryer within a few days.

 

Water quality / Well

 

11.  The tenant claimed that the there was staining in the sink from the water. The water became darker in color in toilets and dishwasher. The tenant stated her clothes started turning red and her hair began to change to an orange color. The tenant contacted the Landlord but nothing was done. The tenant stated the water pressure dropped significantly and eventually turned to sporadic spurts.

 

12.  The tenant stated that the well went dry and she contacted the landlord immediately. The Landlord advised the tenant that she would have someone out to look at it but it would take a couple weeks. The tenant did not have water for her use and was forced to shower at work. The tenant stated that she called a contractor on her own and the technician advised the tenant that the well needed repair and needed filled. The tenant sent the report to the Landlord along with her invoice. The tenant stated that the landlord did not do the work nor was the well filled. The tenant was told to wait for the rain.

 

13.  The landlords disputed that the same day the tenant called on August 20th, 2019, they called a company. The company was not available. The tenant made a suggestion to call another contracted. The Landlord stated that the contractor filled and lowered the pump and paid the bill.

 

Fire risk

 

14.  The tenant stated that the Landlord did not follow their legal obligation to have installed smoke alarms and Co2 detectors in the unit. The tenant told the landlord about the fire code. The tenant purchased and installed the alarms and monitors herself for her own safety. The tenant also stated that the heat shields were removed from the stove.

 

Remedies

 

15.  Under s. 30 of the Act, if the Board determines in an application under paragraph 1 of subsection 29(1) that a Landlord has breached an obligation under subsection 20(1) or

 

section 161, the Board may do one or more remedies the Tenant is claiming. In this case the tenant is seeking the following:

 

a)     The cost of replacing and repairing her property that was damaged or destroyed ($1788.00) specifically, her clothing, staining and mold, disposed at the dump, replacement cost= ($330), toiletries and personal items mold, and disposed at the dump = ($420), towels, staining and mold, disposed of at the dump ($60), sporting equipment, damaged by mold and disposed at the dump=($280), hairdressing ($650 = 5 visits x $130), dry cleaning unforms ($48)

b)     Out of pocket expenses totally $420. Cost for water x 12 weeks 1 large bottle and 3 cases/week

c)      An order that the landlord do repairs, replace or other work necessary, full inspection of home required to prove the home is safe.

 

 

16.  In this case, it was quite clear that the rental unit lacked some repairs and had maintenance issues. Although the Landlord dealt with some items in a reasonable manner certain items were not dealt with in a timely manner or even at all. With regards to the mold and the water supply, I find that the Landlord did breach their obligations under the Act. The Tenant notified the Landlord on several occasions about the mold and the issue was never resolved and ongoing until the day the tenant moved out. The water supply and discoloration should have been taken more seriously by the Landlord and resolved earlier on. The Tenant suffered substantial loss of her ability to use the unit to its full capacity, had damaged personal items and was not able to shower or clean in her unit. I have accepted the tenant’s testimony to be credible. The tenant did not supply any evidence to support the loss of damaged items, no receipts. The Landlords did not raise any objections at the hearing to this regard. I feel the amount the Tenant claimed in the amount of $1778.00 to be reasonable.

 

17.  The tenant is no longer a tenant as she moved out of the unit on November 30, 2019. At the hearing the Landlord stated that the property was sold January 2021. Accordingly, I have no jurisdiction to order repairs or other work necessary that the home required nor a full inspection as requested by the tenant

 

18.  I will award the out of pocket expenses the tenant incurred for the water she had to purchase in the amount of $420.00

 

 

 

It is ordered that:

1.     The Landlords shall pay to the Tenants $1778.00 for the replacement cost of her damaged clothes.

 

2.     The Landlords shall pay to the Tenants $420 for the purchase of water.

 

3.     The Landlords shall pay to the Tenants $50 for the cost of filing the application.

 

4.     The Landlord shall pay the Tenants the full amount owing by February 13th, 2022.

 

5.     If the Landlord does not pay the Tenant the full amount owing on or before February 13th, 2022, the Tenant will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from February 14th, 2022 at 1.22% annually on the balance outstanding.
