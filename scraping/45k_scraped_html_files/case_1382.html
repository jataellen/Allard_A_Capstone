Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: SWL-55236-21

 

 

In the matter of:

708, 395 UNIVERSITY AVENUE E WINDSOR ON N9A2Z2

 

Between:

Windsor Essex Community Housing Corporation

Landlord

 

 

and

 

 

Kaitlyn Girard

Tenant

 

 

 

Windsor Essex Community Housing Corporation (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Kaitlyn Girard (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or a person the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has seriously impaired the safety of any person.

 

This application was heard via videoconference on January 17, 2022. Only the Landlord’s agent,

T. Mokhtari (TM), and legal representative, C. Parrott, attended the hearing. As of 1:12 p.m., the Tenant was not present or represented although properly served with Notice of this hearing by the Board.

 

Determinations:

 

1.     The Landlord applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenant because the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or a person the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has seriously impaired the safety of any person. The Landlord served the Tenant with an N7 Notice of Termination on September 24, 2021.

 

2.     The N7 Notice alleges that on September 8, 2021, the Tenant had disconnected his smoke detector. TM testified to this event and submitted into evidence was a photograph showing the smoke detector missing.

 

3.     Pursuant to section 66 of the Act, a landlord may give a tenant a notice of termination if an act or omission of the tenant seriously impairs the safety of another person, provided that the act or omission complained of occurs in the residential complex.

 

4.     In order to be successful on this ground, the Landlord must establish that the effect of the Tenant’s actions threatens the wellbeing or physical integrity of another person to such a degree that termination of the tenancy is reasonable in order to ensure the safety of others.

 

5.     In my view, the Tenant having removed the fire alarm poses a serious risk to the health and safety of others in the residential complex. TM testified that removal of the smoke alarm poses a safety hazard as there are over one hundred residents in the residential complex. In having the smoke alarmed removed, other tenants would not be expediently alerted to a fire.

 

6.     I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would be unfair to grant relief from eviction pursuant to subsection 83(1) of the Act.

 

7.     TM testified that after the Notice was served, the Tenant removed the smoke detector again on December 9, 2021, and during an inspection, it was found that there was significant damage to the rental unit. The Tenant also sweeps garbage into the hallway and stairwells, obstructs walkways with a shopping cart and allows trespassed people and animals into the residential complex. Further, a person claimed they were held captive in the Tenant’s unit and had to scale the building to escape. These are not positive considerations. The Tenant did not attend the hearing to testify to his circumstances for me to consider refusing or delaying eviction. As such, relief will not be granted.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.     The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant is terminated, as of February 14, 2022. The Tenant must move out of the rental unit on or before February 14, 2022.

 

2.     The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $186.00 for the cost of filing the application.

 

3.     If the Tenant does not pay the Landlord the full amount owing on or before February 14, 2022, the Tenant will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from February 15, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.

 

4.     If the unit is not vacated on or before February 14, 2022, then starting February 15, 2022, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

 

5.     Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord on or after February 15, 2022. The Sheriff is requested to expedite the enforcement of this order.
