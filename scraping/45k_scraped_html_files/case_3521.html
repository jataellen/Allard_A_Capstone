Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: EAL-97407-21

 

 

In the matter of:

2064 DUNDAS STREET CARDINAL ON K0E1E0

 

Between:

Wendy Gates

Landlord

 

 

and

 

 

Garrett Spicer Mandelle Young

Tenants

 

 

 

Wendy Gates (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Garrett Spicer and Mandelle Young (the 'Tenants') because the Tenants did not pay the rent that the Tenants owe.

 

The Landlord also applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenants because they, another occupant of the rental unit or someone they permitted in the residential complex have wilfully or negligently caused undue damage to the premises. The Landlord has also applied for an order requiring the Tenants to compensate the Landlord for the damage; and because the Landlord requires possession of the rental unit for the purpose of residential occupation. The Landlord also claimed compensation for each day the Tenants remained in the unit after the termination date.

 

This application was heard via videoconference on December 20, 2021. Only the Landlord and the Landlord’s legal representative, D. Fernadez-Stoll attended the hearing.

 

Preliminary Issue:

 

1.   As of 9:21 a.m., the Tenants were not present or represented although properly served with notice of this hearing by the Board. As such, the matter proceeded as uncontested. After the hearing block concluded, at 12:38 p.m., the Tenant, M. Young, called into the hearing room. She stated that she had broke her phone and had been trying to call into the hearing room with two phones that she borrowed since 8:30 am. and was only now successful.

 

2.   The Notice of Hearing provides both a video conference line and a telephone line to call into the hearing to participate. The Notice of Hearing also states that:

 

If you cannot participate in the hearing, you should give someone written permission to represent you and to participate on your behalf and email it to the Board in advance.

 

3.   The Tenants had an obligation to attend the hearing, or find another to participate on their behalf, but they did not. The Landlord’s application proceeded as uncontested.

Determinations:

 

L1 Application

 

4.   The Tenants have not paid the total rent the Tenants were required to pay for the period from July 1, 2021 to December 31, 2021. Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective July 27, 2021.

 

5.   The Tenants are in possession of the rental unit.

 

6.   The lawful monthly rent is $780.00.

 

7.   The Landlord is not holding a last month's rent deposit.

 

8.   The Tenants paid $780.00 after the application was filed.

 

9.   Pursuant to section 83(6), I am required to consider whether the Landlord made attempts to negotiate a payment agreement with the Tenants. The Landlord submitted that conversations were had with the Tenants on October 28, 2021. The Tenants replied with their intent to enter a payment agreement, but nothing formalized. I am satisfied that the Landlord attempted to negotiate a payment agreement with the Tenants.

 

L2 Application

 

N12 Notice

 

10. The Landlord applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenants because the Landlord requires possession of the rental unit for the purpose of residential occupation. The termination date in the N12 is September 30, 2021. In the notice, the Landlord indicated that she was seeking to terminate the tenancy for her own use.

 

11. The Landlord provided the Tenants compensation equivalent to one month’s rent on June 25, 2021 and June 28, 2021.

 

12. The Landlord filed her Declaration dated October 29, 2021. It states that she in good faith personally requires the rental unit for a period of at least one year.

 

Good Faith

 

13. The only remaining issue to be determined is whether the Landlord requires the unit in good faith.

 

14. The Landlord bears the obligation to prove the good faith requirement and is required to establish that the person purporting to live there genuinely intends to live in the rental unit

 

for at least one year. The Landlord’s motives are only relevant as evidence from which inferences can be drawn when deciding whether a genuine or sincere intention to occupy the unit exists [Fava v. Harrison, [2014] O.J. No. 2678 (Div. Ct); Salter v. Beljinac, 2001 CanLII 40231 (ON SCDC), [2001] O.J. No. 2792 (Div. Ct.)].

 

15. The Landlord testified that she purchased the unit with the intent of using it as her primary residence. She also purchased the adjoining property. Her daughter and her children will be residing in that unit. This will allow them to be closer together, reduce costs and enjoy more space than their previous home. She testified that while she awaits to take possession of the unit, she is living in an apartment with her daughter and grandson on the couch. She intends to move into the unit immediately, will reside in the rental unit full-time and for a period of at least one year.

 

16. I am therefore satisfied on the balance of probabilities, that the Landlord in good faith requires possession of the rental unit for the purpose of residential occupation and she genuinely intends to reside in the rental unit and for a period of one year.

 

N5 Notice

 

17. The Landlord also applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenants because they, another occupant of the rental unit or someone they permitted in the residential complex have wilfully or negligently caused undue damage to the premises. The N5 notice was served on the tenant on July 7, 2021. The Landlord has also applied for an order requiring the Tenants to compensate the Landlord for the damage

 

18. The N5 notice alleges that an RV backed into the hydro meter for the rental unit and the cost to repair the damage was $1,192.15. An invoice from Plante Electric Ltd. was attached to the N5 notice, outlining this amount. Photographs of the damage were submitted into evidence. The Landlord testified that she received a call from M. Young regarding this incident. She testified that she received an email dated December 17, 2021 where M. Young states her friend backed into the hydro meter with his RV by accident and blamed the poor lighting. Within seven days of the notice being served on the Tenants, the Landlord testified that the Tenants did not pay the Landlord for the damage.

 

19. In order to be successful in the damages claim, the Landlord, as the applicant, must establish all of the elements of sections 62 and 89 of the Act. It must be established that a tenant, an occupant of the rental unit or a person the tenant permitted in the complex negligently or willfully caused undue damage to the rental unit or complex.

 

20. On a balance of probabilities, I find that the Tenant and/or a person she permitted in the residential complex negligently caused undue damage. The damages are well-documented in the photographs. The Landlord’s uncontested evidence was that M. Young conceded the damage was caused by her friend backing up his RV into the hydro meter. I am also satisfied the Tenants did not void the notice by paying for the damage within seven days.

 

21. Regarding the Landlord’s claim for compensation for damages, I am satisfied that the amount to repair the damage of $1,192.15 is reasonable. The invoice attached to the N5

 

and submitted into evidence outlines that the power was restored at the unit after an emergency call.

 

Relief from Eviction

 

22. I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would be unfair to grant relief from eviction pursuant to subsection 83(1) of the Act. The Tenants were not present to disclose any circumstances for me to consider delaying or denying eviction. As such, I find that no relief should be provided to the Tenants.

 

It is ordered that:

 

Regarding the L1:

 

1.     Unless the Tenant voids the order as set out below, the tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenants is terminated. The Tenants must move out of the rental unit on or before January 17, 2022.

 

2.     The Tenants shall pay to the Landlord $4,091.70*, which represents the amount of rent owing and compensation up to January 6, 2022.

 

3.     The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlord $25.64 per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting January 7, 2022 to the date the Tenants move out of the unit.

 

4.     The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlord $201.00 for the cost of filing the application.

 

5.     If the Tenants do not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before January 17, 2022, the Tenants will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from January 18, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.

 

6.     If the unit is not vacated on or before January 17, 2022, then starting January 18, 2022, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

 

7.     Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord, on or after January 18, 2022.

 

8.     If, on or before January 17, 2022, the Tenants pay the amount of $4,881.00** to the Landlord or to the Board in trust, this order for eviction will be void. This means that the tenancy would not be terminated and the Tenants could remain in the unit. If this payment is not made in full and on time, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

 

9.     The Tenants may make a motion to the Board under subsection 74(11) of the Act to set aside this order if they pay the amount required under that subsection on or after January 18, 2022 but before the Sheriff gives vacant possession to the Landlord. The Tenants are

 

only entitled to make this motion once during the period of the tenancy agreement with the Landlord.

 

Regarding the L2:

 

10.  The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenants is terminated, as of January 17, 2022. The Tenants must move out of the rental unit on or before January 17, 2022.

 

11.  The Tenants shall pay to the Landlord $1,192.15, which represents the reasonable costs of repairing the damage.

 

12.  If the Tenants do not pay the Landlord the full amount owing on or before January 17, 2022, they will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from January 18, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.

 

13.  If the unit is not vacated on or before January 17, 2022, then starting January 18, 2022, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

 

14.  Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord on or after January 18, 2022.
