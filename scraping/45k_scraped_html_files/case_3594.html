Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: SOL-19429-21

 

 

In the matter of:

70, 80 GILCREST STREET HAMILTON ON L9C7L9

 

Between:

Victoria Park Community Homes Inc.

Landlord

 

and

 

 

Jessica Mooney

Tenant

 

 

Victoria Park Community Homes Inc. (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Jessica Mooney (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or a person the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has seriously impaired the safety of any person. The Landlord also claimed compensation for each day the Tenant remained in the unit after the termination date.

 

This application was heard via video conference on July 21, 2021.

 

The Landlord’s legal representative, Sara Lange attended the hearing. The Tenant and the Tenant’s legal representative, Michael Blashko attended the hearing.

 

The Landlord called as a witness: Vlado Izmaragdi (VI), the Resident Manager. The initials, LC refers to the Tenant’s friend, Lucas Couto.

Determinations:

 

1.     The Landlord served the Tenant with a Notice to End Your Tenancy for Causing Serious Problems in the Rental Unit or Residential Complex (Form N7). The allegations in the notice states: On or about January 6, 2021, a Facebook video of a visitor or occupant of the Tenant, was discovered. The video was taken in the basement of the Tenant’s unit and shows LC in possession of multiple high-powered firearms, and several cases of ammunition. LC is seen in the video showing off the weapons and shooting towards the camera.

 

2.     The issue to be determined by the Board is whether the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or a person the Tenant permitted in the residential complex has seriously impaired the safety of any person and this behaviour occurred in the residential complex.

 

3.     The residential complex has 70 units. The Tenant rents a semi-detached, three bedroom townhouse.

 

4.     The Landlord’s witness, the Resident Manager, VI testified that he learned of this matter when he was sent an email from another individual that showed ammunition and the weapons. VI stated that he has a photograph taken on October 2, 2020 of 5 rifles lying on a carpet. VI also stated that the photograph was uploaded on the Tenant’s boyfriend (LC’s) account.

 

5.     VI stated that on January 5, 2021 he received a video, from the same individual that provided the photographs of the rifles. The video shows the basement of the Tenant’s unit and LC showing the ammunition and what appears to be assault rifles. LC is shown pointing the rifle at the camera.

 

6.     VI testified that when he saw the video he was scared. He believes that the weapons were real. The weapons were AR 15 and M4 rifles and were similar styles of rifles that are used by the military and police.

 

7.     VI attends the residential complex, 5 days a week. VI stated that since February 2020 he has seen LC living at the unit.

 

8.     The Landlord submitted into evidence the photograph of the 5 rifles and the video.

 

9.     During cross examination VI acknowledged that he has never seen or examined the devices in the video. VI also acknowledged that he did not see the entire 23 minute video. When asked if it is possible that the weapons in the video were fake, VI testified that he was no expert, but the weapons in the video were real. VI also stated that he grew up in a war torn country and is familiar with AR 15 and M4 and the weapons in the video were similar.

 

10.  VI testified that the police were contacted about the weapons in the unit, and all the evidence was sent to the police. The police were told about the content of the video and were give the name and account.

 

11.  VI confirmed that since the complaint to the police (7 months ago), he is not aware of any police investigation, but stated that these investigations are not done overnight.

 

12.  VI stated that there have been no further videos, and no incidents with firearms in the unit.

 

13.  The Tenant testified that she learned about the matter on or about January 1, 2021 when she received a message on Facebook from another tenant, yelling and threatening her

that she would be “kicked out because of LC”. She sent an email to the Landlord about this and the Landlord told her there was no truth to this. She discussed other matters with the Landlord. On January 13, 2021 she received the Form N7 Notice of Termination. She had asked the Landlord to see the video after receiving the Form N7, but only saw the video a week before the hearing.

 

14.  The Tenant testified that she was in the basement at the time of the video. She confirmed that she once dated LC, but he is now a friend. LC no longer has access to her unit.

 

15.  The Tenant testified that the firearms are air soft or bb guns, and Canadian Tire type of guns. The Tenant testified that she would never allow real guns in her home. She purchased them on her credit card for LC; and LC paid for therm. She no longer has them in her unit.

 

16.  The Tenant submitted into evidence documents showing that she purchased air guns and BB rifle on June 18, 2020 from a company called Replica Airguns.

 

17.  Based on the evidence adduced at the hearing, I find that the Landlord has not provided sufficient evidence to establish that the Tenant, another occupant of the rental unit, or a person permitted in the residential complex by the Tenant has seriously impaired the safety of other persons. I say this because, I was not satisfied by the Landlord’s evidence that the firearms shown in the video and the photographs are real weapons. The Landlord’s witness, VI is not an expert in firearms. The Landlord has never viewed the weapons in person and there was no witness called to authenticate the weapons shown in the video or the photograph. I preferred the evidence of the Tenant, including the documentary evidence that the weapons are air rifles and bb guns; and are not real assault rifles. It is clear that these weapons are made to look as realistic as possible, but that does not make them real. I also considered that the Landlord complained to the police, provided the police with all their evidence, and it does not appear that the police have taken any action on this matter. It seems to me, if there were imminent risk to safety, the police would have taken immediate action. In addition, there was no evidence before me that there have been incidents involving firearms, air guns or bb guns at the unit or the residential complex, and that anyone’s safety has been seriously impaired.

 

18.  Based on the above, the Landlord’s application shall be dismissed.

 

19.  The Landlord requested that if the eviction is denied that the Tenant’s guest be banned from the residential complex. This request is denied as the Board has dismissed this application. Moreover, there has been no determination made that there has been a breach of the Act, that warrants this request.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.   The Landlord’s application is dismissed.
