Order under Section 21.2 of the

Statutory Powers Procedure Act

and the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: TSL-18868-20-RV

 

 

In the matter of:

106, 35 LIBERTY STREET TORONTO ON M6K1A6

 

Between:

2-8 St. Thomas St. Holdings Inc.

Landlord

 

 

and

 

 

Neil Williamson

Tenant

 

 

Review Order

 

2-8 St. Thomas St. Holdings Inc. (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Neil Williamson (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes.

 

This application was resolved by order TSL-18868-20 issued on August 25, 2021.

 

On October 12, 2021, the Court Enforcement Office, (the Sheriff) enforced the order issued on August 25, 2021.

 

On October 13, 2021 the Tenant requested a review of the order, and that the order be stayed until the request to review the order is resolved.

 

On October 14, 2021 interim order TSL-18868-20 -IN was issued, staying the order issued on August 25, 2021.

 

On October 19, 2021, Justice Corbitt of the Divisional Court, confirmed a stay on the portion of Interim Order issued October 14, 2021, which directed the Landlord to restore the Tenancy. The remainder direction lifted the Stay of Proceedings for the purpose of the Board to conduct an expedited Review Hearing.

 

The Tenant alleges that he did not have the capacity to self-represent nor to properly consult duty Counsel at the hearing on the merits.

 

The review was heard via videoconference on December 13, 2021.

 

The Landlords Legal Representative Martin Zarnett, and Agents Janice Brown and Eadefemi Adejumo, and the Tenant attended the hearing. The tenant was assisted by he daughter Jennifer Williamson.

 

 

 

Determinations:

 

1.     At the outset, the Tenant requested an adjournment as he had recently been released from hospital and would like time to retain and instruct counsel. He indicates that he and his family have been focusing on his health care and not in preparing for this hearing. It was only once the Notice of hearing was issued that they learned that Legal Aid would not represent them at this hearing. They indicated that they had not made other efforts to retain counsel.

 

2.     The Landlord objected to this on basis that the Tenant was represented by a lawyer when the review was filed and that it would be prejudicial to the Landlord if adjourned.

 

3.     The Tenant confirmed his desire to regain possession of the rental unit. On this basis, I denied the request to adjourn so that the issue of Tenancy could be resolved. I indicated that the health issue might be grounds to adjourn, however as he is assisted by his daughter, that was not necessary. The daughter had been able to read all the documents, and could read anything to her father if necessary for the purposes of this hearing.

 

4.     The Tenant described the circumstances that led to the consent agreement order being issued. He had been in touch with ACTO (Legal Aid clinic) the week prior to the hearing, and after that was prepared to negotiate a consent agreement with the Landlord, which he did. Subsequently, the Tenant was injured, required hospitalization and in fact was in hospital and participated in the hearing from there.

 

5.     The Tenant testified that he was happy with the settlement, and that he just wanted to get it over with as he believed he could pay the rent and live up to his commitments.

 

6.     The hearing recording from June 9, 2021 was played. In it the Tenant is heard actively participating in the hearing process, asking questions and seeking confirmation of the side-deal the parties had entered into when the consent agreement was negotiated.

 

7.     The Tenant can be heard confirming the terms of the consent agreement, his understanding that if not paid he could be evicted. He confirmed that he knew he was doing a consent deal.

 

8.     The Tenant confirmed that he did not have a capacity assessment completed, that he is not represented by a Public Guardian, nor anyone else to represent him to manage his affairs or property. Further the Tenant confirmed that he did not speak with Duty Counsel on the hearing day, when afforded that opportunity as he had “nothing” to discuss with them.

 

9.     Finally, the Tenant confirmed that he understood that he had reached a consent arrangement with the Landlord prior to the day of the hearing and that he had participated in the hearing to have it presented to the Board.

 

10.   Disability is defined as “mentally incapable within the meaning of section 6 or 45 of

the Substitute Decisions Act, 1992 ( the ‘SDA’) in respect of an issue in the proceeding, whether the person has a guardian or not; Rules of Civil Procedure r 1.03(1).

 

11.  Section 2 and 3 of the SDA reads as follows:

 

2 (1) A person who is eighteen years of age or more is presumed to be capable of entering into a contract. 1992, c. 30, s. 2 (1).

 

(2)  A person who is sixteen years of age or more is presumed to be capable of giving or refusing consent in connection with his or her own personal care. 1992, c. 30, s. 2 (2).

 

(3)  A person is entitled to rely upon the presumption of capacity with respect to another person unless he or she has reasonable grounds to believe that the other person is incapable of entering into the contract or of giving or refusing consent, as the case may be. 1992, c. 30, s. 2 (3).

 

12.  The issue before this hearing is was the Tenant mentally incapable in relation to the issues before the Board on June 9, 2021.

 

13.  Did the Tenant have an inability to know or understand the minimum choices or decisions required and to make them? The hearing recording and the Tenant’s admissions indicate that the Tenant understood the hearing, actively participated and was able to ask questions to confirm the side deal.

 

14.  Did the Tenant appreciate the consequences and effects of his choices or decisions? The Tenant confirmed and the hearing recording indicated that he understood that the Landlord could file for eviction without notice under s.78 of the Act.

 

15.  Did the Tenant understand the nature of the proceedings? The proceedings on June 9, 2021 related to residential tenancy, in which as a tenant he understood that he had not been paying rent, for which he could face eviction. These are not complicated proceedings; the legislation is remedial in nature with a tenant protection focus. The Tenant admitted and the recording confirmed the Tenant understood that the purpose of the hearing was to submit the terms of the consent agreement for the Board to issue an order to avoid being evicted.

 

16.  Did the Tenant have an inability to retain and instruct counsel. The Tenant admitted that the spoke with ACTO (Legal Aid) in the week prior to the June 9, 2021 hearing and that he entered into an agreement notwithstanding their advice. The Tenant admitted that he did not speak with Duty Counsel on the day of the hearing when offered the opportunity, as he stated because he had nothing to discuss with them.

 

17.  I am satisfied on a balance of probabilities that the Tenant was capable of giving the consent that he gave at the hearing on June 9, 2020. The Tenant was capable of actively participating in the hearing, confirming the side-deal and that he was aware of the consequences. In addition, at no time did the Tenant indicate he was in hospital, and heavily medicated, or have someone come on the call to assist him, all indications that

 

might have triggered the Presiding Member to ask confirming questions regarding any potential incapacity. As a result, I am satisfied that the Tenant was reasonably able to participate in the June 9, 2021 hearing.

 

18.  On the basis of the submissions made in the request, I am not satisfied that there is a serious error in the order or that a serious error occurred in the proceedings.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.     The request to review order TSL-18868-20 issued on August 25, 2021 is denied. The order is confirmed and remains unchanged.

 

2.     The interim order issued on October 14, 2021 is cancelled. The stay of order TSL-18868- 20 is lifted. The order to reinstate the Tenant back into the unit is also cancelled.
