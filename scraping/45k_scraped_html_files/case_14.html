Order under Section 135

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: CET-95229-20

 

 

In the matter of:

95 CREDITSTONE ROAD BRAMPTON ON L6Y4G1

 

Between:

Braden Mcarthur

Tenant

 

 

and

 

 

Lillian Mendoza

Landlord

 

 

Braden Mcarthur (the 'Tenant') applied for an order determining that Lillian Mendoza (the 'Landlord') has collected or retained money illegally.

 

The hearing was held by videoconference on February 10, 2022. The Tenant and the Landlord were both present at the hearing. The Tenant’s agent, Kim Mcarthur, was also present at the

hearing.

 

Preliminary Issue – Amending the T1 Application:

 

1.      The Tenant’s agent requested that the Tenant’s T1 application be amended to include a second reason for the application – that the Tenant paid an illegal damage deposit charge of $675.00 to the Landlord. The agent submitted that the current reason for the T1 application lists the $675.00 as a rent deposit that was not used for the last rental period of the tenancy. The agent explained that at the time of filing the application the Tenant was not aware that this $675.00 was also potentially an illegal charge by the Landlord.

 

2.      The Landlord questioned the Tenant’s ability to amend the application at the hearing. The Landlord stated that she was prepared to respond to the Tenant’s allegation that she did not use the $675.00 rent deposit for the last month’s rent; however, she was not prepared to respond to the Tenant’s allegation that the $675.00 she collected at the start of the tenancy was an illegal charge.

 

3.      Section 200(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act') states:

 

An applicant may amend an application to the Board in accordance with the Rules.

 

4.      The Board’s Rule 15.4, Amending Applications, states:

 

The LTB may exercise its discretion to grant a request to amend made at the hearing if satisfied the amendment is appropriate, would not prejudice any party and is

consistent with a fair and expeditious proceeding.

 

5.      A T1 is an application by the Tenant for a rebate of money that the Landlord owes. It is clear in the Tenant’s original T1 that the money in dispute is the Landlord’s collection of

$675.00 at the start of the tenancy, and the retention of this $675.00 at the end of the tenancy. The Landlord was prepared to respond to this allegation.

6.      I am satisfied that an amendment to the Tenant’s T1 that adds another reason to the application alleging that the $675.00 was also an illegal charge does not require

additional preparation by the Landlord to respond to the allegation. Whether the $675.00 is identified as a rent deposit, an illegal charge, or both in the application – the Landlord’s preparation remains sufficiently similar. For this reason, I find that amending the Tenant’s application at the hearing does not prejudice the Landlord.

7.      Therefore, I consent to amending the Tenant’s T1 application, pursuant to s. 200(1) of the Act and Board Rule 15.4, by adding the reason to the application that the Tenant paid an illegal charge of $675.00 to the Landlord.

 

Determinations:

 

8.      On July 23, 2020 the Tenant applied to the Board, pursuant to s. 135(1) of the Act with a T1 application (T1) alleging that the Landlord did not use the rent deposit of $675.00 to pay the last month’s rent, and did not return the rent deposit to the Tenant at the end of the tenancy. At the hearing, the Tenant’s application was amended to also allege that the Landlord illegally collected the $675.00 deposit.

9.      The Tenant moved into the unit on August 28, 2019 and paid a deposit of $675.00 to the Landlord via e-transfer on August 29, 2019. The Tenant moved out of the rental unit on April 25, 2020 and alleges that the Landlord did not use the $675.00 to pay the last

month’s rent, and did not return the rent deposit to him. The Tenant filed this application within one-year of the date that the money was allegedly collected and retained illegally pursuant to s. 135(4) of the Act. Therefore, the Tenant’s allegations will be considered.

 

10.   The monthly rent at the termination of the tenancy was $700.00.

 

Deposit of $675.00

 

11.   The Tenant testified that he paid a $675.00 damage deposit to the Landlord via e-transfer on August 29, 2019. He stated that after vacating the unit on April 25, 2020, he asked the Landlord on May 4 and 5, 2020 to return the $675.00 damage deposit to him; however, the Landlord retained the money. The Tenant testified further that the Landlord had retained the deposit because he had not repaired some scuff marks on the walls of the rental unit. The Tenant remarked that he paid rent for April 2020, the last month of the tenancy, and that there were no rent arrears.

12.   The Landlord testified that at the start of the tenancy she requested from the Tenant the first month’s rent of $700.00, and a damage deposit of $675.00. She stated that she received these funds from the Tenant on August 29, 2019. The Landlord testified further that the Tenant’s $675.00 deposit was not returned to him as a result of damage to the walls of the rental unit that occurred during the Tenant’s tenancy.

 

13.   The Landlord explained that she noticed the damage to the unit walls on April 25, 2020, and that the Tenant acknowledged the damage and agreed to complete repairs; however, the repairs were never completed. The Landlord remarked that the damage to the unit walls exceeded $675.00; therefore, she retained the Tenant’s deposit. The Landlord acknowledged that there were no rent arrears when the Tenant vacated the unit on April 25, 2020.

 

Analysis

 

14.   Section 105(1) of the Act states:

 

The only security deposit that a landlord may collect is a rent deposit collected in accordance with section 106.

 

15.   Section 106(10) of the Act states:

 

A landlord shall apply a rent deposit that a tenant has paid to the landlord or to a former landlord in payment of the rent for the last rent period before the tenancy terminates.

16.   Section 134(1) of the Act states:

 

(1)     Unless otherwise prescribed, no landlord shall, directly or indirectly, with respect to any rental unit,

 

(a) collect or require or attempt to collect or require from a tenant, prospective tenant or former tenant of the rental unit a fee, premium, commission, bonus, penalty, key deposit or other like amount of money whether or not the money is refundable.

 

17.   Section 135(1) of the Act states:

 

A tenant or former tenant of a rental unit may apply to the Board for an order that the landlord, superintendent or agent of the landlord pay to the tenant any money the person collected or retained in contravention of this Act or the Tenant Protection Act, 1997.

 

18.   On the basis of the evidence provided, I find that the Landlord collected a $675.00 deposit from the Tenant on August 29, 2019, and this deposit was not used for the last month’s rent of April 2020 pursuant to s. 106(10) of the Act.

19.   On the basis of the evidence provided, I further find that the $675.00 deposit collected by the Landlord on August 29, 2019 was collected for the purpose of potential damage to the unit by the Tenant. Collection of a deposit for this purpose is prohibited by s. 105(1) and

s. 134(1)(a) of the Act, and is not an exemption to s. 134(1)(a) of the Act pursuant to s. 17 of Ontario Regulation 516/06.

20.   I therefore find that the Landlord’s retention of the Tenant’s $675.00 deposit is in

contravention of the Act, and that the Landlord owes the Tenant $675.00 pursuant to s. 135(1) of the Act.

 

21.   The fact that the Landlord believes that the Tenant caused damage does not justify the Landlord’s illegal collection and retention of a damage deposit. If a landlord believes that a tenant or former tenant has willfully or negligently caused undue damage the landlord may file an application at the Board making such a claim.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.      The Landlord shall pay to the Tenant the sum of $675.00 representing the money collected by the Landlord as a damage deposit.

 

2.      The Landlord shall also pay to the Tenant $53.00 for the cost of filing the application.

 

3.      The total amount the Landlord owes to the Tenant is $728.00.

 

4.      If the Landlord does not pay the Tenant the full amount owing by March 31, 2022, the Landlord will owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from April 1, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the outstanding balance.

 

5.      The Tenant has the right, at any time, to collect the full amount owing or any balance outstanding under this order.
