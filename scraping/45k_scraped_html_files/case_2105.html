Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: SOL-23695-21

 

 

In the matter of:

107, 20 TREMONT DRIVE

ST. CATHARINES ON L2T3B1

 

Between:

Squire Apartments Inc

Landlord

 

 

and

 

 

Ashley Dalcourt Benjamin Robida

Tenants

 

 

 

Squire Apartments Inc (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Benjamin Robida and Ashley Dalcourt (the 'Tenants') because the Tenants did not pay the rent that the Tenants owe (‘L1 Application’). The Landlord also applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenants because they, another occupant of the rental unit or someone they permitted in the residential complex have wilfully or negligently caused undue damage to the premises. The Landlord has also applied for an order requiring the Tenants to compensate the Landlord for the damage.

 

This application was heard by videoconference on October 27, 2021. Only the Landlord’s agent, David Rootham, attended the hearing. As of 9:40 a.m. the Tenants were not present or represented at the hearing.

 

Determinations:

 

1.     The Landlord was no longer seeking an eviction order as the Tenants had vacated the rental unit prior to the hearing.

 

L1 Application

 

2.     The Tenants have not paid the total rent the Tenants were required to pay for the period from May 1, 2020 to August 21, 2021. Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective May 15, 2021.

 

3.     The Tenants vacated the rental unit on August 21, 2021. The Tenants were in possession of the rental unit when this application was filed.

 

4.     The lawful monthly rent was $1,528.00.

 

5.     The Tenants made no payments after this application was filed.

 

6.     The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $1,495.00 from the Tenants and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

 

7.     Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenants for the period from July 1, 2019 to May 15, 2021.

 

L2 Application

 

1.     Section 89 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006, S.O. 2006, c.17 (‘Act’,) states the following:

 

A landlord may apply to the Board for an order requiring a tenant to pay reasonable costs that the landlord has incurred or will incur for the repair of or, where repairing is not reasonable, the replacement of damaged property, if the tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or a person whom the tenant permits in the residential complex wilfully or negligently causes undue damage to the rental unit or the residential complex and the tenant is in possession of the rental unit.

2.     The Landlord alleged that the Tenants caused the following undue damages to the rental unit or residential complex:

 

a.   Broken security glass of two interior doors;

b.   Damaged hallway emergency light;

c.   Damaged and stained floors;

d.   Changed looking mechanism;

e.   Damaged outside south entrance building door;

f.     Damaged suite entrance door;

g.   Damaged entrance closet door;

h.   Damaged bathroom towel bar;

i.      Damaged main bathroom door and frame;

j.      Damaged and chipped main bathtub;

k.   Damaged soap dish;

l.      Damaged hallway closet door;

m.  Damaged bedroom closet door;

n.   Damaged two bedroom doors;

o.   Three missing window restrictors in bedrooms;

p.   Damaged wall in the master bedroom;

q.   Damaged window pane frame in the master bedroom;

r.     Damaged faceplate on a living room outlet;

s.   Damaged kitchen countertop; and

 

t.     Damaged kitchen cupboards.

3.     The Landlord’s application claimed $4,672.79 in costs to repair or replace the damaged items in the rental unit. This claim was supported by quotes and invoices totalling more than the amount claimed.

 

4.     The Landlord testified that the nature of the damage was well beyond normal wear and tear and that it was caused by the Tenants. The Landlords’ testimony was supported by photographs. The photographs showed the damage and were consistent with the Landlord’s testimony about the nature and the cause of the damage. I found the Landlord’s testimony to be credible and reliable.

 

5.     Based on the uncontested evidence before the Board, on the balance of probabilities, I find that the Tenants have wilfully or negligently caused undue damage throughout the residential unit and that this damage is undue.

 

6.     On the uncontested evidence before the Board, I find that the Landlord has incurred costs and will incur future costs of $4,672.79 to repair the damage to the rental unit and replace the garage door.

 

8.   The Landlord has incurred the cost of $201.00 for filing the application and is entitled to reimbursement of those costs.

 

It is ordered that:

 

L1 Application

 

1.     The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenants is terminated effective August 21, 2021, the date the Tenants vacated the rental unit.

 

2.     The Tenants shall pay to the Landlord $4,080.31*, which represents the amount of rent owing and compensation up to August 21, 2021, less the rent deposit and interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.

 

L2 Application

 

3.     The Tenants shall pay to the Landlord $4,672.79, which represents the reasonable costs of repairing the damage and replacing the damaged property.

 

Total

 

4.     The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlord $201.00 for the cost of filing the application.

 

5.     The Total amount that the Tenants owe the Landlord under the L1 and the L2 components is $8,954.10. The Tenants shall pay this amount to the Landlord on or before February 6, 2022.

 

6.     If the Tenants do not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before February 6, 2022, the Tenants will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from February 7, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
