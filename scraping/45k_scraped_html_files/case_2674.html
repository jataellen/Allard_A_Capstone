Order under Section 78(11)

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: EAL-97705-21-SA

 

 

In the matter of:      403, 722 JOHN COUNTER BOULEVARD

KINGSTON ON K7K6N9

 

Between:                  Skyline Living                                                                                                Landlord

 

 

and

 

Karen Mainprize                                                                                                                 Tenant

 

 

 

Skyline Living (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Karen Mainprize (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant failed to meet a condition specified in the order issued by the Board on September 7, 2021 with respect to application EAL-95464-21.

 

The Landlord's application was resolved by order EAL-97705-21, issued on October 14, 2021. The Tenant filed a motion to set aside order EAL-97705-21.

 

This motion was heard by video conference on January 7, 2022. The Landlord’s Agent Brad Casnaw and the Tenant attended the hearing.

 

 

Determinations:

 

1.        The Landlord applied for an order terminating the tenancy and evicting the Tenant because the Tenant failed to meet a condition specified in the order issued by the Board on September 7, 2021 with respect to application EAL-95464-21.

 

2.        The Landlord claims that on September 7, 2021, the Tenant’s guest entered the residential building without a face mask. The relevant condition is paragraph 1 of the mediated agreement, which states:

 

The Tenant, any occupants and/or guests shall wear a facemask at all times when in the common areas of the residential complex for as long as this policy exists.

 

3.        The parties understood the condition meant that the Tenant would ensure that her guests wear masks in the building. The Tenant lives with a disability, and her source of income is disability benefits. She testified that she cannot compel her guests to actually wear face masks but can merely insist that they do so. She cannot afford to supply her guests with face masks on her budget. She stated that she has taken all reasonable steps to comply

 

with the conditions of the order, including (i) insisting to her guests that they wear a face mask in the building, and (ii) providing face masks to them when they enter the building, if she has them available. She testified that she did so on the incident date.

 

4.        I find that, on the balance of probabilities, the Tenant has not substantively breached the condition of the agreement. Rather, I am satisfied that she has made her best effort to comply with the condition. Therefore, the motion to set aside the order is granted.

 

5.        Pursuant to O.Reg.364/20 (Rules for Step 3 and Roadmap Exit, as of September 2021) and O.Reg.263/20 (Rules for Step 2, current), the onus is upon the person responsible for a building that is open to the public to ensure that any person inside the premises wears a properly fitting mask or face covering unless the individual entering the premises is exempt from wearing a mask under the regulations.

 

6.        The municipality of Kingston has put into place By-Law 2020-068 and Order stating that “every person shall wear a face covering while in any enclosed common area” in a multi- residential complex, which includes apartment buildings. Consistent with the regulations, owners of the residential complex must ensure that all persons in the common area wear a face covering. Owners must post a copy of the requirement in the building for visitors and residents to read. While the Landlord did not present these documents as evidence, there is no question that they exist and may be relied upon by residents and business owners.

 

7.        There is a joint responsibility of the Landlord and the Tenant in ensuring that the regulations and the by-law are followed. I note that these laws do not provide for disability accommodation with respect of the ability of a person living with a disability being able to understand and/or strictly comply with the rules. The concept of joint responsibility, then, results in the benefit to a landlord and a tenant of working together to ensure the face mask requirement is met.

 

8.        It is undisputed that wearing a face mask as required during the Covid-19 pandemic is critically important to the health and safety of individuals, among other considerations. At the hearing, the parties mutually agreed to an order varying or replacing the conditions set out in Order EAL-95464-21, to facilitate future compliance.

 

9.        I was satisfied that the parties understood the consequences of varying or replacing the terms of the order. As a result, the terms of the previous order EAL-95464-21 shall be varied and a new order issued: paragraphs 1, 3 and 4 shall be amended in accordance with the terms below. Paragraph 2 of the Order EAL-95464-21 will remain in force.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.        The motion to set aside Order EAL-97705-21, issued on October 14, 2021 is granted. Order EAL-95464-21 is cancelled and replaced with the following.

 

2.        Commencing on January 1, 2022 and until June 1, 2022, the Tenant, any occupants of the Tenant’s rental unit and guests of the Tenant shall not walk their dog(s) through the front entrance of the building.

 

3.        For the duration of the time period that the Government of Ontario recommends or requires that individuals wear face masks during the Covid-19 pandemic,

 

(i)        the Tenant shall wear a face mask in the common areas of the building,

(ii)      the Tenant shall ask her guests in advance- before they visit- to bring and wear a properly fitting face mask before or at the time they enter the building, unless the guest is not required to wear a mask for medical reasons, or any other reason set out in section 2(4) of Ontario Regulation 263/20), and

(iii)     the Tenant shall inform her guests, with as much notice as possible in advance, that if the guest does not wear a face mask when he/she/they enters the building, the guest will not be allowed into the building.

 

4.        In a situation where the Tenant’s guest is required to wear a mask and the Tenant knows that the guest is entering the building without wearing a mask, the Tenant shall immediately contact the Landlord or the Landlord’s designated property manager or superintendent by telephone, text message or email to inform the Landlord that she has informed her guest of the need to wear a mask, of any refusal by that guest to wear a mask, and that the guest is intending on entering the building. In such a case, the Tenant shall also refuse the guest’s visit.

 

5.        The Landlord shall document the incident in paragraph 4 above, in writing and provide a copy to the Tenant in a timely manner.

 

6.        If a guest of the Tenant informs the Landlord or Landlord’s agent that he/she/they is exempt from wearing a mask, the Landlord shall not refuse permission for the person to enter the building but may remind the person of the social distancing policy of the Government of Ontario as it relates to common areas of the building.

 

7.        Commencing on February 1, 2022, the Tenant shall pay the $186.00 application filing fee to the Landlord or Landlord’s agent in the amount of $20.00 per month, and shall include this amount with her regular monthly rent payments as follows:

8.        If the Tenant does not pay the Landlord the full amount owing in paragraph 7, above, on or before November 1, 2022, the Tenant will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from November 2, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.

 

9.        If the Tenant fails to meet any of the terms in paragraphs 2, 3 and 4, above, the Landlord may apply under s.78 of the Act, without notice to the Tenant, for an order terminating the tenancy and evicting the Tenant. The Landlord must make this application no later than 30 days after the Tenant fails to comply with the condition.

 

10.   The Landlord shall forthwith review this order with the Tenant, or arrange for a tenant support person, to review the order with the Tenant, to promote compliance with the terms.

 

11.   The Landlord shall provide the Tenant with written notice of any changes to face mask requirements as they occur. The notice shall include a plain language description of the changes, the dates the changes are to occur and the responsibilities of the Tenant, if any.
