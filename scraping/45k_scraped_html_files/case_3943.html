Order under Section 78(6)

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

MISSISSAUGA ON L4Y2N9

and

 

Guillermo Bartlett Jessica Bartlett

Tenants

 

 

 

On August 9, 2021, Quadreal Residential Properties G.p.inc (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Jessica Bartlett and Guillermo Bartlett (the 'Tenants') and for an order to have the Tenants pay the rent they owe because the Tenants failed to meet a condition specified in the order issued by the Board on July 26, 2021 with respect to application CEL-99391-21.

 

Determinations:

 

1.     The order provided that the Landlord could apply to the Board under section 78 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act') without notice to the Tenants to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenants if the Tenants did not meet certain condition(s) specified in the order.

 

2.     I find that the Tenants have not met the following condition specified in the order:

 

The Tenants failed to pay to the Landlord $1,845.73 on or before August 1, 2021 towards rent.

 

3.     The previous application included a request for an order for the payment of arrears of rent. The resulting order required the Tenants to pay rent or some or all of the arrears of rent. Accordingly, in addition to eviction, the Landlord is entitled to request an order for the payment of arrears of rent and compensation.

 

4.     The Tenants were ordered to pay $13,145.84 for rent arrears and the costs related to the Landlord's application fee in Order CEL-99391-21. The amount that is still owing from that order is $13,145.84 and that amount is included in this order. As a result, the previous order CEL-99391-21 is cancelled.

 

5.     The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $1,845.73 from the Tenants and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

 

6.     Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenants for the period from August 1, 2020 to January 4, 2022.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.     Order CEL-99391-21 is cancelled.

 

2.     The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenants is terminated. The Tenants must move out of the rental unit on or before January 15, 2022.

 

3.     The Tenants shall pay to the Landlord $13,143.98*. This amount represents the rent owing up to August 31, 2021 and the costs related to the application fee for the previous application, less the rent deposit and interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.

 

4.     The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlord $60.68 per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting September 1, 2021 the date the Tenants move out of the unit.

 

5.     If the Tenants do not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before January 15, 2022, the Tenants will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from January 16, 2022 at .00% annually on the balance outstanding.

 

6.     If the unit is not vacated on or before January 15, 2022, then starting January 16, 2022, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

 

7.     Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord on or after January 16, 2022.
