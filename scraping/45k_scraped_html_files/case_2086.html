Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: TEL-19621-21

 

 

In the matter of:

58 HYDRANGEA HOLLOW HOLLAND LANDING ON L9N0T1

 

Between:

Maryam Bakhshi

Landlord

 

and

 

 

Charles Chessell Sokunthea Path

Tenants

 

 

 

Maryam Bakhshi (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Charles Chessell and Sokunthea Path (the 'Tenants') because the Tenants did not pay the rent that the Tenants owe (L1 application).

 

The Landlord also applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenants because they, another occupant of the rental unit or someone they permitted in the residential complex have wilfully or negligently caused undue damage to the premises. The Landlord has also applied for an order requiring the Tenants to compensate the Landlord for the damage; and because they, another occupant of the rental unit or someone they permitted in the residential complex have substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment or lawful right, privilege or interest of the Landlord or another tenant. The Landlord also claimed compensation for each day the Tenants remained in the unit after the termination date (L2 application).

 

This application was heard by videoconference on January 11, 2022.

 

The Landlord, the Landlord's Legal Representative, Katy Sanaye, and Sokunthea Path, on behalf of the Tenants attended the hearing.

 

Determinations:

 

L2 application

 

1.     For the reasons that follow, the Landlord’s L2 application is dismissed.

 

2.     On December 13, 2020 the Landlord served a notice of termination because the Tenant has substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment of the residential complex a lawful right, privilege or interest of the Landlord, and the Tenant caused willful or negligent damage. The termination date on the notice was January 4, 2021.

 

3.     The particulars in the notice were with respect to the Tenants not paying the water and sewage bill, consequently it was transferred into the Landlords name. The amount owing per the notice is $2,892.60.

 

4.     It is undisputed that the Tenants vacated January 31, 2021, therefore, as eviction is not necessary, the monies are being pursued pursuant to section 89 of the Act; an order for payment based on damages.

 

5.     Black’s Law Dictionary defines damage as:

 

Loss, injury, or deterioration, caused by the negligence, design, or accident of one person to another, in respect of the latter’s person or property.

 

6.     Section 89 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the ‘Act’) says:

 

(1)     A landlord may apply to the Board for an order requiring a tenant to pay reasonable costs that the landlord has incurred or will incur for the repair of or, where repairing is not reasonable, the replacement of damaged property, if the tenant, another occupant of the rental unit or a person whom the tenant permits in the residential complex wilfully or negligently causes undue damage to the rental unit or the residential complex and the tenant is in possession of the rental unit.

 

7.     It should be noted that effective September 1, 2021, section 88.2 of the Act came into force, and a Landlord can now file the new L10 application which addresses the issue of unpaid utilities. With respect to L2 applications where the Tenant was in possession at the time of filing, the application had to be filed on or after September 1, 2021; here the application was filed December 22, 2020.

 

L1 application

 

8.     The Tenants have not paid the total rent the Tenants were required to pay for the period from October 1, 2020 to January 31, 2022. Because of the arrears, the Landlord served a Notice of Termination effective January 31, 2021.

 

9.     The Tenants vacated the rental unit on May 31, 2021. The Tenants were in possession of the rental unit on the date the application was filed.

 

10.  The monthly rent is $2,450.00.

 

11.  The Landlord collected a rent deposit of $2,450.00 from the Tenants and this deposit is still being held by the Landlord.

 

12.  Interest on the rent deposit is owing to the Tenants for the period from May 24, 2019 to January 31, 2021.

 

13.  The Tenants did not pay the Landlord any monies after the application was filed. The total amount owing to the Landlord up to January 31, 2021, the date the Tenants vacated, is

$7,496.24.

 

14.  The Tenants did not dispute the amount owing but requested, due to COVID-19, one- year o repay the Landlord. the Landlord's Legal Representative submitted that a year was unreasonable and requested the standard 11-days.

 

15.  I find requiring the Landlord to waiting another year before being able to pursue the debt

the Tenants’ owe to be unreasonable. The application was filed December 21, 2020, over a year ago, it is unfair and prejudicial to the Landlord to require them to wait another year before repayment can begin; no monies have been received from the Tenants since September 2020.

 

16.  This order contains all the reasons for the decision within it. No further reasons shall be issued.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.     The Landlord’s L2 application is dismissed.

 

2.     The tenancy is terminated as of January 31, 2021, the date the Tenants gave vacant possession of the rental unit to the Landlord.

 

3.     The Tenants shall pay to the Landlord $7,292.24*, which represents the amount of rent owing and compensation up to January 31, 2022, less the rent deposit and interest the Landlord owes on the rent deposit.

 

4.     The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlord $201.00 for the cost of filing the application.

 

5.     If the Tenants do not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before February 6, 2022, the Tenants will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from February 7, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
