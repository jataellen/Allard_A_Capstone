Review Order

 

Global Properties Limited and Sunnydale Holdings Limited C/o Artisan Corp (the 'Landlords') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Rosa Marin (the 'Tenant') because the Tenant did not pay the rent that the Tenant owes.

 

This application was resolved by order SWL-48689-21 issued on October 19, 2021.

 

On November 16, 2021, the Tenant requested a review of the order alleging the order contains a serious error with respect the calculations in the N4 Notice of Termination, L1 application and the order itself

 

On November 23, 2021, the Board issued interim order SWL-48689-21-RV-IN staying the order issued October 19, 2021.

 

The Tenant’s request was heard by way of video conference on January 28, 2022. The Landlord’s Agent, Stephanie Kelly, the Landlord’s Representative, Nathan Chang, the Tenant, the Tenant’s daughter, Aline Blanco and the Tenant’s Legal Representative, Kristen Thompson attended the hearing.

 

Determinations:

 

Tenant’s Request to Review

 

1.        The Tenant’s Representative submitted that the calculations in the N4 Notice of Termination are incorrect, because the Landlord indicated the wrong amount for the monthly rent. The Landlord calculated the monthly rent since 2018 with lawful annual rent increases. The Tenant’s Representative entered in evidence emails between the Landlord’s property manager and the Tenant’s housing subsidy program confirming the

monthly rent has never been increased since the Tenant moved into the rental unit. One of the emails dated November 12, 2021 from the property manager to the housing subsidy program clearly states that there have been no rent increases and the monthly rent is $1,299.00.

 

2.        The Landlord’s Representative is the former owner of the rental property. As part of the purchase of sale, he now represents the Landlord for all matters before the Board. The Landlord’s Representative testified that the monthly rent was subject to lawful yearly rent increases for which he served to the Tenant himself by placing them in the mailbox. The Landlord’s Representative had no evidence in support of his position.

 

Findings

 

3.        On a balance of probabilities, I am satisfied that the order contains a serious error in the calculations of arrears of rent which stem from the Landlord’s incorrect calculations on the N4 Notice of Termination. In making this finding, I considered the compelling evidence of the Tenant, which was not reasonably challenged by the Landlord’s Representative, that the monthly rent never increased during the tenancy. The Tenant’s evidence on this issue is well supported by the email of November 12, 2021 between the Landlord’s property manager and the housing subsidy program which confirms the monthly rent never increased and remained $1,299.00, being the same amount since the tenancy began in 2018.

 

4.        Given my finding above, the Tenant’s request for review is granted.

 

New Hearing of Landlord’s L1 Application

 

5.        The Landlord’s Representative sought the consent of the Board to withdraw the application. I consented to this request.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.        The Tenant’s request for review is granted.

 

2.        Order SWL-48689-21 issued on October 19, 2021 is cancelled and replaced by the following:

 

3.        The Landlord’s application is dismissed.
