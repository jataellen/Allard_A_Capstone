Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: SOL-23389-21

 

 

In the matter of:

3, 11 STIRTON STREET HAMILTON ON L8L6E7

 

Between:

Olga Stech Stefan Stech

Landlords

 

and

 

 

Adam Barker

Tenant

 

 

 

Olga Stech and Stefan Stech (the 'Landlords') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Adam Barker (the 'Tenant') because the Landlord requires possession of the rental unit for the purpose of residential occupation. The Landlords also claimed compensation for each day the Tenant remained in the unit after the termination date.

 

This application was heard by videoconference on October 20, 2021.

 

The Landlord, Olga Stech, and the Tenant attended the hearing. Patricia Golden, the Tenant’s aunt, provided support for the Tenant. The Tenant spoke with Tenant Duty Counsel prior to the hearing.

 

Determinations:

 

1.     On July 7, 2021 the Landlords gave the Tenant an N12 notice of termination for landlord’s own use. The termination date on the N12 notice is September 30, 2021. The notice indicated that the child of the Landlords required the use of the rental unit.

 

2.     The Landlords have not met the requirements under subsection 72(1)(a) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the ‘Act’), which requires the filing with the Board an affidavit sworn by the person who personally intends to occupy the rental unit in good faith for a period of at lest one year.

 

3.     The Landlords filed a letter from the Landlords’ daughter that is signed and dated July 14, 2021. The letter does not indicate how long she will reside in the unit, nor does it indicate that she requires the rental unit for residential purposes in good faith. Moreover, the letter is not “sworn”.

 

4.     Rule 1.5 of the Landlord and Tenant Board’s Rules of Procedure sets out that where the Act requires an affidavit respecting a specified statement or specified information, the LTB will also accept a signed and dated declaration containing the specified statement or

specified information provided the declaration confirms the truth of the information or statement and acknowledges that making a false declaration is an offense.

 

5.     The Landlords elected not to call their daughter as a witness to testify at the hearing.

 

6.     I find that the letter does not meet the legal requirements under subsection 72(1)(a) of the Act and does not comply with the Rules.

 

7.     I therefore must dismiss the Landlords’ application for failing to comply with affidavit requirements.

 

8.     The Landlord testified at the hearing that they provided the Tenant with a cheque for one month’s rent as compensation for the N12 in accordance with subsection 55.1 of the Act in the amount of $590.00 on July 20, 2021. The Landlord indicated at the hearing that the Tenant had not cashed the cheque.

 

9.     However, since I am refusing to grant the Landlord’s application for not providing the required affidavit, I will hereby order that the Tenant either return the cheque or, if they have cashed it, pay the amount back to the Landlords pursuant to subsection 73.1 of the Act.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.     The Landlords’ application to evict the Tenant for the purpose of residential occupation by the Landlords’ daughter is dismissed.

 

2.     The Tenant shall return the compensation cheque to the Landlords or, if they have cashed it, pay the amount that he received back to the Landlords by no later than February 28, 2022.

 

3.     If the Tenant does not pay the full amount outstanding by February 28, 2022 then the Tenant will owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated at a rate of 2.0% annually on the outstanding amount.
