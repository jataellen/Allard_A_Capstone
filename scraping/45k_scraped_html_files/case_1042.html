Amended Order under Section 126 Order under Section 21.2 of the Statutory Powers Procedure Act

and the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

Amended Review Order

 

This amended review order is issued to correct clerical original order issued on November 17, 2021. The corrections have been bolded and underlined for ease of reference in the order.

 

CLP (the 'Landlord') applied for an order permitting the rent charged to be increased by more than the guideline for one or more of the rental units in the residential complex.

 

This application was resolved by written hearing and order TEL-99791-19 was issued on February 18, 2021.

 

On March 17, 2021 tenant TB from suite XXX (the ‘Tenant’) filed a request for review of this order on the grounds of serious error and not being reasonably able to participate in the hearing.

 

On March 25, 2021 the Board issued an interim order directing this matter to a review hearing to determine whether or not the Tenant was reasonably able to participate in the process. On March 30, 2021 the Landlord consented to granting of the Tenant’s request for review.

 

On June 28, 2021 the Board issued an interim order directing the matter to a new hearing and extending the timelines for the written hearing to allow the Tenant to provide written submissions. The Landlord had until July 7, 2021 to provide its submissions, the Tenant had until August 2, 2021 to provide his submissions, and the Landlord had until August 8, 2021 to reply. August 8, 2021 was a Sunday, the Landlord filed his reply on August 9, 2021, and requested an extension of time to file its reply if necessary. This request is granted as the deadline of August 8, 2021 instead of August 9, 2021 was set pursuant to a clerical error.

 

A written review hearing was held.

It is determined that:

 

1.                  The Landlord justified a rent increase above the guideline because of capital expenditures.

 

2.                  The maximum rent increase above the guideline because of capital expenditure is 2.02%.

 

3.                  The Board received submissions from the Tenant, TB. In his submissions, the Tenant raises maintenance issues regarding his bathroom tile and a leak it causes. The Tenant also argues that other tenants allegedly have maintenance issues in their units, such as broken fridges. Finally, the Tenant argues that there is a two-tier maintenance system whereby the Landlord deliberately fails to maintain older units in the residential complex. These submissions were taken into consideration to the extent we can under the legislation.

 

4.                  The Landlord submits it has maintained its obligations and fulfilled maintenance requests as required for any unit that made such requests. The Landlord also submits that the Tenant declined to file written maintenance requests.

 

5.                  I do not find that the Tenant proved that it is more likely than not the Landlord deliberately neglects older units in the residential complex. These allegations were speculative and unsupported by independent or documentary evidence.

 

6.                  The Tenant’s submissions state that this application should be dismissed because the Landlord failed to maintain his unit along with some others contrary to s. 20(1) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the ‘Act’). He argues that his bathroom needs to be redone as the tile is broken and the bathroom leaks into the unit below. He also argues that other tenants have broken fridges and leaking AC units. The allegations about his own unit are supported by photographs and audio recordings in which the Tenant speaks to Landlords’ agents and argues with them about the necessity of repairing the bathroom. The Tenant’s submissions acknowledge that the Landlord made repairs to stop the leak but argues that this was a band aid temporary solution.

 

7.                  Based on the Tenant’s submission and evidence filed, I find the maintenance issues raised by the Tenant do not rise to the level of a serious breach as required by the Act and therefore cannot be considered in an AGI application. The Tenant has the right to file his own application with regard to these matters.

 

8.                  The Board also received a submission that any above guideline increase awarded to the Landlord would pose a financial hardship on the tenants. The Board is required to calculate any above guideline increase in accordance with the

legislation and lacks the discretion to reduce the amount the landlord’s evidence justifies.

 

9.                  The Tenant also filed a petition apparently signed by 59 residents of the residential complex saying that they do not agree with the Landlord’s application.

In the column entitled “reason” majority of the Tenants indicated “building security”, “loss of income”, “repairs” or some combination thereof. I note that only the Tenant participated in this hearing by making submissions to the Board. Unfortunately, the Board cannot consider the petition. The questions before the Board is whether the Landlord has met the conditions of s. 126 of the Act for an above-guideline increase and in this case the Landlord has. As a result, the Board must issue an order in favour of the Landlord.

 

10.              The weighted useful life for the capital expenditures is 10 years.

 

11.              The first effective date of the rent increase above the guideline is July 1, 2019.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.                  The Landlord may increase the rents charged by 2.02% for unit 1513.

 

2.                  The percentage increase set out in paragraph 1 is to be taken as follows:

a.      The Landlord may increase the rents charged by 2.02% within the time period of July 1, 2019 to June 30, 2020.

 

3.                  The percentage increase set out in paragraph 1 may be taken in addition to the annual guideline in effect on the increase date for the unit.

 

4.                  The Landlord or the Tenants shall pay to the other any sum of money that is owed as a result of this order within 30 days.

 

5.                  If the Tenants’ rent is increased pursuant to the percentage increase ordered for capital expenditures and the same Tenant remains in the unit after the expiration of the weighted useful life for capital expenditures, then the rent will be reduced.

 

6.                  The annual guideline for 2019 is 1.8% and for 2020 is 2.2%.

 

7.                  The interim order issued on March 25, 2021 is cancelled. The stay of order TEL-99791-19 issued on February 18, 2021 is lifted.

 

February 8, 2022
