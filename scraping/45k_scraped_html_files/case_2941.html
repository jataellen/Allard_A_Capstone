Order under Section 69

Residential Tenancies Act, 2006

 

File Number: TEL-18062-21

 

 

In the matter of:

7 CEDAR STREET AJAX ON L1S1T9

 

Between:

Firmland (Cedar)

Landlord

 

 

and

 

 

Steven Mcadam Tracey Mcadam

Tenants

 

 

 

Firmland (Cedar) (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Tracey Mcadam and Steven Mcadam (the 'Tenants') because they, another occupant of the rental unit or someone they permitted in the residential complex have substantially interfered with the reasonable enjoyment or lawful right, privilege or interest of the Landlord or another tenant; and because they, another occupant of the rental unit or a person the Tenants permitted in the residential complex have seriously impaired the safety of any person. The Landlord also claimed compensation for each day the Tenants remained in the unit after the termination date.

 

This application was heard by videoconference on October 5, 2021. The Landlord’s legal representative, Bryan Rubin, and the Tenants attended the hearing. Martin Appel (M.A.), the Landlord’s property manager, attended the hearing as a witness for the Landlord.

 

Determinations:

 

1.     On June 30, 2021 the Landlord served the Tenants with an N5 and an N7 notice of termination. The notices allege that the Tenant substantially interfered with the Landlord’s lawful rights, privileges or interest, and that the Tenants seriously impaired the safety of another person respectively.

 

2.     Same facts gave rise to both notices of termination. On or about June 3, 2021 the Landlord conducted a unit inspection. The notices allege that the unit was found to be cluttered, unclean, and unsafe due to electrical wires installed by the Tenants throughout the unit. These electrical wires were hanging loosely throughout the unit and were in locations that were unsafe for electrical wiring. The rental unit is a townhouse in complex of 36 townhouses owned by the Landlord. The unit shares walls with two other units.

 

3.     Based on the evidence adduced at the hearing I find that the Tenants substantially interfered with the Landlord’s lawful rights and privileges and seriously impaired the safety of other residents at the residential complex by improperly installing electrical wires

 

throughout the unit. I find that it is not unfair to grant relief from eviction in the form of a conditional order requiring in light of all of the circumstances.

 

Substantial interference and impairment to safety

 

4.     Most of the facts in this case were undisputed.

 

5.     M.A. testified about the June 3, 2021 inspection and the Landlord presented photographic evidence in support of the testimony. The Tenants did not seriously challenge this testimony and admitted that they installed all of the additional wires other than the two orange wires in the basement. They also conceded that they are not licensed to install wires. The Landlord did not seriously challenge this statement and did not present any evidence that the two orange wires were in fact installed by the Tenants. Based on the evidence adduced at the hearing I find that on June 3, 2021 the rental unit was cluttered, unclean, and that all haphazardly installed wires other than the two orange wires were installed by the Tenants.

 

6.     M.A. also testified about the subsequent inspection that took place on July 7, 2021 and presented photographic evidence about it. The Landlord argued that the unit was still cluttered, unclean and unsafe. The Tenants conceded that the wiring was still present throughout the unit but contested the other assertions. They testified that they cleaned and de-cluttered the unit within the 7-day voiding period. The Landlord’s own photographic evidence shows substantial improvement throughout the rental unit by July 7, 2021. No pathways are blocked, no stacks of boxes reach the ceiling.

 

7.     I know from previous cases that many fire departments now use the Clutter Image Rating Scale developed by the International OCD Foundation. It is a series of photographs showing varying degrees of clutter that help quantify the severity of the cluttering problem. There are different sets of photographs for a kitchen, bedroom and living room and they are ranked on a numerical scale. They are available on-line at https://www.hoardingconnectioncc.org/Hoarding_cir.pdf. Based on the photographs of the rental unit provided I would say that after the Tenants cleaned up, the Tenants’ unit was about a 3 on the scale with 9 being the rank associated with the most amount of cluttering. Generally speaking a 3 or lower on the clutter scale is considered to be acceptable; a 4 or higher indicates something needs to be done.

 

8.     I am satisfied that the Tenants voided the clutter and cleanliness component of the N5 notice within the 7-day voiding period after the N5 was served on June 30, 2021. The photographs show a generally clean unit. While some clutter remains, hallways, entry points and exit points a clear enough that anyone can access the rental unit in the event of a fire or another emergency. The Tenants testified that the rental unit is older and does not provide much storage forcing them to store some of their belongings throughout the unit.

 

9.     Based on the Landlord’s uncontradicted evidence I find that the Tenants substantially interfered with the Landlord’s lawful rights and privileges and seriously impaired the safety of other residents of the residential complex by improperly installing electrical wires throughout the unit without permits or a license. The wires are not properly installed and

 

one blue wire is located in dangerous proximity to the stove and close enough to the kitchen sink that it presents a serious risk of causing a fire.

 

Relief from eviction

 

10.  I have considered all of the disclosed circumstances in accordance with subsection 83(2) of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act'), and find that it would not be unfair to grant relief from eviction subject to the conditions set out in this order pursuant to subsection 83(1)(a) and 204(1) of the Act.

 

11.  At the hearing the Tenants expressed readiness to immediately remove all of the wiring and extension cords other than the two orange wires that they did not install. They have resided at the rental unit for 12 years and it would be difficult for them to find an alternative place to live given the low monthly rent they are paying. The Tenants are a one-income family as the female Tenant has health issues and is unable to work. This evidence was not seriously undermined on cross-examination.

 

12.  The Landlord requested that the tenancy be terminated or in the alternative that a conditional order be issued that would allow the Landlord to terminate the tenancy if the rental unit is cluttered or if wiring is not removed or if dangerous wiring is installed again in the future.

 

13.  Given my finding that the Tenants voided the cleanliness and clutter components of the notice, I will not impose any conditions relating to the cleanliness of the unit. I find that it is not unfair to allow the Tenants to continue the tenancy provided that they remove all of the wires they installed and that they do not install any such wires for a period of 3 years.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.     The Landlord’s application to evict is denied unless the Tenants fail to meet the any of the requirements set out in paragraphs 2 and 3 below.

 

2.     On or before January 25, 2022, the Tenants shall remove any wires that they installed in the rental unit.

 

3.     For a 3-year period starting January 26, 2022 and ending January 25, 2025, the Tenants shall not install any wires in the rental unit.

 

4.     If the Tenants do not meet any of the conditions contained in paragraphs 2 or 3 of this Order and by the date required, the Landlord may apply without further notice to the Tenant for eviction pursuant to section 78 of the Act. Such application must be made within 30 days of the Tenants’ failure to comply.

 

5.     The Tenants shall also pay to the Landlord $186.00 for the cost of filing the application.

 

6.     If the Tenants do not pay the Landlord the full amount owing on or before January 25, 2022, they will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from January 26, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.
