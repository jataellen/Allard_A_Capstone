Skyline Living (the 'Landlord') applied for an order to terminate the tenancy and evict Christa Jackson (the 'Tenant') and for an order to have the Tenant pay the rent the Tenant owes because the Tenant failed to meet a condition specified in the order issued by the Board on October 5, 2021 with respect to application EAL-96000-21.

 

Determinations:

 

1.     The order provided that the Landlord could apply to the Board under section 78 of the Residential Tenancies Act, 2006 (the 'Act') without notice to the Tenant to terminate the tenancy and evict the Tenant if the Tenant did not meet certain condition(s) specified in the order.

 

2.     I find that the Tenant has not met the following conditions specified in the order: the Tenant did not pay $166.04 on or before November 20, 2021, nor did they pay the lawful rent for December on or before December 1, 2021.

 

3.     The previous application included a request for an order for the payment of arrears of rent. The resulting order required the Tenant to pay rent or some or all of the arrears of rent. Accordingly, in addition to eviction, the Landlord is entitled to request an order for the payment of arrears of rent and compensation.

 

4.     The Tenant was ordered to pay $1,992.48 for rent arrears and the costs related to the Landlord's application fee in Order EAL-96000-21. The amount that is still owing from that order is $1,826.44 and that amount is included in this order. As a result, the previous order EAL-96000-21 is cancelled.

 

5.     Since the date of the order, the Tenant has failed to pay the full rent that became owing for the period from October 1, 2021 to December 31, 2021.

 

It is ordered that:

 

1.     The previous order EAL-96000-21 is cancelled.

File Number: EAL-99142-21

 

2.     The tenancy between the Landlord and the Tenant is terminated. The Tenant must move out of the rental unit on or before January 28, 2022.

 

3.     The Tenant shall pay to the Landlord $1,988.69*. This amount represents the rent owing up to December 31, 2021 and the costs related to the application fee for the previous application.

 

4.     The Tenant shall also pay to the Landlord $25.58 per day for compensation for the use of the unit starting January 1, 2022 to the date the Tenant moves out of the unit.

 

5.     If the Tenant does not pay the Landlord the full amount owing* on or before January 28, 2022, the Tenant will start to owe interest. This will be simple interest calculated from January 29, 2022 at 2.00% annually on the balance outstanding.

 

6.     If the unit is not vacated on or before January 28, 2022, then starting January 29, 2022, the Landlord may file this order with the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) so that the eviction may be enforced.

 

7.     Upon receipt of this order, the Court Enforcement Office (Sheriff) is directed to give vacant possession of the unit to the Landlord on or after January 29, 2022.
